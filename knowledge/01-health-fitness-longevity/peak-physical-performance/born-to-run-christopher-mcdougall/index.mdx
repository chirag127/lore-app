Born to Run is Christopher McDougall's 2009 investigative narrative that ignited a global barefoot and minimalist running revolution. Framed as a personal quest — McDougall, a former Associated Press war correspondent turned running journalist, was suffering from chronic foot injuries that doctors told him would end his running career — the book tracks the author's journey into Mexico's remote Copper Canyons (Barrancas del Cobre) to find the Tarahumara, a reclusive indigenous people who run 50 to 100+ mile ultramarathons at extraordinary speeds without ever suffering the injuries that plague modern runners. Along the way, McDougall weaves in paleoanthropology, running shoe industry history, interviews with elite ultrarunners, and the larger-than-life story of an American expatriate known as Caballo Blanco who dedicated his life to bridging the Tarahumara and the competitive ultrarunning world.

<BookHeader />

The book's significance extends beyond adventure storytelling: it reframed running as an evolutionary act rather than a sport, challenged the entire athletic footwear industry, and sparked a grassroots movement toward natural, barefoot-style movement. Sold over three million copies worldwide and on The New York Times Best Seller list for over four months, Born to Run won a Notable Book Award in 2010 and became one of the most influential nonfiction books in the health and fitness domain of the 21st century.
