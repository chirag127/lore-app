Sleep is not merely rest — it is an active, highly structured biological process essential for cognitive function, physical recovery, and long-term health. This subcategory covers the architecture of sleep (NREM and REM stages), the glymphatic system's waste-clearance function during deep sleep, the molecular genetics of circadian rhythms (clock genes, the suprachiasmatic nucleus), and the entrainment of the circadian cycle by light, temperature, and behavior.
