# Public Health & Epidemiology

## Overview

Public health focuses on the health of populations rather than individual
patients. Epidemiology provides the methods to study disease patterns,
causes, and effects in populations. This subcategory covers epidemiological
methods and study design (cohort, case-control, randomized trials, bias,
confounding), global health and infectious disease control, and health
policy and healthcare systems.

## Books

- [**The Ghost Map** — Steven Johnson (2006)](the-ghost-map-steven-johnson)
  The gripping story of the 1854 Broad Street cholera outbreak and how two
  unlikely investigators — Dr. John Snow and Reverend Henry Whitehead — used
  mapping, shoe-leather epidemiology, and multidisciplinary thinking to defeat
  the epidemic and create modern public health.
