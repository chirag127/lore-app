# 💰 Money, Markets & Wealth

## 📚 Overview

This category covers the full spectrum of financial knowledge — from personal
finance and wealth building to institutional investing, from behavioral
finance to quantitative trading, from economic theory to market history.
The books here teach you how money works, how markets behave, how to invest
wisely, and how economic systems shape societies.

The thread connecting every book: the intelligent management of scarce
resources under uncertainty.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **05 Business, Strategy & Organizations** covers **corporate finance,
  accounting, and business economics** — the firm-level perspective. This
  category covers **markets and personal/institutional investing** — the
  capital-allocation perspective.
- **01 Mind, Behavior & Human Performance** covers **behavioral psychology
  generally**; this category covers its **application to financial markets**
  (behavioral finance).
- **08 Society, History & Power** covers **economic history and political
  economy** from a societal lens; this category covers them from a market
  lens.

## 📖 Major Topics

- **Personal Finance**: Budgeting, saving, debt management, retirement
  planning, tax optimization, insurance, FIRE movement, asset allocation.
- **Value Investing**: The Graham-Buffett approach, intrinsic value,
  margin of safety, moats, circle of competence, annual reports.
- **Index & Passive Investing**: Efficient markets, index funds, factor
  investing, rebalancing, cost minimization, Bogleheads philosophy.
- **Behavioral Finance**: Prospect theory, mental accounting, overconfidence,
  herding, loss aversion, framing, market anomalies.
- **Venture Capital & Private Equity**: Fund structure, deal sourcing,
  due diligence, valuation, term sheets, exits, LP-GP dynamics.
- **Economics**: Supply and demand, monetary policy, fiscal policy, business
  cycles, inflation, unemployment, game theory, behavioral economics,
  development economics.
- **Financial History**: Bubbles and crashes, market manias, the evolution
  of banking, the Great Depression, 2008 financial crisis.
- **Advanced Investing**: Options, futures, derivatives, hedge funds, short
  selling, distressed assets, arbitrage, risk parity.

## 🧠 Reading Order

1. Start with **The Psychology of Money** for the right mindset
2. Then **The Intelligent Investor** + **A Random Walk** for core philosophy
3. Then **Poor Charlie's Almanack** for multidisciplinary thinking
4. Then **The Most Important Thing** for risk mastery
5. Then deep dives into specific areas (behavioral finance, VC, etc.)

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Financial literacy is a core life skill. Whether you're managing a household
budget or a billion-dollar portfolio, the principles are the same: understand
risk, think long-term, control what you can, and stay humble before markets.
