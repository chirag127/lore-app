## Strengths

- **Comprehensive and authoritative.** No other single volume covers
  as much ground with the same depth. The three-author team brings
  complementary expertise in theory, algorithms, and applications.
- **Mathematical rigor without sacrificing intuition.** The book
  provides formal derivations alongside clear conceptual explanations.
- **Excellent treatment of generative models.** Chapters on GANs,
  VAEs, Boltzmann machines, and autoregressive models form the best
  single-volume reference available.
- **Free online availability.** The decision to make the full text
  freely available was transformative for the field.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Minimal code or implementation guidance.** The book is entirely
  theory. Readers seeking to implement the algorithms must look
  elsewhere.
- **No exercises or problem sets.** Unlike most textbooks, there are
  no end-of-chapter exercises, making self-study harder.
- **Assumes significant mathematical maturity.** Readers without
  graduate-level math will struggle.
- **Already showing its age in some areas.** Published in 2016, it
  predates transformers, diffusion models, and many recent advances.

---

## Criticism

- **Too theoretical for practitioners.** The book has been criticized
  for being inaccessible to the engineers who most need to understand
  deep learning.
- **Uneven depth.** Some topics (GANs, RNNs) receive extensive
  treatment while others (attention, transfer learning) are covered
  briefly.
- **The GAN emphasis reflects the author's bias.** Goodfellow's
  invention of GANs receives proportionally more attention than other
  generative approaches.
