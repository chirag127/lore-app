Winner of the Pulitzer Prize for General Nonfiction in 1992, *The Prize* is widely regarded as the definitive history of the oil industry. Daniel Yergin traces the epic story of petroleum from its first commercial drilling in 1859 through the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait in 1990, demonstrating how the quest for oil has shaped the rise and fall of empires, the outcome of world wars, and the fundamental structure of the modern global economy.
