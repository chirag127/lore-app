# Confidence, Self-Esteem & Assertiveness

## Overview

This subcategory addresses the psychological foundations of confident living. Self-esteem refers to an overall sense of self-worth. Confidence is the belief in one's ability to succeed in specific domains. Assertiveness is the ability to express one's needs, opinions, and boundaries clearly and respectfully. Together these qualities enable individuals to pursue their goals, form healthy relationships, and handle criticism, rejection, and failure. The subcategory also addresses imposter syndrome — the feeling of being a fraud despite evidence of competence.
