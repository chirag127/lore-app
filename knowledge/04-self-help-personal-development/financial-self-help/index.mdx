# Financial Self-Help

## Overview

Financial self-help addresses the psychological and behavioral dimensions of personal finance. Unlike formal finance (which focuses on markets, instruments, and optimization), financial self-help examines money mindsets, the psychology of spending and saving, and the habits that lead to financial well-being. It draws on behavioral economics (Thaler, Kahneman), popular personal finance literature (Kiyosaki, Ramsey, Sethi), and research on happiness and money.

## Books

- **[The Richest Man in Babylon](the-richest-man-in-babylon-george-clason)** — George S. Clason (1926) — The foundational classic of personal finance: seven cures for a lean purse, five laws of gold, and the timeless discipline of saving 10% of everything you earn.
