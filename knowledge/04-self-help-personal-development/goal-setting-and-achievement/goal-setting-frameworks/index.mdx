# Goal-Setting Frameworks

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Goal-setting theory is one of the most robust findings in organizational psychology. Edwin Locke and Gary Latham's research, spanning decades, demonstrated that specific, challenging goals consistently lead to higher performance than vague or easy goals — provided the person has the ability, commitment, and feedback to pursue them. This research gave rise to a family of practical frameworks: SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound), OKRs (Objectives and Key Results), BHAGs (Big Hairy Audacious Goals), and backward planning.

This sub-subcategory exists because the difference between having a goal and having an effective goal is large and consequential. People fail not because they lack ambition but because they frame their goals poorly — too vague, too ambitious without a plan, or disconnected from daily action. Goal-setting frameworks provide the structure that turns intention into achievement.

## What Belongs Here

- SMART goal criteria and application
- OKR methodology for personal and team goals
- BHAGs for long-term vision
- Backward planning and reverse engineering
- Implementation intentions (if-then planning)
- Goal commitment, specificity, and difficulty calibration
- Written goals, visualization, and goal accountability
- Sub-goaling and goal hierarchies

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- Habit formation and systems (belongs in Systems Thinking for Personal Goals)
- Motivation theory and research (belongs in Motivation Science Applied)
- Productivity and task management systems (belongs in Productivity)
- Vision boards and law-of-attraction approaches (pseudoscience, not included)
- General life planning without goal specificity

## Essential Books

1. **Goals!: How to Get Everything You Want — Faster Than You Ever Thought Possible** — Brian Tracy (2003) — Practical, accessible goal-setting system drawing on decades of success coaching.
2. **Measure What Matters: How Google, Bono, and the Gates Foundation Rock the World with OKRs** — John Doerr (2018) — The definitive book on OKRs, adapted from business to personal use.
3. **The 12 Week Year: Get More Done in 12 Weeks than Others Do in 12 Months** — Brian P. Moran and Michael Lennington (2013) — Compressed goal-setting framework that creates urgency and focus.
4. **Your Best Year Ever: A 5-Step Plan for Achieving Your Most Important Goals** — Michael Hyatt (2018) — Evidence-based annual goal-setting system with clear implementation steps.
5. **The One Thing: The Surprisingly Simple Truth Behind Extraordinary Results** — Gary Keller and Jay Papasan (2012) — On focusing on the single most important goal rather than spreading effort thin.
6. **Goals: How to Set and Achieve Goals** — Dean Bokhari (2020) — Concise framework combining SMART, implementation intentions, and accountability structures.
7. **The Desire Map: A Guide to Creating Goals with Soul** — Danielle LaPorte (2014) — Goal-setting centered on desired feelings rather than achievements alone.
8. **The Gap and the Gain: How to Measure Progress and Achieve Your Goals** — Dan Sullivan and Benjamin Hardy (2021) — Reframing goal measurement as progress from the starting point (gain) rather than shortfall from the ideal (gap).

## Key Concepts

- Goal specificity and difficulty principles (Locke & Latham)
- SMART criteria
- OKR structure: objective (qualitative) + key results (quantitative)
- BHAG concept (Collins & Porras)
- Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer)
- The goal gradient effect
- Approach vs. avoidance goals
- Goal disengagement and flexibility
