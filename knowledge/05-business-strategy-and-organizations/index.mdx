# 🏢 Business, Strategy & Organizations

## 📚 Overview

This category explores how organizations create value, compete, and succeed. From crafting strategy to leading teams, from entrepreneurship to corporate finance, these books provide frameworks for understanding business dynamics and building successful organizations.

The focus is on **organizational behavior**, **strategic thinking**, and **business systems** — not the financial markets (that's 03) or the psychology of individuals (that's 01).

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **03 Money, Markets & Wealth** covers **capital allocation** and **financial markets**. This category covers **firm-level strategy** and **organizational execution**.
- **01 Mind, Behavior & Human Performance** covers **individual psychology**. This category covers **organizational behavior** and **group dynamics**.
- **05 Business** is about **building and running organizations**. **08 Society** covers **political economy** and **social institutions**.

## 📖 Major Topics

- **Business Strategy**: Competitive advantage, blue ocean strategy, Porter's five forces, generic strategies, resource-based view.
- **Organizational Behavior**: Culture, motivation, team dynamics, power structures, bureaucracy, change management.
- **Leadership**: Transformational leadership, servant leadership, situational leadership, emotional intelligence.
- **Entrepreneurship**: Startup formation, scaling, venture capital, founder psychology, innovation processes.
- **Corporate Finance**: Capital structure, valuation, financial reporting, mergers & acquisitions, capital budgeting.
- **Marketing**: Brand positioning, consumer behavior, product marketing, growth strategies, customer acquisition.
- **Operations**: Supply chains, lean manufacturing, Six Sigma, quality management, process optimization.
- **Product Management**: Product discovery, roadmaps, user research, metrics, cross-functional leadership.

## 🧠 Reading Order

1. Start with **Good to Great** for organizational excellence
2. Then **The Lean Startup** for innovation frameworks
3. Then **Competitive Strategy** for Porter's fundamentals
4. Then **The Fifth Discipline** for systems thinking in business
5. Then specialized topics based on role (leadership, marketing, ops)
