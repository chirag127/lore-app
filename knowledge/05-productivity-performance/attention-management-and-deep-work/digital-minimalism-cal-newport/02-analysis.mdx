## Strengths

- **Philosophical depth.** Goes beyond tips to provide a coherent
  framework for technology use.
- **Practical method.** The 30-day digital declutter is a specific,
  actionable protocol.
- **Well-researched.** Draws on psychology, neuroscience, and
  sociology to support its arguments.
- **Excellent writing.** Clear, persuasive, and engaging.
- **Balanced tone.** Not Luddite — acknowledges technology's value
  while insisting on intentionality.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Privileged perspective.** The advice assumes significant control
  over one's schedule and obligations. Not everyone can take a
  30-day break from social media.
- **Overstates the problem.** Some readers may feel Newport
  exaggerates the harms of moderate technology use.
- **Light on systemic critique.** Focuses on individual behavior
  rather than the economic incentives driving addictive design.
- **Repetitive.** Core arguments are stated multiple times across
  chapters.
