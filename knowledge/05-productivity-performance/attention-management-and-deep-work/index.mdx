Cal Newport's deep work hypothesis — that intense, distraction-free concentration produces both superior output and meaning — meets empirical work on cognitive bandwidth, attentional residue, and the costs of task-switching. This subcategory covers the theory and practice of reclaiming focus from the attention economy, including digital minimalism, technology boundaries, and the cognitive science of distraction.
