Better Than Before is Gretchen Rubin's exploration of the one question that most habit books ignore: what works for whom? Drawing on her Four Tendencies framework of personality types, Rubin argues that the effectiveness of every habit strategy depends on how a person responds to expectations — both inner expectations (self-imposed goals) and outer expectations (work deadlines, social commitments). The book sold over a million copies, spawned a standalone framework book (The Four Tendencies, 2017), and has been taken as a quiz by over 30 million people. It challenges the 1% improvement orthodoxy of Atomic Habits by arguing that the right strategy matters more than the right system, and the right strategy depends entirely on who you are.
