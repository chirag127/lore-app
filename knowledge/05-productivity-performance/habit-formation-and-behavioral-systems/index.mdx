Habits are the infrastructure of productivity — automated routines that conserve willpower and cognitive resources. This subcategory covers the cue-routine-reward loop (Duhigg), the four laws of behavior change (Clear), identity-based habits, keystone habits, and the design of friction and defaults. The applied discipline of making good behavior easy and bad behavior hard.
