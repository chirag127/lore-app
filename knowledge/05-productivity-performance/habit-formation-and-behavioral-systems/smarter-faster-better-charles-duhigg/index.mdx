Smarter Faster Better is Charles Duhigg's follow-up to The Power of Habit, applying the same journalistic approach to the science of productivity. Rather than offering a single productivity system, Duhigg identifies eight concepts that underpin effectiveness: motivation, teams, focus, goal-setting, managing others, decision-making, innovation, and data absorption. The book argues that productivity is not about doing more things faster but about making better choices about what to do and how to think. It spent over a year on the New York Times bestseller list and has sold over 2 million copies.
