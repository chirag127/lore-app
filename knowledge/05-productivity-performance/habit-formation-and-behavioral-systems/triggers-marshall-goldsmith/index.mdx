Triggers is Marshall Goldsmith's exploration of why we behave in ways that contradict our stated goals — and what to do about it. Goldsmith, one of the world's most influential executive coaches, argues that the gap between knowing and doing is not a knowledge problem but a trigger problem: our environment, emotions, and social interactions trigger behaviors that undermine our intentions. The book's central tool is the daily "active questions" process, a structured self-assessment that creates the accountability most people lack. Triggers draws on Goldsmith's decades of coaching Fortune 500 executives and the research behind his bestselling What Got You Here Won't Get You There.
