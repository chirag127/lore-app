Willpower: Rediscovering the Greatest Human Strength is the definitive scientific treatment of self-control. Roy Baumeister, the social psychologist who pioneered the study of ego depletion, teams with New York Times science writer John Tierney to present the research on why self-control matters, how it works, and how to strengthen it. The book argues that willpower is not a metaphor but a biological resource — powered by glucose, depleted by use, and trainable with practice. Published in 2011, it remains the most authoritative scientific treatment of willpower available to general readers, though subsequent replication controversies have complicated its legacy.
