What if you could get the essence of the world's best research on skill development distilled into fifty-two short, practical tips? That is exactly what *The Little Book of Talent* delivers. Daniel Coyle, author of *The Talent Code*, distills his findings from visiting the world's greatest talent hotbeds into a pocket-sized manual of improvement.

Each tip is backed by neuroscience—specifically the science of myelin, the neural insulation that grows with proper practice and makes skills faster and more accurate. Coyle organizes the tips into three sections: getting started, improving skills, and sustaining momentum. Whether you are learning a language, an instrument, a sport, or a professional skill, the advice is practical, concise, and immediately applicable.

The book's central message is that talent is not mysterious. It is the product of specific, repeatable actions performed consistently over time. *The Little Book of Talent* gives you the actions—all fifty-two of them—in a format you can read in an afternoon and reference for a lifetime.
