## Strengths

- **Memorable metaphor.** "Eat the frog" is one of the stickiest
  productivity concepts ever.
- **Actionable.** Each technique is a concrete, do-today behavior.
- **Concise.** Can be read in a single sitting.
- **Broadly applicable.** Works for students, executives, freelancers.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Repetitive.** The same idea is restated 21 times with slight
  variation.
- **Lacks depth.** Does not address the psychological roots of
  procrastination.
- **No supporting research.** Tracy cites almost no studies despite
  drawing on well-documented psychology.
- **Oversimplified.** Real productivity involves managing people,
  context switching, and creative work that resists rigid scheduling.

---

## Final Assessment

| Dimension | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Memorability | 10/10 | Best metaphor in productivity |
| Practical Utility | 8/10 | Works immediately |
| Depth | 4/10 | Surface-level advice |
| Research Base | 2/10 | Almost no citations |
| Originality | 5/10 | Synthesizes known concepts |
| **Overall** | **5.5/10** | Useful but not profound |
