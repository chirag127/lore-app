## Overview

*Eat That Frog!* (2001, updated 2017) by Brian Tracy is a concise
productivity guide built on one central metaphor: if the first thing
you do each morning is eat a live frog, you can go through the rest
of the day knowing it is the worst thing you will have to do.

The "frog" is your most important task — the one you are most likely
to procrastinate on. Tracy's framework delivers 21 practical
techniques for identifying and tackling that task first, every day.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start your day with the hardest task.** Your willpower is
   highest in the morning. Use it on what matters most.

2. **The 80/20 rule applies to tasks.** 20% of your tasks produce
   80% of your results. Identify and prioritize that 20%.

3. **Plan every day in advance.** Ten minutes of planning saves
   hours of execution.

4. **Apply the ABCDE method.** Rank tasks A (must do), B (should do),
   C (nice to do), D (delegate), E (eliminate).

5. **Use the 10/90 rule.** The first 10% of time spent planning
   saves 90% of execution time.

6. **Practice single-handling.** Do one task at a time until
   complete. Multitasking is a myth.

## Why This Book Matters

*Eat That Frog!* sells because its metaphor sticks. Twenty years
after publication, "eating the frog" is part of the productivity
lexicon. Tracy distilled complex time-management research into a
single memorable image and 21 actionable techniques.
