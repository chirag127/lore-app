Why We Work is a compact, urgent manifesto that challenges the most fundamental assumption of modern capitalism: that people work only for money. Drawing on decades of research in psychology, behavioral economics, and his own work on practical wisdom, Barry Schwartz argues that the way we have structured work — around financial incentives, tight rules, and narrow metrics — has created a self-fulfilling prophecy. By designing workplaces that strip work of meaning, autonomy, and engagement, we have created workers who care only about pay, thereby proving the very theory that gave rise to those workplaces in the first place. Published as a TED Book in 2015, it distills a career of thinking into a pocket-sized polemic.
