Behavioral decision research has identified dozens of systematic biases — confirmation bias, availability heuristic, anchoring, survivorship bias, hindsight bias, and many more — that reliably distort human judgment. This subcategory catalogues these errors and, crucially, examines debiasing strategies: techniques like considering the opposite, pre-mortems, red teams, and decision hygiene that help individuals and organizations think more clearly. The goal is not to eliminate bias (impossible) but to build systems that reduce its damage.
