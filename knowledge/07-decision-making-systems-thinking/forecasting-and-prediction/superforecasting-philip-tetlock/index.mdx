*Superforecasting: The Art and Science of Prediction* (2015) is the definitive account of what makes some people consistently better at predicting the future — and whether those skills can be learned. Philip Tetlock, a psychologist at the University of Pennsylvania, spent decades studying political and economic forecasters, and his earlier work *Expert Political Judgment* (2005) delivered a devastating verdict: experts were barely more accurate than dart-throwing chimpanzees. *Superforecasting* tells the next chapter, showing how the Good Judgment Project proved that ordinary people, trained in probabilistic reasoning, can outperform professional intelligence analysts with access to classified data.
