---
title: 'Cooperative vs. Non-Cooperative Games'
parent: '07-decision-making-systems-thinking/game-theory-applied'
order: 2
description: 'When players can form binding agreements versus when they act independently.'
---

In cooperative games, players can form coalitions and make binding agreements; the focus is on how the surplus is divided (the Shapley value, the core, Nash bargaining). In non-cooperative games, players act independently and agreements must be self-enforcing. This subcategory covers bargaining theory, coalition formation, the Nash bargaining solution, and the fundamental question of when cooperation can emerge from self-interested behavior.
