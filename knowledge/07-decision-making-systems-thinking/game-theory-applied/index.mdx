Game theory is the study of strategic interaction — situations where the outcome of your decision depends on the decisions of others. This subcategory covers the foundational concepts (Nash equilibrium, prisoners' dilemma, coordination games, zero-sum games), the distinction between cooperative and non-cooperative games, and mechanism design (reverse game theory: designing rules to produce desired outcomes). Applications range from economics and business strategy to political science, evolutionary biology, and everyday social reasoning.

## Sub-Subcategories

- **[Strategic Interaction & Nash Equilibrium](strategic-interaction-and-nash-equilibrium)** — The core concepts of strategic decision-making.
- **[Cooperative vs. Non-Cooperative Games](cooperative-vs-noncooperative-games)** — Alliances, coalitions, and competitive dynamics.
- **[Mechanism Design & Incentive Engineering](mechanism-design-and-incentive-engineering)** — Designing rules and incentives to achieve desired outcomes.
