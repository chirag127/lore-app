Mechanism design is game theory in reverse: instead of analyzing an existing game, you design the rules to achieve a specific outcome. Core concepts include the revelation principle, incentive compatibility, the Vickrey-Clarke-Groves mechanism for truthful bidding, and the design of auctions, matching markets, and voting systems. Applications include spectrum auctions (which earned governments billions), school choice systems, kidney exchange, and online advertising markets.
