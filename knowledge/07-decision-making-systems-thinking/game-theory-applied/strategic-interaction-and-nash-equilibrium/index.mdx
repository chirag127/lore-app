Nash equilibrium is the central solution concept of game theory: a set of strategies where no player can improve their outcome by unilaterally changing their strategy. This subcategory covers the prisoners' dilemma, the tragedy of the commons, coordination games, the game of chicken, and the evolution of cooperation (Axelrod's tournaments). Applications include business competition, arms races, social dilemmas, and the emergence of norms and conventions.
