Probabilistic reasoning is the correct language for uncertainty. Bayesian reasoning provides a formal framework for updating beliefs in light of new evidence. This subcategory covers Bayes' theorem and its applications, Bayesian vs. frequentist interpretations, base rate neglect, calibration training, and the practical habit of assigning probabilities to beliefs rather than holding them as certainties. The key insight: rational thinking is not about being right — it is about being less wrong over time.
