Expected value is the foundational concept: multiply each possible outcome by its probability and sum them. Utility theory extends this by recognizing that the subjective value of money (or any outcome) is nonlinear — the difference between $0 and $1M matters more than between $10M and $11M (diminishing marginal utility). Covers the St. Petersburg paradox, von Neumann-Morgenstern utility, prospect theory (Kahneman & Tversky), and the practical use of expected value calculations in everyday decisions.
