Risk and uncertainty are not the same thing. Risk can be measured and modeled; uncertainty cannot. This subcategory covers the mathematical tools for navigating both: expected value and utility theory (the rational framework for decisions under risk), the Kelly criterion for optimal bet sizing, and the epistemology of tail risks — the rare, high-impact events that standard models miss. Nassim Taleb's work on black swans and antifragility provides the philosophical framework: instead of trying to predict rare events, build systems that benefit from volatility.

## Sub-Subcategories

- **[Expected Value & Utility Theory](expected-value-and-utility-theory)** — The mathematics of rational choice under risk.
- **[Risk of Ruin & Kelly Criterion](risk-of-ruin-and-kelly-criterion)** — Optimal bet sizing and avoiding catastrophic loss.
- **[Tail Risk, Black Swans & Antifragility](tail-risk-black-swans-and-antifragility)** — Rare events, the limits of prediction, and systems that gain from disorder.
