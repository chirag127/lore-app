The Kelly criterion solves a deceptively simple problem: what fraction of your bankroll should you bet when you have an edge? Bet too little and you grow slowly; bet too much and you risk ruin. This subcategory covers the Kelly formula, its application to investing and gambling, the concept of risk of ruin (the probability of losing everything before reaching your goal), and the critical distinction between maximizing expected value and avoiding scenarios that end the game entirely.
