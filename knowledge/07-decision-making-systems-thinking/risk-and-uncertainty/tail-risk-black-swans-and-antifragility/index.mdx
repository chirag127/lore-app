Tail risks are the extreme outcomes in the fat tails of probability distributions — events that standard models treat as virtually impossible but that occur with disturbing regularity. Nassim Taleb's black swan theory argues that these events dominate history yet are fundamentally unpredictable. His response is antifragility: systems that gain from volatility, disorder, and stressors. This subcategory covers power-law distributions, the limits of Gaussian models, the Turkey problem, and practical strategies for robustness and antifragility.
