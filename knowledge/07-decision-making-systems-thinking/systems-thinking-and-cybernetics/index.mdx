Systems thinking provides tools for understanding complex wholes rather than isolated parts. It draws on cybernetics (Wiener, Ashby, Beer), general system theory (Bertalanffy), and the feedback-loop thinking popularized by Donella Meadows and Peter Senge. Core concepts include reinforcing and balancing feedback loops, stocks and flows, leverage points, emergence, and the difference between complicated and complex systems. This thinking is essential for anyone who wants to intervene effectively in organizations, ecosystems, economies, or any interconnected system.

## Books

- Thinking in Systems: A Primer — Donella H. Meadows (2008)
- Stafford Beer: A Cybernetician
