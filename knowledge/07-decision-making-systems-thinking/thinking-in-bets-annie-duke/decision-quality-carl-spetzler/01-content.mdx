## Part I: The Decision Quality Framework

**Chapter 1 — Why Decision Quality Matters**: Spetzler makes the case that organizational performance is bounded by decision quality. Better decisions compound over time.

**Chapter 2 — The Six Requirements for Decision Quality**: Frame, creative alternatives, meaningful reliable information, clear values and tradeoffs, logically correct reasoning, commitment to action. These six elements form the DQ framework.

**Chapter 3 — The Decision Hierarchy**: Distinguishing strategic decisions (long-term, framing), tactical decisions (implementation), and operational decisions (execution). The hierarchy prevents confusion about what level a decision lives at.

## Part II: The Dialogue Decision Process

**Chapter 4 — Creating the Decision Process**: The Dialogue Decision Process is the organizational mechanism for achieving decision quality. It alternates between analytic work (done by small teams) and dialogue sessions (involving decision-makers).

**Chapter 5 — Framing the Decision**: Framing is the most critical phase — it sets the boundaries and perspective for everything that follows. A bad frame guarantees a bad decision.

**Chapter 6 — Generating Alternatives**: Creative alternatives are a requirement for quality. The authors recommend deliberate divergence before convergence.

## Part III: Embedding Decision Quality

**Chapter 7 — Building Organizational Decision Competency**: Individual decision skills are not enough. Organizations need shared language, processes, and culture.

**Chapter 8 — Decision-Making in the Boardroom**: Governance implications and accountability for decision process.

**Chapter 9 — Tools and Methods**: Overview of key analytical tools including decision trees, influence diagrams, and value-of-information analysis.
