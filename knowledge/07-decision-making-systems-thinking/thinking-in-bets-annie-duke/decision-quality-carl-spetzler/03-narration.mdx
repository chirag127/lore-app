## Writing Style & Voice

The writing is clear, structured, and professional — in the style of experienced consultants writing for executives. The voice is authoritative without being academic. Sentences are direct and consistently focused on practical application.

## Narrative Structure

The book is tightly structured around the six-requirement framework and the Dialogue Decision Process. Each section builds on the previous one. The structure is logical and easy to follow, though the book works better as a reference than as a linear narrative.

## Rhetorical Techniques

The authors rely on authority (SDG's track record) and logic (the internal consistency of the framework). Case studies from SDG consulting engagements provide concrete illustration. The book makes extensive use of diagrams and frameworks.

## Readability

Reading level is approximately 10th grade. The vocabulary is accessible. Technical terms (influence diagram, value-of-information, tornado chart) are explained when introduced.

## Comparative Context

*Decision Quality* fills the organizational slot in this category. It is more practical than academic textbooks and more rigorous than popular decision books. Its closest companion is *Smart Choices* — together they provide the complete individual-to-organization spectrum of analytical decision-making.
