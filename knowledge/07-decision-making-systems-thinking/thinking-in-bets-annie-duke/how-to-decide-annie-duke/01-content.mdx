## Part I: The Decision-Making Framework

Duke recaps the core concepts from *Thinking in Bets* — resulting, bet-framing, probabilistic thinking — but moves quickly into actionable exercises. She introduces the "Decision Journal" as the central tool: a structured template for recording decisions, reasoning, expected probabilities, and emotional state at the time of the decision.

## Part II: Common Decision Traps

This section catalogs the most frequent decision errors with specific counter-exercises:

**Resulting**: Duke provides a "Resulting Checker" — a three-question protocol to determine whether you're judging a decision by its outcome. Did the outcome contain significant luck? Would you make the same decision again with the same information?

**Overconfidence**: She introduces the "Confidence Calibration" exercise — make 10 predictions with confidence percentages, then check your accuracy. Over time, this trains you to express uncertainty more precisely.

**Analysis Paralysis**: Duke offers the "Decision Deadline" tool — set a maximum time for any decision based on its importance and reversibility. Low-stakes, reversible decisions should take minutes, not hours.

**Emotional Hijacking**: The "Tilt Detector" helps readers recognize when emotions are driving decisions. Duke draws on poker's concept of "tilt" — playing badly because of emotional distress — and provides reset strategies.

## Part III: Decision-Making in Groups

Duke addresses the challenge of making decisions with others, including strategies for surfacing dissenting opinions without creating conflict. She introduces the "Pre-Mortem" as a group exercise and the "Red Team/Blue Team" technique for structured debate.

## Part IV: Building Your System

The final section guides readers in creating a personalized decision-making system: selecting tools, setting up journaling routines, identifying truth-seeking partners, and establishing review cadences. Duke emphasizes that the goal is not perfection but gradual improvement through consistent practice.
