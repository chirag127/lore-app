*How to Decide* is the practical workbook that *Thinking in Bets* promised. Where the earlier book established the why, this one delivers the how — structured exercises, decision journal templates, and step-by-step protocols for applying probabilistic thinking to real decisions.

<BookHeader />

Duke organized the book around the most common decision pitfalls and provides specific countermeasures for each. The book functions as a training manual for the mind: it teaches readers to recognize cognitive biases in real time, separate luck from skill in evaluating outcomes, and build systems that make good decisions automatic rather than effortful.
