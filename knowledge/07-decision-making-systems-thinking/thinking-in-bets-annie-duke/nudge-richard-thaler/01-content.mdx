## Part I: Humans and Econs

**Chapter 1 — The Two Systems**: Humans make most decisions automatically, influenced by biases and heuristics. Econs (the rational actor of economics) do not exist. Good choice architecture recognizes Human nature.

**Chapter 2 — Biases and Blunders**: A tour of the cognitive biases that affect decisions: anchoring, availability, representativeness, overconfidence, loss aversion, and status quo bias.

**Chapter 3 — Thinking Automatically**: The default effect. People overwhelmingly stick with default options, and good choice architecture sets defaults that serve choosers' interests. The chapter reviews evidence from organ donation, retirement savings, and insurance.

## Part II: Money, Health, Education

**Chapter 4 — Save More Tomorrow**: Thaler's signature program that uses pre-commitment and automatic escalation to increase retirement savings. This chapter alone changed how millions of Americans save for retirement.

**Chapter 5 — Naive Investing**: How to design investment choice architecture that protects naive investors from their biases.

**Chapter 6 — Credit Markets**: Mortgages, credit cards, and payday lending. Transparency and simplification can improve borrowing decisions.

**Chapter 7 — Health**: Prescription drug plans, Medicare Part D, and health savings accounts. The chapter shows how poorly designed choice creates suffering.

**Chapter 8 — Education**: School choice and college savings plans. The design of enrollment options dramatically affects who applies where.

## Part III: Objections and Applications

**Chapter 9 — What Nudge Is Not**: Distinguishing nudges from mandates, bans, and manipulation. The "libertarian" part is essential: nudges preserve freedom.
