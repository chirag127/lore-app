*Nudge* is the founding text of behavioral public policy. Thaler and Sunstein introduced "choice architecture" and "libertarian paternalism" into the policy vocabulary, arguing that governments can improve citizen outcomes by designing decision environments carefully.

<BookHeader />

The book argues that humans are not the rational actors assumed by classical economics. We are predictably irrational, and this creates opportunities for well-designed nudges — small changes in the decision environment that produce large changes in behavior.
