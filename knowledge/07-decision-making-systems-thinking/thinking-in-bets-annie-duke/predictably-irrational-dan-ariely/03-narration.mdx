## Writing Style & Voice

Ariely writes in a friendly, conversational, self-deprecating style. He uses his own vulnerabilities (the injuries that shaped his perspective) and his students' experiments as narrative material. The voice is approachable, curious, and persuasive.

## Narrative Structure

Each chapter is a self-contained exploration of one irrationality, typically beginning with a question, proceeding through experiments, and ending with implications. This modular structure makes the book easy to dip into.

## Rhetorical Techniques

Ariely relies heavily on experimental demonstration: "What would you choose?" followed by the results. This creates engagement — readers experience the irrationality before having it explained. He also uses personal narrative effectively.

## Readability

Reading level is approximately 9th-10th grade. The book is highly accessible. Experimental methods are explained without jargon. Ariely avoids footnotes and academic references in the main text.

## Comparative Context

*Predictably Irrational* sits at the descriptive end of the decision-making spectrum. It explains why we make the choices we do. It is best read alongside prescriptive books (Smart Choices, Decision Quality) that offer methods for overcoming the biases Ariely documents.
