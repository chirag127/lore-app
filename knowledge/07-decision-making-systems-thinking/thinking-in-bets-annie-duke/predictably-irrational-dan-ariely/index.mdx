Dan Ariely's *Predictably Irrational* is a landmark in behavioral economics — the first popular book to systematically demonstrate through original experiments that human decision-making deviates from rational choice in predictable, systematic ways.

<BookHeader />

Drawing on ingenious experiments conducted over a decade at MIT, Ariely shows that our decisions are shaped not by stable preferences and rational calculation but by context, emotion, comparison, and the order in which options are presented.
