## Chapter 1: The ProACT Approach

The authors introduce the eight elements of any decision and argue that the quality of a decision depends on how well each element is handled. They provide an overview of the method and a roadmap for the book.

## Chapter 2: Problem

Define the problem correctly. The authors show how misdefined problems lead to wrong decisions. Example: an airline that defined its problem as "how to reduce maintenance costs" when the real problem was "how to improve aircraft reliability."

## Chapter 3: Objectives

Identify what matters. Objectives should be comprehensive, measurable, and independent. The authors recommend listing objectives without considering alternatives to avoid rationalization bias.

## Chapter 4: Alternatives

Create good alternatives. The authors distinguish between obviously good alternatives (easily identified) and creative alternatives (requiring deliberate search). They provide techniques for generating alternatives: brainstorming, constraint relaxation, and the "ideal solution" method.

## Chapter 5: Consequences

Describe the consequences of each alternative. The authors introduce the consequences table — a matrix showing each alternative's performance on each objective. They recommend using consistent, measurable scales.

## Chapter 6: Tradeoffs

Make tradeoffs explicit. When objectives conflict (cost vs. quality, speed vs. accuracy), the decision requires explicit tradeoffs. The authors introduce the "even swap" method for identifying and evaluating tradeoffs systematically. They show how to construct indifference curves and use weighting.

## Chapter 7: Uncertainty

Handle uncertainty. The authors distinguish between risk (known probabilities) and uncertainty (unknown probabilities). They introduce decision trees, probability assessment, and expected value calculations. They also address the psychological biases that distort probability judgments.

## Chapter 8: Risk Tolerance

Understand your appetite for risk. The authors show how risk tolerance varies across individuals and situations, and provide tools for aligning decisions with risk preferences.

## Chapter 9: Linked Decisions

Handle decisions that affect future choices. The authors introduce the concept of decision trees with sequential decisions, showing how early choices constrain later options. They emphasize thinking ahead — considering how today's decision affects tomorrow's possibilities.
