## Book Context & Background

*Smart Choices* was published in 1999 at the intersection of two traditions: the academic field of decision analysis (Raiffa was a pioneer) and the popular management literature seeking to apply analytic rigor to business decisions. It followed Raiffa's seminal *Decision Analysis* (1968) and Keeney and Raiffa's *Decisions with Multiple Objectives* (1976), but was the first attempt to make multi-attribute decision analysis accessible to a general audience.

## About the Authors

John S. Hammond was a professor at Harvard Business School and a consultant in decision analysis. Ralph L. Keeney is a professor at Duke University and a leading figure in multi-objective decision analysis. Howard Raiffa, the senior author, was a professor at Harvard Business School and Kennedy School of Government, widely considered the father of decision analysis. Together, they brought over 100 years of combined experience in decision consulting.

## Core Thesis

The ProACT framework provides a universal structure for any decision. By systematically addressing each element — problem, objectives, alternatives, consequences, tradeoffs, uncertainty, risk tolerance, and linked decisions — decision-makers can dramatically improve outcomes regardless of domain.

## Strengths

The book's greatest strength is its systematic completeness. No other popular decision book provides a more comprehensive vocabulary for decision-making. The examples are well-chosen and span multiple domains. The even-swap method for tradeoffs is a genuinely useful technique not found in other popular books.

## Criticisms

The book has been criticized for being overly rationalistic. It assumes that objectives can be fully articulated and weighted, which critics argue underestimates the role of intuition and emergent preferences. The book is also dated in its treatment of uncertainty, predating Taleb's work on black swans and Kahneman's comprehensive catalog of biases. The Journal of Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis review noted that the book sacrifices depth for breadth, with no element treated in sufficient detail for professional use.

## Comparative Analysis

*Smart Choices* is more rigorous than *Decisive* or *Thinking in Bets* but less engaging. It is the closest popular book to an actual decision analysis textbook. Compared to Spetzler's *Decision Quality*, it is more accessible but less focused on organizational implementation.

## Impact & Legacy

The ProACT framework remains a standard reference in decision analysis education. The book is still in print and widely used in MBA programs. Its influence is visible in many subsequent decision-making books that borrow elements of the framework without attribution.
