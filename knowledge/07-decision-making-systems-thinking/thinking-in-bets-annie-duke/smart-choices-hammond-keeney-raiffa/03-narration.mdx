## Writing Style & Voice

The writing is clear, structured, and slightly academic. The three-author collaboration produces a consistent, authoritative voice — never conversational, always instructional. Sentences are precise and definitions are exact. The tone is that of expert consultants explaining their method.

## Narrative Structure

Each chapter follows a consistent pattern: introduce the element, explain why it matters, provide a method for addressing it, illustrate with examples, and summarize. This structure is predictable but effective for learning. The book lacks a central narrative arc; it is a reference work meant to be read sequentially or consulted by need.

## Rhetorical Techniques

The authors rely primarily on logos — logical argument and systematic demonstration. Examples are used for illustration, not emotional impact. The even-swap method is presented as a logical tool, not a story. The book's authority comes from the authors' reputations and the internal consistency of the framework.

## Readability

Reading level is approximately 10th-11th grade. The vocabulary includes technical terms (indifference curve, expected value, dominated alternative) that are explained but require attention. The book includes worksheets and sample calculations.

## Comparative Context

*Smart Choices* is the most textbook-like of the books in this category. It lacks the narrative flair of the Heath brothers and the personal voice of Annie Duke, but it provides a depth of systematic method that neither attempts. It belongs on the shelf of anyone who wants to make decision-making a professional competency rather than an occasional skill.
