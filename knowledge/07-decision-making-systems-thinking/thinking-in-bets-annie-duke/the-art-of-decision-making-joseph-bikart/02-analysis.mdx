## Book Context & Background

*The Art of Decision Making* was published in 2017, joining a crowded field of decision-making books. Bikart distinguished his contribution by focusing on the existential and emotional dimensions of choice — territory largely ignored by the analytic tradition of Raiffa, Hammond, and Keeney, and given only superficial treatment by the popular press.

## About the Author

Joseph Bikart is a French-born business strategist and executive coach based in London. He has advised executives at major corporations on strategic decision-making. His intellectual background combines business strategy, philosophy, and clinical psychology. This is his first book on decision-making.

## Core Thesis

Indecision is not a cognitive problem but an emotional and existential one. The tools for overcoming it are not analytical frameworks but psychological insight and courage.

## Strengths

The book's emphasis on the emotional dimension of decision-making fills a genuine gap. The integration of philosophical and psychological perspectives is thoughtful and well-executed. Bikart's coaching experience gives the book an empathetic, practical quality.

## Criticisms

The book lacks the rigor of academic work or the specificity of popular analytical books. Some reviewers noted that the philosophical references can feel forced. The practical guidance is less specific than readers might expect — the book is stronger on diagnosis than prescription. A review in the Journal of Business Strategy noted that Bikart's framework would benefit from case studies showing its application in organizational settings.

## Comparative Analysis

Bikart's book is closer to Schwartz's *The Paradox of Choice* than to Duke or the Heath brothers — it focuses on the psychological costs of decision-making rather than the mechanics of better decisions. Compared to the analytical tradition, it is more personal and less systematic.
