Joseph Bikart, a business strategist and executive coach, takes a different approach from the decision-analysis tradition. Rather than offering analytical frameworks, he explores the psychological and existential roots of indecision — the fear, anxiety, and avoidance that prevent people from making choices.

<BookHeader />

Drawing on philosophy (Kierkegaard, Sartre, Camus), psychology (Freud, Jung, Kahneman), and his own coaching practice, Bikart argues that the real barrier to good decisions is not lack of information or analytical tools but lack of courage. The book is a meditation on what it means to choose — and why we so often fail to do so.
