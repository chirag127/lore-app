Barry Schwartz's *The Paradox of Choice* is one of the most influential psychology books of the 2000s. It introduced the concept of the "maximizer" vs. the "satisficer" into popular discourse and argued that the explosion of consumer choice in modern society has made us less happy rather than more free.

<BookHeader />

Schwartz grounds his argument in both experimental psychology (Herbert Simon's work on satisficing, research on regret and counterfactual thinking) and vivid examples from everyday life — from choosing a pair of jeans to selecting a health insurance plan.
