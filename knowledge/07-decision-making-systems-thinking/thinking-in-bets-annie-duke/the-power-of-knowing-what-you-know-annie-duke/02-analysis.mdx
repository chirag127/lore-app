## Book Context & Background

*Quit* was published in 2022, four years after *Thinking in Bets*. The persistence-is-always-good cultural narrative had been challenged before — by Seth Godin's *The Dip* (2007) and by research on escalation of commitment — but no book had provided a systematic framework for quitting decisions. Duke filled this gap at a moment when the pandemic had forced many people to reconsider career and life choices.

## About the Author

Annie Duke's poker background makes her an ideal guide to quitting. Professional poker players fold (quit) on most hands; the skill is knowing when. Duke's transition from poker to corporate consulting gives her credibility across both domains.

## Core Thesis

Knowing when to quit is a decision-making skill as important as knowing when to start. The costs of irrational persistence are enormous — wasted time, money, and emotional energy — yet the culture celebrates persistence and stigmatizes quitting.

## Strengths

The book's greatest contribution is making quitting a legitimate decision topic rather than a character failure. The kill-criteria framework is practical and actionable. The opportunity cost framing is compelling. Duke's poker examples translate naturally to the topic.

## Criticisms

The book can feel repetitive — the core idea is strong but perhaps better suited to a long article than a full book. A review in the Financial Times noted that some examples are stretched to fill pages. The overlap with *Thinking in Bets* is significant: readers familiar with Duke's earlier work will recognize most of the psychological concepts.

## Comparative Analysis

Compared to Godin's *The Dip*, Duke's book is more systematic and evidence-based. Godin's book is a short manifesto; Duke's is a full decision framework. Compared to academic work on escalation of commitment (Staw, 1976), Duke's book is more accessible but less rigorous.

## Impact & Legacy

*Quit* has been praised by investors and entrepreneurs for filling a genuine gap in decision-making literature. It has not had the sales impact of *Thinking in Bets* but has been influential in startup and investing communities.
