## Writing Style & Voice

Duke maintains her conversational, direct style. The tone is more urgent than in *Thinking in Bets* — the costs of irrational persistence are high, and she wants readers to act. She uses shorter chapters and more bullet points, giving the book a manifesto-like quality.

## Narrative Structure

Part I diagnoses the problem, Part II provides the solution, Part III applies it. The structure is effective but the middle section can feel like a list of techniques rather than a flowing argument.

## Rhetorical Techniques

Duke uses contrast effectively — juxtaposing stories of irrational persistence with stories of timely quitting. She relies on the sunk cost metaphor repeatedly, which is appropriate given its centrality. The book's rhetoric is designed to make quitting feel like an active, strategic choice rather than a passive failure.

## Readability

Approximately 8th grade reading level. Technical concepts (expected value, opportunity cost) are explained clearly. The book includes summary sections and "try this" exercises.

## Comparative Context

*Quit* is Duke's most focused book — the narrowest topic, the most actionable framework. It complements her earlier books by addressing the specific decision of when to stop, which *Thinking in Bets* and *How to Decide* treated only peripherally.
