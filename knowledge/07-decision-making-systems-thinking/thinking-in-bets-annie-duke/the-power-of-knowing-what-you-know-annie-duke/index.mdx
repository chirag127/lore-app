In a culture that worships persistence — "never give up," "winners never quit" — Annie Duke makes the counterintuitive case that quitting is a skill, not a failure. Drawing on poker, investing, and business, she shows that the best decision-makers are not the ones who persist longest but the ones who know when to fold.

<BookHeader />

The book dismantles the psychological barriers that keep people in losing situations: sunk cost bias, ego, social pressure, and the trained incapacity to quit that comes from being rewarded for persistence. Duke provides a systematic framework for making quit decisions — pre-committing to kill criteria, using expected value calculations, and separating quitting from identity.
