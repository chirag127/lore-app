# 🔢 Math, Logic & Science

## 📚 Overview

This category covers the foundations of rational thought: mathematics, logic, and the scientific method. From the beauty of pure numbers to the mechanics of empirical investigation, these books develop the tools for precise thinking and evidence-based understanding.

The focus is on **formal reasoning** and **scientific methodology** — not applied mathematics (that's integrated into 07) or data science practice (that's 04).

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **04 Computers, AI & Software** covers **applied mathematics** and **computational methods**. This category covers **pure mathematics** and **formal reasoning**.
- **02 Body, Health & Life Sciences** covers **biological sciences** and **medical science**. This category covers the **philosophy and foundations** of science.
- **07 Math** provides the **theoretical foundation**. **08 Society** covers the **social implications** of science.

## 📖 Major Topics

- **Foundational Mathematics**: Set theory, mathematical logic, proof techniques, number theory, discrete math.
- **Algebra & Analysis**: Linear algebra, abstract algebra, real analysis, complex analysis.
- **Geometry & Topology**: Euclidean geometry, differential geometry, algebraic topology.
- **Logic & Foundations**: Propositional logic, predicate logic, model theory, proof theory.
- **Probability & Statistics**: Probability theory, statistical inference, decision theory.
- **History & Philosophy of Science**: Scientific revolution, paradigms, falsifiability, explanation.
- **Computational Mathematics**: Numerical methods, symbolic computation, algorithmic complexity.

## 🧠 Reading Order

1. Start with **Mathematics for the Nonmathematician** for motivation
2. Then **How to Prove It** for logical reasoning
3. Then **Calculus** or **Linear Algebra** for core concepts
4. Then **The Princeton Companion** for breadth
5. Then specialized areas based on interest

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Mathematics is the language of structure and pattern. Logic is the tool of correct reasoning. Science is the method for discovering truth about the world. Mastery of these foundations enables clear thinking, rigorous analysis, and evidence-based decision making across all domains.
