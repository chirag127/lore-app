---
title: "Classical & Operant Conditioning"
parent: "08-psychology/behavioral-psychology-and-learning-theory"
order: 2
description: "The two fundamental learning processes by which organisms form associations between stimuli and responses."
---

Classical conditioning (Pavlov, Watson) involves learning through stimulus association. Operant conditioning (Skinner) involves learning through consequences. Together they explain the acquisition of phobias, habits, preferences, and many automatic behavioral patterns.
