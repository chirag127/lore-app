---
title: "Behavioral Psychology & Learning Theory"
parent: "08-psychology"
order: 1
description: "The study of how behavior is acquired, maintained, and modified through environmental contingencies."
---

Behavioral psychology examines the principles by which organisms learn from their environment. Drawing on classical conditioning (Pavlov), operant conditioning (Skinner), and observational learning (Bandura), this subfield provides the empirical foundation for behavior modification, applied behavior analysis, and habit formation. It remains the most rigorously experimental branch of psychology, with direct applications in education, therapy, and self-improvement.
