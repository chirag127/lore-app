# Cognitive Biases

## Why This Exists

Human beings are not rational agents in the sense assumed by classical economics and traditional logic. Decades of research in cognitive and social psychology — beginning with the pioneering work of Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky in the 1970s — have demonstrated that human judgment is systematically distorted by a finite set of mental shortcuts (heuristics) that produce predictable errors (biases). Understanding these biases is essential not only for psychology but for any domain that involves human judgment: finance, medicine, law, policy, management, and everyday life.

The heuristics and biases research program identified three fundamental heuristics — availability, representativeness, and anchoring and adjustment — and the biases to which each gives rise. Kahneman's subsequent work on prospect theory (with Tversky) provided a descriptive model of decision-making under risk that replaced expected utility theory as the dominant account. More recent research has expanded the catalog of biases to include confirmation bias, hindsight bias, the Dunning-Kruger effect, overconfidence, the planning fallacy, and many others. The dual-process theory of cognition — System 1 (fast, intuitive, automatic) versus System 2 (slow, deliberative, analytical) — provides an organizing framework for understanding when and why biases emerge.

## What Belongs Here

The complete catalog of cognitive biases, heuristics and their effects, dual-process theories of cognition, debiasing techniques and interventions, the relationship between intelligence and bias susceptibility, bias in specific domains (medical diagnosis, legal judgment, investment decisions, personnel selection), individual and cultural differences in bias, and the replication and extension of classic heuristics and biases findings.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Social biases and prejudice (those are studied in Social Psychology under intergroup relations), general cognitive psychology of attention and memory (belongs in Cognitive Psychology), and clinical cognitive distortions studied in psychopathology (belongs in Clinical Psychology).

## Essential Books

- Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — The definitive synthesis of the heuristics and biases research program
- Daniel Kahneman, Paul Slovic & Amos Tversky, eds., *Judgment Under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases* — The foundational collection of papers
- Amos Tversky & Daniel Kahneman, *Choices, Values, and Frames* — Prospect theory and framing effects
- Richard H. Thaler, *Misbehaving: The Making of Behavioral Economics* — The application of biases to economic behavior
- Dan Ariely, *Predictably Irrational* — Accessible demonstrations of systematic irrationality
- Gerd Gigerenzer, *Simple Heuristics That Make Us Smart* — An alternative perspective arguing heuristics are adaptive
- Robert Cialdini, *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* — Bias in social influence contexts
- Jonathan Baron, *Thinking and Deciding* — A comprehensive textbook on judgment and decision-making
