Brainstorm, published in 2013, is Daniel Siegel's most optimistic book about the teenage years. Countering the dominant cultural narrative that adolescence is a period of inevitable turmoil and risk, Siegel argues that the very characteristics parents find most challenging about teenagers — emotional intensity, novelty-seeking, social engagement, and creative exploration — are the brain's adaptive design for launching the young adult into an independent life.

The book is organized around the acronym ESSENCE: Emotional Spark, Social Engagement, Novelty-seeking, and Creative Explorations. Each of these qualities, Siegel argues, emerges from specific brain changes during adolescence and serves an essential developmental purpose. Brainstorm offers a framework for parents and teens to navigate the adolescent years not as a storm to be weathered but as a period of remarkable growth.
