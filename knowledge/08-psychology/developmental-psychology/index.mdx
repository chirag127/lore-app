---
title: "Developmental Psychology"
parent: "08-psychology"
order: 4
description: "The study of how humans change across the lifespan — cognitively, socially, and emotionally."
---

Developmental psychology traces the trajectory of human growth from infancy through old age. It covers attachment theory, Piaget's stages of cognitive development, moral development (Kohlberg), identity formation (Erikson), and the distinct challenges of adolescence. It provides the empirical basis for understanding how early experience shapes adult outcomes and how development continues across the entire lifespan.
