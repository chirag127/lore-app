Mindsight, published in 2010, is Daniel Siegel's first book written specifically for a general adult audience — not parents, not clinicians, but anyone interested in how the mind works and how it can change. It introduces the concept of mindsight: a focused form of attention that allows people to perceive their own mental processes and those of others. Siegel argues that mindsight is the fundamental skill underlying emotional intelligence, empathy, and personal transformation.

The book bridges the gap between The Developing Mind (1999), Siegel's academic magnum opus, and his more accessible parenting books. It presents the same interpersonal neurobiology framework in a format accessible to any reader, using case studies from Siegel's clinical practice to illustrate how mindsight changes lives. The book has been translated into more than 20 languages and introduced the concept of mindsight — now widely used in therapy, education, and leadership training.
