No-Drama Discipline, published in 2014, is the second collaboration between Daniel J. Siegel and Tina Payne Bryson following the bestselling The Whole-Brain Child. Where their first book focused on helping children build integrated brains through everyday interactions, No-Drama Discipline tackles the most emotionally charged aspect of parenting: how to respond when children misbehave.

The book argues that most discipline strategies fail because they focus on stopping behavior in the moment rather than building the neural architecture for self-regulation, empathy, and problem-solving over time. Drawing on brain science, attachment research, and decades of clinical experience, Siegel and Bryson offer an approach that treats discipline as teaching — not punishment. The book provides concrete strategies organized around the mantra "connect first, then redirect."
