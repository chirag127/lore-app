NurtureShock, published in 2009, is a collection of ten chapters — originally conceived as magazine articles — each focused on a piece of conventional parenting wisdom that turns out to be wrong. Written by journalist Po Bronson and Ashley Merryman, the book draws on hundreds of research studies to challenge common assumptions about praise, sleep, sibling relationships, language development, lying, race, teen rebellion, and self-control.

The book's subtitle, "New Thinking About Children," captures its mission: to replace feel-good parenting advice with evidence-based understanding. Each chapter opens with a common belief ("praise builds confidence," "talking to babies builds their vocabulary," "teens rebel because of hormones") and then systematically dismantles it with research. NurtureShock became a New York Times bestseller and sparked widespread conversation about the gap between parenting culture and developmental science.
