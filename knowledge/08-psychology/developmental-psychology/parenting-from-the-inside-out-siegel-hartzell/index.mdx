Parenting from the Inside Out, first published in 2003, was Daniel Siegel's first book written for a general audience and remains a landmark text in attachment-informed parenting. Co-authored with early childhood educator Mary Hartzell, the book argues that the most important tool parents have is not any technique but their own self-understanding. Drawing on attachment research, neurobiology, and mindfulness, Siegel and Hartzell show how parents' unresolved childhood experiences — stored as implicit memories in the brain — can hijack parenting responses in ways that replicate old patterns.

The book emerged from a series of parent workshops that combined Siegel's clinical research on interpersonal neurobiology with Hartzell's decades of experience in early childhood education. The 10th anniversary edition (2013) incorporates updated research on neuroplasticity and attachment. Unlike most parenting books that focus on what to do, Parenting from the Inside Out focuses on who to be — and how to understand the forces inside that shape how parents show up.
