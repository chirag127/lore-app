The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog, first published in 2006 (updated in 2017), is Bruce Perry's most famous book — a collection of clinical case studies that illustrate how trauma affects the developing brain and how healing happens through relationship. Each chapter tells the story of a child Perry treated, from the titular boy raised in a cage to children who witnessed genocide, experienced extreme neglect, or suffered sexual abuse.

The book is organized around the neurosequential model of development — Perry's framework for understanding how the brain organizes from simplest (brainstem) to most complex (cortex) and how trauma disrupts this sequential organization. The message is both devastating and hopeful: trauma damages children, but with patient, attuned care, even the most profound wounds can heal.
