The End of Average, published in 2016, is Harvard scientist Todd Rose's subversive and readable manifesto against what he calls "averagarianism"—the pervasive belief that the average of any group tells us something meaningful about the individuals within it. Rose argues that this assumption, inherited from 19th-century statistics and industrial-age management, has distorted education, hiring, healthcare, and virtually every institution that makes consequential decisions about human potential.

The book weaves together the history of statistical thinking (Quetelet, Galton, Taylor), concrete failures of average-based design (the US Air Force cockpit disaster), and the emerging "science of the individual" to show that no one is average. Rose introduces three principles—jaggedness, context, and pathways—that reveal our true individuality and provide a framework for redesigning institutions around the people they are supposed to serve. A companion volume, Dark Horse (2018, with Ogi Ogas), applied these principles to career success.
