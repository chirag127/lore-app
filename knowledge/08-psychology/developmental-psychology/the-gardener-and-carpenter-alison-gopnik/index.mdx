The Gardener and the Carpenter, published in 2016, is Alison Gopnik's most ambitious book for a general audience. A leading developmental psychologist at UC Berkeley, Gopnik argues that the dominant metaphor for parenting — the child as material to be shaped into a particular outcome — is both scientifically wrong and practically harmful. She proposes an alternative: parents are gardeners, not carpenters, providing the conditions for growth rather than trying to engineer specific results.

The book synthesizes evolution, neuroscience, and developmental psychology into a powerful argument that children are designed to be variable, exploratory, and shaped by multiple influences. Gopnik challenges the assumption that parental inputs directly determine child outcomes and argues for a more humble, scientifically grounded view of what parents can and should do.
