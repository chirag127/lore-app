---
title: "Brain-Behavior Relationships"
parent: "08-psychology/neuropsychology-and-biological-psychology"
order: 1
description: "How the structure and function of the brain gives rise to behavior and cognition."
---

Clinical neuropsychology uses lesion studies, neuroimaging (fMRI, PET, EEG), and cognitive assessment to map brain regions to psychological functions. Topics include hemispheric specialization, localization of function, neuroplasticity, and the neural bases of memory, emotion, and executive control.
