description: "The study of the biological foundations of behavior, emotion, and cognition."
---

Biological psychology examines the neural, hormonal, and genetic mechanisms underlying psychological processes. Neuropsychology studies brain-behavior relationships through lesion studies, neuroimaging, and cognitive assessment. Psychopharmacology investigates how drugs affect the brain and behavior. This subfield provides the biological bridge between psychology and neuroscience.
