## Book Summary

**The Highly Sensitive Person: How to Thrive When the World Overwhelms You**
(1996, Broadway Books, ~320 pp; reprint 2013 with new foreword) is the
seminal work by psychologist and researcher **Elaine N. Aron** that brought
the scientifically validated personality trait of **sensory processing
sensitivity (SPS)** — the HSP trait — into mainstream discourse.

Aron's research, originating at **UC Berkeley and institutions including
Stony Brook and UC Santa Cruz**, established that approximately **15–20%
of the population** carries this innate, heritable trait. HSPs process
stimuli — sensory, emotional, and social — more deeply than the
neurotypical majority. Rather than a disorder or weakness, Aron frames SPS
as an **evolutionary adaptation**: organisms who sense subtle changes
earlier had a survival advantage across human and animal evolution.

This archive entry synthesises Aron's core framework, the empirical
evidence for SPS, actionable strategies for managing overstimulation, and
the broader social and cultural implications of the HSP discovery.
