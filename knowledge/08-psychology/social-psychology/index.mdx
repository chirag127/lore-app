---
title: "Social Psychology"
parent: "08-psychology"
order: 10
description: "The study of how people's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are influenced by the actual or imagined presence of others."
---

Social psychology examines the power of situations to shape individual behavior. Its landmark experiments — Milgram's obedience studies, Zimbardo's prison experiment, Asch's conformity studies — reveal the startling extent to which context overwhelms disposition. Topics include group dynamics, conformity and obedience, prejudice and discrimination, prosocial behavior and bystander intervention, and intergroup conflict and cooperation.
