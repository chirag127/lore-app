An investigation of ten Great Ideas from ancient civilizations — Buddha's non-attachment, the Golden Rule, Nietzsche's "what doesn't kill me," Aristotle's virtue ethics — tested against the findings of modern psychological science. Haidt's central metaphor of the rider (conscious reasoning) and the elephant (automatic intuition) explains why we so often know what is good for us yet fail to do it, and offers practical paths to align the two.
