# 🏛 Society, History & Power

## 📚 Overview

This category examines human societies as social systems — how they form, how they change, how power operates within and between them. From ancient civilizations to modern states, from social movements to global governance, these books provide frameworks for understanding the forces that shape human collective life.

The focus is on **social analysis** and **historical understanding** — not political theory (that's 06) or economic systems (that's 03).

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **05 Business, Strategy & Organizations** covers **organizational dynamics**. This category covers **social institutions** and **large-scale historical forces**.
- **06 Philosophy, Religion & Indian Thought** covers **political philosophy**. This category covers **empirical social science** and **historical analysis**.
- **03 Money, Markets & Wealth** covers **economic systems**. This category covers **social outcomes** and **cultural patterns**.

## 📖 Major Topics

- **Social Theory**: Functionalism, conflict theory, symbolic interactionism, network theory, power structures.
- **History**: Ancient civilizations, medieval societies, modern nation-states, global transitions.
- **Political Systems**: Democracy, authoritarianism, nation-states, international relations, global governance.
- **Cultural Studies**: Identity, representation, media, race, gender, class, postcolonial theory.
- **Public Policy**: Healthcare, education, welfare, criminal justice, environmental policy.
- **Geopolitics**: Balance of power, sphere of influence, resource competition, military strategy.
- **Social Movements**: Civil rights, feminism, environmentalism, labor movements, revolutions.

## 🧠 Reading Order

1. Start with **Guns, Germs, and Steel** for big-picture history
2. Then **The Social Animal** for modern social theory
3. Then **A People's History** for alternative perspectives
4. Then **The Structure of Social Theory** for deeper frameworks
5. Then specialized areas based on interest
