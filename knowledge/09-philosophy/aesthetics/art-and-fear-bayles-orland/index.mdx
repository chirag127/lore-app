*Art and Fear* is a small book with a large reputation. Written by two working artists, David Bayles (photographer) and Ted Orland (photographer and writer), it addresses the singular question: why do so many people who start making art stop — and what keeps the others going? The answer, they argue, is fear: fear of failure, fear of judgment, fear of not being good enough, fear that the work doesn't matter.

The book is not a philosophy of art in the grand tradition of Hegel or Dewey. It is a practical, empathetic, often funny guide to the inner life of the artist, written from the trenches rather than the academy. Its wisdom is concrete, its examples drawn from the authors' own studio practice and teaching experience.

<BookHeader />

David Bayles is a photographer and writer whose work has appeared in numerous publications. Ted Orland (1939-2014) was a photographer, writer, and longtime assistant to Ansel Adams. He taught at the University of California, Santa Cruz, and was known for his wit and directness. Together they wrote the book that became a cult classic among artists of all media.
