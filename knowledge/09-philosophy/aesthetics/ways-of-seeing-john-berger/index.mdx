John Berger's *Ways of Seeing* (1972) is one of the most widely read and influential books on art ever published. Based on a four-part BBC television series created with Mike Dibb, the book consists of seven essays, three of which use only images. Berger draws on Walter Benjamin's concept of mechanical reproduction and Marxist analysis to argue that the Western tradition of oil painting is inextricably linked to the ideology of private property, and that the female nude is structured by the male gaze.

<BookHeader />

Born in London in 1926, John Berger was an art critic, novelist, and painter. His novel *G.* won the Booker Prize in 1972. Deeply influenced by Marxist thought and the writings of Walter Benjamin, Berger spent much of his life in the French Alps. *Ways of Seeing* was conceived as a direct response to Kenneth Clark's *Civilisation* series, which Berger saw as a conservative, mystifying account of Western art.
