Arthur Danto's *What Art Is* (2013) is the final book by one of the most influential philosophers of art of the late twentieth century. Published in the year of his death, it distills a career's worth of reflection into a compact, accessible argument for a real definition of art. Against the prevailing view that art is an open or indefinable concept, Danto insists that art can be defined: it is embodied meaning.

<BookHeader />

Born in Ann Arbor in 1924, Arthur Danto was Johnsonian Professor of Philosophy at Columbia University and art critic for *The Nation* for twenty-five years. He was deeply influenced by Hegel, Nietzsche, and the analytic tradition. His earlier book *The Transfiguration of the Commonplace* (1981) established his theory of indiscernibles; *What Art Is* is its final, definitive statement.
