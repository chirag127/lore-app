# Eastern Philosophy

## Overview

Eastern philosophy encompasses the major philosophical traditions of East Asia. Chinese philosophy includes Confucianism (ethics, social harmony, ritual), Daoism (the Way, naturalness, non-action), and Legalism (pragmatic statecraft). Japanese philosophy integrates indigenous Shinto thought, Buddhist and Confucian influences, and the distinctive Kyoto School of modern philosophy. These traditions often emphasize practice, harmony, and the interdependence of all things over abstract metaphysical system-building.
