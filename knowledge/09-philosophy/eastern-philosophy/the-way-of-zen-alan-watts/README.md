## Key Critics Referenced

1. **D.T. Suzuki** — criticized Watts for overemphasizing Rinzai sudden-enlightenment at expense of Soto gradual practice
2. **Philip Kapleau** — *The Three Pillars of Zen* (1965) — argued Watts dismissed zazen based on "only half a koan"
3. **Robert Sharf** — contemporary scholar, critiqued Watts's "modernist" Zen shaped by Western assumptions
4. **Dale S. Wright** — critiqued romanticization of Zen in Western interpretation
5. **James Ford** — Zen priest/historian, nuanced assessment of Watts's influence and misrepresentation
6. **Shunryu Suzuki** — defended Watts as a "great bodhisattva" for bringing Zen to the West
