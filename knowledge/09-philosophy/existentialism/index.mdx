# Existentialism

## Overview

Existentialism emerged in 19th- and 20th-century European thought, emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and responsibility. Key figures include Kierkegaard (the leap of faith), Nietzsche (the will to power, eternal recurrence), Heidegger (Being-toward-death), Sartre (existence precedes essence, radical freedom), Beauvoir (the ethics of ambiguity), and Camus (the absurd). Despite their differences, existentialists share a focus on authentic living in the face of contingency, finitude, and meaninglessness.
