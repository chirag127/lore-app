# Indian Philosophy

## Overview

Indian philosophy represents one of the world's oldest continuous philosophical traditions, encompassing systematic inquiry into metaphysics, epistemology, logic, ethics, and liberation. It is traditionally organized into orthodox schools (astika) that accept Veda authority — Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Samkhya, Yoga, Purva Mimamsa, and Vedanta (Advaita, Dvaita, Vishishtadvaita) — and heterodox schools (nastika) that do not — Buddhism, Jainism, Carvaka. Each school developed sophisticated philosophical systems with rigorous debate across traditions.
