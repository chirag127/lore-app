# Logic & Critical Thinking

## Overview

Logic is the science of valid inference — the study of what follows from what. Formal logic develops precise languages for representing arguments and proving their validity. Informal logic and argumentation theory analyze reasoning in natural language, including fallacies, dialectical structure, and the norms of rational debate. Critical thinking applies logical principles to real-world reasoning, emphasizing the evaluation of evidence, identification of assumptions, and avoidance of cognitive biases.
