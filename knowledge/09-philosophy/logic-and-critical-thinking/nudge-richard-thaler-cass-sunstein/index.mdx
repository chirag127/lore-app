<BookHeader />

Nudge introduced the world to choice architecture — the idea that how options are presented powerfully shapes what people choose. Written by Nobel laureate Richard Thaler and legal scholar Cass Sunstein, the book argues that since choice environments are never neutral, policy makers and institutions should design them to improve welfare while preserving freedom. The concept sparked hundreds of government nudge units worldwide and reshaped how nations approach retirement savings, organ donation, health care, and climate policy.
