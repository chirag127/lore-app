*Philosophical Investigations* is Ludwig Wittgenstein's second masterpiece, published posthumously in 1953. It repudiates the central doctrines of his early work, the *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus*, replacing the idea that language pictures reality with the claim that meaning is determined by use within social practices called language-games. Written as a series of numbered remarks rather than a continuous argument, the book attacks the urge to seek essences, essences, and philosophical foundations, arguing that most philosophical problems are confusions arising from the misuse of language. It is widely regarded as the most important philosophical work of the 20th century.
