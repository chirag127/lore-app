# Political Philosophy

## Overview

Political philosophy asks normative questions about political life: what makes a government legitimate? What is justice? What rights do individuals have? How should resources be distributed? It spans the history of political thought from Plato to Rawls and engages contemporary debates about liberalism, communitarianism, libertarianism, and critical theory. Political philosophy differs from political science by focusing on how things ought to be rather than how they are.
