# Western Philosophy by Era

## Overview

Western philosophy is conventionally periodized into ancient (Pre-Socratics through Neoplatonism), medieval (Augustine through Ockham), modern (Descartes through Kant), and contemporary (19th century to present). Each era addresses characteristic problems — ancient: the nature of reality and the good life; medieval: faith and reason; modern: the foundations of knowledge and the subject; contemporary: language, history, and the critique of tradition.
