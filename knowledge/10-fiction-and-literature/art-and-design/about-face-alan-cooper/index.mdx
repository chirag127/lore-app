*About Face* began as Alan Cooper's practical guide to interaction design, first published in 1995 when the field was barely recognized as a discipline. Over three editions, it evolved into the definitive textbook, co-authored with Robert Reimann and David Cronin. The book provides a complete methodology — from user research through requirements definition to detailed design — built on the foundation of goal-directed design.

Unlike more abstract design books, *About Face* gives practitioners concrete methods: how to create personas, how to write scenarios, how to define requirements, and how to design interactions that align with human goals rather than technical capabilities.
