Steve Krug's *Don't Make Me Think* is the most widely recommended introductory book on web usability for one simple reason: it is short, funny, and practical. The title is its thesis: the most important design goal is to make the user's cognitive load as low as possible. Every question mark a user encounters is a failure of design.

Krug brings a commonsense approach that demystifies usability. He shows that designing for the web is not about following complex rules but about understanding a few basic principles of human behavior: people scan, they satisfice, and they do not want to figure things out.
