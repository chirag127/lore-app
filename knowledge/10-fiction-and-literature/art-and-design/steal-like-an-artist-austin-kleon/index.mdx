# Steal Like an Artist

> "Nothing is original. Every act of creation is an act of recombination."

## Overview

_Steal Like an Artist_ is Austin Kleon's deceptively simple and beautifully illustrated guide to creativity. Written in a direct, manifesto style, Kleon argues that creativity is not about inventing something entirely new — it's about collecting, remixing, and transforming influences. The book's 10 brief chapters each present a principle, supported by anecdote, quote, and Kleon's own artwork and handwriting.

Published in 2012 by Workman, the slim 160-page book became a surprise hit, eventually translated into more than 40 languages. Kleon's central argument is that there is no shame in "stealing" — on the contrary, imitation is how every artist finds a voice. The book is part manifesto, part memoir, part art object.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Steal like an artist.** Nothing is original; creativity is about remixing and recombining influences.
2. **Start copying.** Before you have a style, you copy those you admire — that's how all great artists begin.
3. **Make the work you want to see in the world.** Don't hold back waiting for permission.
4. **Write the book you want to read.** Create the work that satisfies your own taste.
5. **Creative work is a process of subtraction, not addition.** Less is more.
6. **Keep a daily logbook.** Collect, don't create — accumulate material.
7. **Get a day job.** Financial freedom gives you creative freedom.
8. **Travel changes your work.** Move through new spaces to see differently.
9. **Be nice.** The world is small; kindness gives you access.
10. **Create the work that only you can make.** Your secret interests and obsessions are your unique power.

## Who Should Read

- **Writers, designers, artists, and musicians** who feel blocked or unoriginal
- **Creative professionals** struggling with self-doubt or feeling they have nothing new to say
- **Students** beginning a creative practice
- **任何人 who wants a fast, memorable, and literally illustrated motivation boost**

## Who Should Skip

- **Those looking for a detailed academic grounding** in the psychology of creativity
- **Storytellers who prefer traditional prose format** over Kleon's collage/playground approach
- **Readers seeking a comprehensive systematic method** — this is a booklet of sparks, not a workshop

---

## Core Themes

- All creativity is recombination
- Creative community (or its opposite, isolation) is the decisive context
- Public sharing is as important as private work
- Constraints breed creativity
- The strangeness of your own obsessions is your signature
- Authenticity over artistic purity
- The tension between amateur and professional
- Collecting and documenting as creative acts

---

## Why It Matters

In a culture that endlessly asks "how do I be creative?", Kleon gives the counterintuitive answer: stop trying. Load up on influences. Document your life. Be an amateur. Make things. A generation of internet creators has adopted Kleon's "steal" vocabulary (often stripped of its original nuance) — making the book more culturally influential than many weightier volumes on the psychology of creativity.

## Final Verdict

_Rating: 8/10_

_Strength:_ Accessible, compact, visually distinctive, and culturally catalytic.
_Weakness:_ Some readers find it undersupplied — either because it's too brief or because its message is "obvious" once stated plainly.
_Verdict:_ Read it in an hour. It will unlock something. That's enough.
