In *The Design of Future Things*, Don Norman turns his attention to the coming wave of smart, autonomous products — cars that drive themselves, homes that anticipate our needs, appliances that make decisions without our input. He argues that engineers are building these systems without understanding how people actually interact with machines.

Norman's central warning: adding intelligence to products does not automatically make them better. When machines take control without clear communication, humans are left confused, frustrated, and sometimes endangered. The book offers a framework for designing human-machine collaboration that respects human autonomy while leveraging machine capabilities.
