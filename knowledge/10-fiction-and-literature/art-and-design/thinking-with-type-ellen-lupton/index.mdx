Ellen Lupton's *Thinking with Type* is the most widely used introductory typography textbook in the English-speaking world. First published in 2004, it demystifies typography for designers, writers, editors, and students by presenting its principles clearly with striking visual examples.

The book is organized around three core topics — letter, text, grid — each covering the fundamental knowledge needed to work with type effectively. Lupton, a curator at the Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum and director of the Graphic Design MFA program at MICA, combines historical context, practical guidance, and critical analysis. The result is a book that teaches not just how to set type but how to think about type as a medium.
