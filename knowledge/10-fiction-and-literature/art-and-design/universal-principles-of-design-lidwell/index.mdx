*Universal Principles of Design* is the most comprehensive single-volume reference on design principles ever published. Each two-page spread covers one principle — from the 80/20 Rule to Zeigarnik Effect — with a definition, research background, examples, and practical application. The book deliberately bridges design disciplines: a principle from architecture (Defensible Space) sits alongside one from graphic design (Figure-Ground Relationship) and one from psychology (Cognitive Dissonance).

The book's core premise is that effective design rests on evidence-based principles that transcend medium, style, or culture. Understanding these principles enables designers to make better decisions and communicate more effectively with colleagues.
