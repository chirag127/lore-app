*The Autobiography of Malcolm X* is the collaborative memoir of Malcolm X, as told to journalist Alex Haley, published in 1965 shortly after Malcolm assassination. It chronicles Malcolm Little journey through poverty, crime, imprisonment, religious conversion, political activism, and finally a transformative pilgrimage to Mecca that reshaped his worldview. The book has sold over six million copies and been named by *Time* as one of the ten most important nonfiction books of the twentieth century. It remains the definitive entry point into the life and thought of one of America most complex and consequential public figures.
