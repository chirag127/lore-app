*Steve Jobs* by Walter Isaacson is the authorized biography of the Apple co-founder, published just 19 days after Jobs death in October 2011. Based on more than 40 interviews with Jobs and over 100 conversations with family, friends, colleagues, and competitors, Isaacson paints an unflinching portrait of a man whose intensity, vision, and perfectionism transformed personal computing, animated movies, music, phones, and tablet publishing. The book does not shy away from Jobs darker traits — his cruelty, his tantrums, his reality distortion field — but it also captures the magnetism that drove people to do the best work of their lives.
