*The Rest Is Noise* is the definitive popular history of twentieth-century classical music, told with the narrative sweep of a novel and the intellectual precision of a critic. Alex Ross, music critic for *The New Yorker*, traces the journey from the opulent tonality of Richard Strauss to the austere minimalism of Philip Glass, showing how composers responded to -- and shaped -- the most turbulent century in human history. The book won the National Book Critics Circle Award and was a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize.
