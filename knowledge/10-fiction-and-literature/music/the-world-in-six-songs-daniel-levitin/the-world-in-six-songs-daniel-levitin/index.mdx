*The World in Six Songs* extends Daniel Levitin's exploration of music and the brain from the mechanistic (how the brain processes sound) to the evolutionary (why the brain processes sound at all). Levitin argues that music is not a cultural ornament but a biological adaptation that shaped human nature. He identifies six fundamental song types -- friendship, joy, comfort, knowledge, religion, and love -- each serving a distinct evolutionary function that contributed to the emergence of human social intelligence.
