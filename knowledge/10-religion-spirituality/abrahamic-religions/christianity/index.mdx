# Christianity

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Christianity emerged in the 1st century CE as a messianic movement within Second Temple Judaism centered on Jesus of Nazareth, whom Christians affirm as the Christ (Messiah) and incarnate Son of God. Its sacred scripture comprises the Old Testament (shared with Judaism) and the New Testament (Gospels, Epistles, Revelation). Christianity developed through the Patristic period (Church Fathers, ecumenical councils), the medieval synthesis (Augustine, Thomas Aquinas, Scholasticism), the Reformation (Luther, Calvin, the Radical Reformation), and the modern era (liberal theology, evangelicalism, liberation theology, Pentecostalism).

With roughly 2.4 billion adherents worldwide across three major branches — Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant — Christianity is the largest religion on earth. Its theology, institutional structures, ethical teachings, and cultural influence require dedicated treatment.

## What Belongs Here

Biblical studies (New Testament and Old Testament), Patristics and Church history, systematic theology (Christology, soteriology, eschatology, Trinity), denominational studies (Catholicism, Eastern Orthodoxy, Protestantism, Anglicanism, Restorationist movements), Christian ethics, liturgy and sacraments, modern Christian movements (liberation theology, evangelicalism, Pentecostalism), ecumenism, Christian art and music in their religious context.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

The historical Jesus as a figure of secular history (belongs in History of the Ancient Near East or Historical Jesus studies), Christian mysticism as a distinct contemplative tradition (belongs in Christian Mysticism), Church-state relations from a political science perspective (belongs in Religion & Society), Christian art separated from liturgical context (belongs in Art History).

## Key Scriptures/Texts

- **Old Testament** (Hebrew Bible) — In Christian ordering
- **New Testament**: Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John), Acts of the Apostles, Pauline Epistles, General Epistles, Revelation
- **Apostolic Fathers** (Clement, Ignatius, Polycarp)
- **Creeds**: Nicene Creed, Apostles' Creed, Chalcedonian Definition
- **Augustine**: Confessions, City of God, On the Trinity
- **Thomas Aquinas**: Summa Theologica
- **Luther's Small Catechism**, Calvin's Institutes of the Christian Religion

## Key Concepts

Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit), Incarnation, Atonement (substitution, ransom, Christus Victor), Grace, Faith, Justification, Sanctification, Sacraments (Baptism, Eucharist), Ecclesiology (doctrine of the Church), Eschatology (last things), Salvation, Sin, Redemption, the Kingdom of God.

## Connections

- **Judaism** is Christianity's parent faith; the Old Testament is foundational.
- **Islam** reveres Jesus as a prophet and shares many biblical figures.
- **Christian Mysticism** deepens the contemplative and experiential dimension.
- **Comparative Religion** examines Christianity alongside other world religions.
- **Religion & Society** studies Christianity's role in politics, culture, and secularization.
