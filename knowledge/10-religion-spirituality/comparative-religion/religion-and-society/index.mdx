# Religion & Society

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Religion never exists in a vacuum: it shapes and is shaped by the societies in which it is embedded. Religion & Society examines this two-way relationship through the lenses of sociology, political science, and cultural studies. Foundational thinkers — Émile Durkheim (The Elementary Forms of Religious Life), Max Weber (The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism), and Peter Berger (The Sacred Canopy) — established the frameworks for analyzing religion as a social phenomenon.

Key topics include secularization theory (is modernization incompatible with religion?), civil religion (Robert Bellah), fundamentalism (both religious and political), religion and politics (theocracy, secularism, established churches), religious freedom and toleration, religion and law (Sharia in modern states, religious exemption), religion and gender (patriarchy in religious institutions, feminist theology), and the resurgence of religion in the public sphere.

## What Belongs Here

Sociology of religion (Durkheim, Weber, Berger), political theology (Carl Schmitt, Johann Baptist Metz), religion and law (First Amendment jurisprudence, European secularism), fundamentalism studies (the Fundamentalism Project at the University of Chicago), religion and violence, religion and science debates, religion and gender/sexuality, religious demographics, new religious movements in social context, religion and globalization.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

The internal theological debates of any particular religion (belongs in that religion's subcategory), the history of religion prior to its modern social engagement (belongs in History of World Religions), personal or devotional spirituality (belongs in Mind, Body, Spirit & Spirituality), interfaith dialogue as a distinct practice (belongs in Interfaith Dialogue).

## Key Scriptures/Texts

- Émile Durkheim, The Elementary Forms of Religious Life
- Max Weber, The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism
- Peter L. Berger, The Sacred Canopy
- Robert N. Bellah, "Civil Religion in America"
- Charles Taylor, A Secular Age
- José Casanova, Public Religions in the Modern World
- Mark Juergensmeyer, Terror in the Mind of God

## Key Concepts

Secularization, secularism, civil religion, religious pluralism, privatization of religion, fundamentalism, political Islam, liberation theology, theocracy, laïcité (French secularism), religious exemption, conscientious objection, blasphemy, apostasy, religious nationalism, post-secularism, disenchantment (Weber), sacred canopy (Berger).

## Connections

- **History of World Religions** provides the longue durée backdrop for current social phenomena.
- **Interfaith Dialogue** addresses the practical engagement between traditions inside pluralistic societies.
- **Philosophy of Religion (14)** examines rational arguments about God, the problem of evil, and religious epistemology.
- **Sociology (17)** provides the disciplinary methodology for social analysis.
