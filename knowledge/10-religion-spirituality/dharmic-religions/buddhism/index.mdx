# Buddhism

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Buddhism arose in the 5th–6th century BCE in northeastern India with Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha ("the Awakened One"), who diagnosed the human condition as pervaded by suffering (dukkha) and prescribed the Noble Eightfold Path as the way to liberation (nirvana). Rejecting both the eternal self (atman) of Hindu orthodoxy and outright materialism, the Buddha taught the Middle Way between asceticism and indulgence.

After the Buddha's death, Buddhism split into multiple schools. The oldest surviving tradition, Theravada ("Way of the Elders"), emphasizes monastic discipline and the Pali Canon. Mahayana Buddhism (including Zen, Pure Land, and Tibetan Vajrayana) developed new scriptures and doctrines — the bodhisattva ideal, emptiness (shunyata), Buddha-nature — and spread throughout East Asia. Vajrayana, dominant in Tibet and Mongolia, incorporates tantric methods and elaborate ritual. Today Buddhism has roughly 500 million adherents worldwide.

## What Belongs Here

The life of the Buddha, the Pali Canon and its commentaries, Abhidhamma philosophy, Theravada practice (monasticism, vipassana), Mahayana sutras (Lotus, Heart, Diamond, Pure Land), Madhyamaka emptiness philosophy (Nagarjuna), Yogacara idealism (Asanga, Vasubandhu), Zen/Chan (koans, zazen, sudden enlightenment), Pure Land Buddhism (faith in Amitabha), Tibetan Buddhism (Dalai Lama, stages of the path, Dzogchen, Mahamudra), Buddhist ethics (Pancha Shila, Vinaya), modern Buddhism (Engaged Buddhism, Western Buddhism).

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Mindfulness as a secular therapeutic practice (belongs in Mindfulness & Meditation), Indian history during the Buddhist period (belongs in Indian History), Hindu or Jain philosophy as background (belongs in their respective subcategories), comparative philosophical analysis of Buddhist and Western concepts (belongs in Indian Philosophy or Philosophy), the political history of Tibet (belongs in History of East Asia).

## Key Scriptures/Texts

- **Pali Canon (Tipitaka)**: Vinaya Pitaka (monastic rules), Sutta Pitaka (discourses), Abhidhamma Pitaka (philosophy)
- **Mahayana Sutras**: Lotus Sutra, Heart Sutra, Diamond Sutra, Pure Land Sutras, Lankavatara Sutra, Avatamsaka Sutra
- **Nagarjuna**: Mulamadhyamakakarika (Fundamental Wisdom of the Middle Way)
- **Shantideva**: Bodhicaryavatara (Guide to the Bodhisattva's Way of Life)
- **Milinda Panha** (Questions of King Milinda)

## Key Concepts

Four Noble Truths, Noble Eightfold Path, Dukkha (suffering), Anicca (impermanence), Anatta (no-self), Karma (volitional action), Samsara (rebirth), Nirvana (liberation), Pratityasamutpada (dependent origination), Shunyata (emptiness), Bodhisattva (enlightenment-being), Upaya (skillful means), Bhavana (meditation), Sila (ethics), Samadhi (concentration), Prajna (wisdom).
