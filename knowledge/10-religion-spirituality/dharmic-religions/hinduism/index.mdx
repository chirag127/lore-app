# Hinduism

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Hinduism is not a single religion but a vast and diverse family of traditions that originated in the Indian subcontinent over four millennia. It has no single founder, no single creed, and no centralized authority. Its earliest layers are the Vedas (c. 1500–500 BCE), composed in Sanskrit by the Indo-Aryan peoples, followed by the philosophical Upanishads, the epic narratives of the Ramayana and Mahabharata (which contains the Bhagavad Gita), and the Puranas. Hindu traditions developed through the classical age (Gupta period), the Bhakti movement (medieval devotionalism), the encounter with Islam and British colonialism, and modern reform movements (Vivekananda, Gandhi, Aurobindo).

The category includes the six orthodox schools of Hindu philosophy (Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Samkhya, Yoga, Purva Mimamsa, Vedanta), multiple streams of theology (Vaishnavism, Shaivism, Shaktism, Smartism), and diverse practices ranging from temple ritual and pilgrimage to yogic meditation and tantric initiation.

## What Belongs Here

Vedic and Upanishadic studies, the six orthodox schools (darshanas), Hindu theology (Vaishnavism, Shaivism, Shaktism, Smartism), the Bhagavad Gita and its commentaries, the Puranas and Itihasas (Ramayana, Mahabharata), Bhakti traditions (Alvars, Nayanars, Chaitanya, Mirabai, Tulsidas), Hindu law (Dharmashastra), temple architecture and ritual, caste and its religious justification, modern Hinduism (Arya Samaj, Brahmo Samaj, ISKCON, Ramakrishna Mission), Hinduism and nationalism.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Indian philosophy as a philosophical rather than religious discipline (belongs in Indian Philosophy), Hindu mysticism and Tantra as a distinct esoteric tradition (belongs in Hindu Mysticism & Tantra), Buddhism and Jainism as independent religions (they have their own subcategories), the secular history of India (belongs in History of South Asia).

## Key Scriptures/Texts

- **Shruti** ("that which is heard"): Vedas (Rig, Yajur, Sama, Atharva), Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads
- **Smriti** ("that which is remembered"): Ramayana (Valmiki), Mahabharata (Vyasa), Bhagavad Gita, Puranas, Manusmriti
- **Darshanas**: Yoga Sutras (Patanjali), Brahma Sutras (Badarayana), Nyaya Sutras, Vaisheshika Sutras
- **Devotional**: Bhagavata Purana, Tirukkural (Tamil), works of Tulsidas (Ramcharitmanas), Mirabai, Kabir

## Key Concepts

Dharma (cosmic order/duty), Karma (action/reaction), Samsara (cycle of rebirth), Moksha (liberation), Atman (self/soul), Brahman (ultimate reality), Advaita (non-duality), Bhakti (devotion), Jnana (knowledge), Karma Yoga (path of action), Raja Yoga (path of meditation), Ahimsa (non-violence), Varna (social class), Ashrama (stages of life), Purusharthas (four aims of life: dharma, artha, kama, moksha).

## Connections

- **Buddhism** and **Jainism** emerged as reform movements within the same cultural milieu.
- **Sikhism** shares concepts of karma, rebirth, and divine unity.
- **Hindu Mysticism & Tantra** treats the esoteric and yogic dimensions.
- **Indian Philosophy (14)** covers the philosophical schools that intersect with Hindu theology.
- **Comparative Religion** situates Hinduism globally.
