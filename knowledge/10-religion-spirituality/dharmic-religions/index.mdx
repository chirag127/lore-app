# Dharmic Religions

## Overview

The dharmic religions share key concepts including dharma (cosmic order, duty), karma (action and its consequences), and moksha/nirvana (liberation from the cycle of rebirth). Hinduism is the oldest, a diverse family of traditions rather than a single creed. Buddhism arose as a reform movement emphasizing the Middle Way. Jainism stresses non-violence (ahimsa) and asceticism. Sikhism synthesizes Bhakti devotion and Islamic mystical influences.
