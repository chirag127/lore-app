# East Asian Religions

## Overview

East Asian religions are often described more as philosophical systems or ways of life than Western-style religions. Confucianism emphasizes social harmony through ritual, filial piety, and ethical cultivation. Daoism focuses on harmony with the Dao (the Way), naturalness (ziran), and non-action (wuwei). Shinto, indigenous to Japan, centers on kami (spirits) and ritual purity. These traditions have coexisted and intermingled for centuries.
