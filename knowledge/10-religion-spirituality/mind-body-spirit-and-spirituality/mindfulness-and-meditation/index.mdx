# Mindfulness & Meditation

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Mindfulness — the practice of maintaining non-judgmental awareness of the present moment — has roots in Buddhist Vipassana (insight) meditation and Yogic traditions, but has been adapted in recent decades into secular and therapeutic contexts. Dr. Jon Kabat-Zinn's Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) program, developed at the University of Massachusetts Medical School in 1979, was the watershed event that launched mindfulness into mainstream Western medicine, psychology, and popular culture.

The field now encompasses a vast range of practices: focused attention meditation (samatha), open monitoring (vipassana), loving-kindness meditation (metta), body scans, mindful breathing, walking meditation, yoga nidra, and secular "mindfulness exercises." A substantial body of scientific research has demonstrated benefits for stress reduction, emotional regulation, pain management, and cognitive performance, leading to the integration of mindfulness into schools, corporations, prisons, and the military.

## What Belongs Here

Secular mindfulness programs (MBSR, MBCT), the neuroscience and psychology of meditation, Vipassana as taught in the West, loving-kindness and compassion meditation (metta, tonglen), meditation in therapeutic contexts (dialectical behavior therapy, acceptance and commitment therapy), meditation instruction and techniques, the ethics and critiques of secular mindfulness (McMindfulness), meditation apps and technology, meditation retreats as a cultural phenomenon, the history of the mindfulness movement (Kabat-Zinn, S.N. Goenka, Thich Nhat Hanh).

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Zen or Buddhist practice as a religious tradition (belongs in Zen-Zen or Buddhism), yogic meditation as part of Hindu practice (belongs in Hinduism or Hindu Mysticism), contemplative prayer as Christian practice (belongs in Christian Mysticism), New Age practices lacking evidence or linked to alternative spirituality (belongs in New Age & Alternative Spirituality), the philosophy of consciousness and mind (belongs in Philosophy of Mind).

## Connections

- **Zen-Zen** and **Buddhism** provide the original Buddhist framework for mindfulness.
- **Hindu Mysticism & Tantra** is the source of Yogic meditative traditions.
- **Christian Mysticism** offers contemplative and centering prayer parallels.
- **Positive Psychology (16)** overlaps in the study of well-being and flourishing.
- **Mind-Body Medicine (Health)** applies meditation in clinical settings.
- **New Age & Alternative Spirituality** is adjacent but less evidence-based in orientation.
