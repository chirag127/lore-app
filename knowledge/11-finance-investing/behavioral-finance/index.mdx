# Behavioral Finance

## Overview

Behavioral finance challenges the traditional assumption of rational
investors. It studies how cognitive biases (overconfidence, loss aversion,
confirmation bias, anchoring, herding) systematically affect financial
decisions and create market anomalies. This subcategory covers the major
cognitive biases in investor decision-making and the study of market
psychology and crowd behavior — explaining bubbles, crashes, and the
persistent underperformance of active investors.
