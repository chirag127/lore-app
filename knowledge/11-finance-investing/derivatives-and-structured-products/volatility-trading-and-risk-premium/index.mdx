# Volatility Trading and Risk Premium

## Why This Exists

Volatility is not merely a risk metric to be managed — it is an investable asset class in its own right. The volatility risk premium — the systematic tendency of implied volatility to exceed realized volatility — represents one of the most robust and economically significant anomalies in financial markets. It reflects the price that hedgers are willing to pay for protection against tail events and the compensation that sellers of volatility demand for bearing crash risk. Understanding and harvesting this premium is a core strategy for many hedge funds and institutional investors.

The premise of volatility trading is straightforward: implied volatility on options is typically higher than subsequent realized volatility, meaning that selling options (being short volatility) generates positive expected returns, albeit with significant tail risk. The VIX index, computed from S&P 500 option prices, has become the benchmark for U.S. equity volatility and itself trades through futures and options. Variance swaps and volatility swaps allow direct exposure to realized volatility without the path-dependence of standard options positions. Volatility is also cross-sectional — some stocks are structurally more volatile than others — and term-structured, with different volatility regimes across maturities. Volatility risk premium exists across asset classes, including equities, currencies, commodities, and interest rates.

## Essential Books

- Emanuel Derman, *The Volatility Smile* — A thorough treatment of volatility modeling by a pioneering quant
- Sheldon Natenberg, *Option Volatility and Pricing* — The practitioner classic, with vol trading strategies
- Nassim Nicholas Taleb, *Dynamic Hedging* — Advanced volatility trading and risk management
- Colin Bennett, *Trading Volatility: Correlation, Term Structure, and Skew* — Practical variance swap and vol trading
- Alan L. Lewis, *Option Valuation Under Stochastic Volatility* — The mathematics behind vol modeling
- Jim Gatheral, *The Volatility Surface: A Practitioner's Guide* — The definitive work on volatility surface modeling
- Peter Carr & Liuren Wu, *Stochastic Skew in Currency Options* — Vol trading in FX markets
- Paul Wilmott, *Paul Wilmott Introduces Quantitative Finance* — Volatility in the broader quant finance context
