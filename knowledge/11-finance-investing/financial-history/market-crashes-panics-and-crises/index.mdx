# Market Crashes, Panics & Crises

## Overview

Financial crises are recurrent features of market economies. This subcategory
examines the greatest market crashes and panics: tulip mania, the South Sea
Bubble, the Great Depression, the 1987 crash, the Japanese asset price
bubble, the Asian financial crisis, the dot-com bubble, and the 2008 global
financial crisis. Each crisis reveals patterns of human behavior, regulatory
failure, and systemic risk.
