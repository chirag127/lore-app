# Yield Curve, Duration, and Interest Rate Risk

## Why This Exists

Interest rates are the most important systematic risk factor for fixed-income investors. A 1% change in yields can produce double-digit percentage changes in the price of a long-term bond — and the direction of rate changes is famously difficult to predict. The analytical tools of duration and convexity, combined with yield curve analysis, provide bond investors with a rigorous framework for measuring and managing this risk exposure. Without these tools, fixed-income investing would be a guessing game.

The yield curve — the relationship between bond yields and maturities — encapsulates the market's expectations for future short-term interest rates and the term premium demanded for bearing duration risk. Its shape (upward-sloping, flat, inverted, humped) conveys information about monetary policy expectations, economic growth, and inflation. Duration is a first-order approximation of a bond's price sensitivity to yield changes, while convexity captures the second-order effect. Key rate duration and principal component analysis decompose interest rate risk across the maturity spectrum. Portfolio-level risk management involves matching the duration of assets to liabilities (immunization), constructing bond ladders, and hedging with interest rate futures, swaps, and options.

## What Belongs Here

Yield curve construction and interpretation, term structure theories (expectations hypothesis, liquidity preference theory, market segmentation theory), duration and convexity calculations and applications, key rate duration, portfolio immunization and dedication strategies, interest rate hedging with derivatives, and the empirical behavior of interest rates across economic cycles.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Credit spread analysis (belongs in Corporate Bonds and Credit Analysis), sovereign credit risk, and monetary policy determination (central bank decision-making belongs in macroeconomics).

## Essential Books

- Frank J. Fabozzi, *Fixed Income Analysis* — Comprehensive coverage of duration, convexity, and yield curve analytics
- Bruce Tuckman & Angel Serrat, *Fixed Income Securities: Tools for Today's Markets* — The standard textbook for interest rate risk management
- John C. Hull, *Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives* — Chapters on interest rate derivatives and hedging
- Salomon Brothers (various authors), *Understanding the Yield Curve* — Classic practitioner guides to curve analysis
- Gerald W. Buetow, Jr. & James Fabozzi, *The Active Bond Investor* — Duration management strategies for portfolio managers
- William F. Sharpe & Lawrence G. Tint, *Liabilities: A New Approach* — Liability-driven investing and immunization
- Andrew Ang & Monika Piazzesi, *A No-Arbitrage Vector Autoregression of Term Structure Dynamics* — Academic model of yield curve dynamics
- Francis X. Diebold & Canlin Li, *Forecasting the Term Structure of Government Bond Yields* — The dynamic Nelson-Siegel model
