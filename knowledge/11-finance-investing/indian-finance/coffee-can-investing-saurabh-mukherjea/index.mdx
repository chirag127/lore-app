## Overview

*Coffee Can Investing* (2018) by Saurabh Mukherjea, Rakshit Ranjan, and
Pranab Uniyal adapts the classic "coffee can" portfolio approach —
buying great companies and holding for decades — to the Indian stock
market. The name comes from a 1980s story of a man who put stock
certificates in a coffee can and forgot about them for decades,
emerging vastly wealthy.

The book provides a systematic, seven-filter stock screen that
identifies high-quality Indian companies, then argues for holding them
for 10-20+ years while ignoring all macro noise.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Quality first.** The seven-filter screen eliminates 99% of listed
   stocks, leaving only the highest-quality companies.

2. **ROCE is king.** Return on Capital Employed above 15% for 10+
   consecutive years is the strongest signal of competitive advantage.

3. **Promoter holding matters.** Founders/families holding 50-60%+
   shares indicates alignment with minority shareholders.

4. **Ignore macro noise.** GDP growth, interest rates, elections,
   budgets — none of it matters for a coffee can portfolio.

5. **Let winners run.** The biggest mistake is selling a quality
   company because it seems "expensive" or because you want to book
   profits.

6. **Small STIRs kill returns.** Stop-losses and short-term trading
   destroy the compounding that makes coffee can investing work.

7. **Dividends compound.** Reinvesting dividends from quality companies
   dramatically accelerates wealth creation.

8. **15-20 stocks is enough.** A concentrated portfolio of high-quality
   names provides sufficient diversification.

9. **India is fertile ground.** India's demographic dividend and
   economic growth create a favorable environment for long-term equity
   investing.

10. **Simplicity beats complexity.** A simple, disciplined system
    applied consistently beats complex analysis and frequent trading.

## Why This Book Matters

*Coffee Can Investing* is the most practical, systematic guide to
long-term equity investing in the Indian market. It bridges the gap
between Western value investing classics (Graham, Buffett, Fisher) and
the Indian investor's reality.

The book's influence comes from its simplicity: seven clear filters,
15-20 stock portfolios, and a decades-long horizon. This is accessible
to any investor who can read a balance sheet.

## Final Verdict

*Coffee Can Investing* is an excellent practical guide for Indian
investors. The seven-filter screen is clear, actionable, and
well-researched. The case studies demonstrate the approach with real
Indian companies. The emphasis on patience and ignoring noise is
correct.

The book's limitations: it is entirely India-focused, the back-tested
results may not repeat, and the "seven filters" eliminate so many
stocks that building a diversified portfolio is challenging.

**Rating: 8/10** — The best systematic investing guide for the Indian
market. Essential for Indian long-term investors.
