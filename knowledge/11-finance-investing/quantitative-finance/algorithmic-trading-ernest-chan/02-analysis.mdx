## Strengths

**Practitioner perspective.** Chan has actually traded the strategies
he describes. The book avoids academic abstraction and focuses on
what works in practice. Transaction cost analysis and execution
mechanics get the attention they deserve.

**Reproducible code.** Every strategy comes with complete MATLAB
code. Readers can test the strategies immediately and modify them
for their own use. This makes the book a working reference, not just
a theoretical discussion.

**Framework orientation.** Rather than promoting a single approach,
Chan gives readers a systematic framework for developing and
evaluating any strategy. The backtesting methodology chapter should
be required reading for anyone building trading systems.

## Weaknesses

**MATLAB dependency.** MATLAB is expensive and less popular in the
quant community than Python. The code examples are not directly
usable for readers who prefer Python or R. Chan has since published
Python-focused books.

**Limited strategy universe.** Two strategy families — mean
reversion and momentum — cover a limited space. Chan does not discuss
machine learning strategies, option strategies, or event-driven
approaches in meaningful depth.

**Retail focus.** The book is geared toward individual traders.
Institutional concepts like portfolio optimization, beta hedging,
and factor models are not covered.

## Criticism

**Academic reviewers** note that Chan's backtesting methodology,
while practical, lacks the statistical rigor of academic research.
His sample sizes are small, and his significance testing is informal.

**Practitioners** generally praise the book's practicality but note
that the strategies are too simple to sustain profitability in
competitive markets. The mean reversion strategies, in particular,
have been widely implemented and may have degraded.

## Impact

The book has become a standard reference for retail algorithmic
traders. Chan's emphasis on transaction costs and execution quality
influenced a generation of independent quants. The MATLAB code
continues to be used as a starting point for strategy development.
