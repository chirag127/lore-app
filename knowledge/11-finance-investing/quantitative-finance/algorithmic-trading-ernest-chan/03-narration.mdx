Ernest Chan writes in a direct, no-nonsense style. Unlike Michael
Lewis, Chan is not a storyteller — he is an engineer and former
physicist. The prose is clear and functional, designed to convey
information efficiently.

The book is structured like a textbook but with a practical bent.
Each chapter begins with a concept, moves through theory, and ends
with concrete implementation. The MATLAB code is interleaved with
the exposition rather than relegated to appendices.

Chan's voice is authoritative but accessible. He acknowledges the
limitations of his approaches and warns readers about common
pitfalls. His tone is that of a patient teacher who has made every
mistake he is warning against.

The book's readability is good for its technical depth. Readers
with MATLAB experience will find it clear. Those without programming
background will struggle. The book is not designed to be read cover
to cover — it works best as a reference, with readers jumping to
chapters as needed.
