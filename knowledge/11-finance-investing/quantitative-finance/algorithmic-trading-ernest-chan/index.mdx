## Overview

*Algorithmic Trading: Winning Strategies and Their Rationale* by
Ernest P. Chan is a practical guide for designing and implementing
quantitative trading strategies. Chan provides a systematic framework
for strategy development, from idea generation through backtesting
to live implementation, using MATLAB throughout.

The book covers two main strategy families — mean reversion and
momentum — and provides concrete rules, code, and performance metrics
for each. Chan also addresses risk management, transaction cost
analysis, and the critical question of how to combine strategies
into a diversified portfolio.

## Executive Summary

Chan's core thesis is that successful algorithmic trading requires
a scientific approach. Strategies must be testable, their assumptions
must be understood, and their performance must be evaluated with
realistic costs. The book provides a complete toolkit: strategy
blueprints, MATLAB code, performance metrics, and risk management
techniques.
