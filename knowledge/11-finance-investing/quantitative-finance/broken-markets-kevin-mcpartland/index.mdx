## Overview

*Broken Markets* examines the transformation of U.S. equity market
structure from the perspective of an industry insider. Kevin
McPartland argues that technological innovation has outpaced
regulation, creating a market that serves insiders at the expense
of ordinary investors. The book analyzes specific market structure
issues — fragmentation, dark pools, maker-taker pricing, and
predatory HFT — and proposes regulatory solutions.

McPartland writes from the unique perspective of someone who has
worked both in the industry (BlackRock) and as a market structure
analyst (Coalition Greenwich). He combines technical knowledge of
market mechanics with a clear-eyed view of what needs to change.

## Executive Summary

McPartland's central argument is that the stock market is not broken
in the sense of being unable to function. It is broken in the sense
of being unfair. The fragmentation of orders across dozens of
exchanges and dark pools, combined with the speed advantages of HFT
firms, has created a system where insiders profit at the expense of
long-term investors. The solution is not to ban technology but to
redesign market rules for a technological age.
