## Overview

*Dark Pools* is the definitive history of the electronic trading
revolution. Scott Patterson traces the story from the early vision
of a democratic, technology-driven stock market to the fragmented,
machine-dominated market of today. The book centers on Josh Levine,
the brilliant programmer who created Island, the first successful
electronic communication network (ECN), and watched as his creation
was transformed by high-frequency traders.

Patterson weaves together multiple narratives: the idealists who
wanted to break the NYSE's monopoly, the HFT firms that exploited
the new technology, and the regulators struggling to keep pace. The
result is a sweeping history of the most dramatic transformation in
financial markets since the Great Depression.

## Executive Summary

The book's central tragedy is that the technology built to
democratize markets was repurposed to create a two-tiered system.
Josh Levine's Island ECN gave every trader equal access to the
market. But the speed advantages of HFT firms created a new elite.
Dark pools — private trading venues that hide orders from public
view — were supposed to protect large traders from predatory HFTs
but instead created opaque markets where the rules favor insiders.
