## Overview

*Flash Boys: A Wall Street Revolt* is Michael Lewis's 2014 exposé of
high-frequency trading (HFT) in U.S. equity markets. The book follows
Brad Katsuyama, a former Royal Bank of Canada trader, who discovered
that the stock market was systematically rigged against ordinary
investors. HFT firms used speed advantages — co-location, faster
fiber-optic cables, and complex trading algorithms — to see orders
before they could be executed and trade ahead of them.

Lewis tells the story through a cast of characters — Katsuyama and his
team at IEX (the Investors Exchange), a Canadian engineer named Ronan
Ryan, and a whistleblower named Sergey Aleynikov. The book sparked an
FBI investigation, multiple congressional hearings, and a national
debate about the fairness of modern financial markets.

## Executive Summary

The U.S. stock market had become a two-tiered system: insiders with
speed advantages could front-run the orders of ordinary investors with
impunity. Katsuyama's team built IEX, an exchange that used a 38-mile
coil of fiber-optic cable to deliberately slow down trading and
eliminate the speed advantage. The book is both a thriller about
financial innovation and a moral argument about market fairness.
