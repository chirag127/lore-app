Irene Aldridge writes in the style of a technical consultant —
clear, organized, and practical. The prose is straightforward and
avoids unnecessary jargon. Technical concepts are explained with a
minimum of mathematics.

The book is structured as a survey. Each chapter covers a distinct
topic with a consistent format: definition, explanation, examples,
and implications. Bullet points and tables organize information
efficiently.

Aldridge's voice is authoritative and experienced. She writes as
someone who has built HFT systems and understands the operational
realities. The technology chapters convey genuine expertise about
hardware and networking decisions.

The writing is accessible for its technical content. Readers with
basic finance knowledge will understand the strategy chapters.
The technology chapters require more specialized knowledge.

Overall, the book is well-written for its target audience. It is
not as elegant as Michael Lewis or as rigorous as O'Hara, but it
effectively serves its purpose as a practical guide to HFT.
