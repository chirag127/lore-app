Maureen O'Hara writes in the formal style of academic economics.
The prose is precise, technical, and mathematical. Definitions are
exact, assumptions are stated explicitly, and derivations are
presented step by step.

The book follows the structure of a graduate textbook. Each chapter
presents a model: assumptions, equilibrium, comparative statics,
and empirical implications. O'Hara provides verbal intuition
alongside the mathematics, helping readers understand what the
models mean.

The writing is clear by academic standards but dense for
practitioners. O'Hara assumes readers are comfortable with
stochastic calculus, game theory, and optimization. The verbal
intuitions are helpful but subordinate to the formal analysis.

The book's narrative arc traces the evolution of the field from
simple inventory models through sophisticated information and
strategic trader models. This historical progression helps readers
understand why the models developed as they did.

Overall, the writing achieves its academic purpose. It is not
accessible to general readers, but it was not intended to be. For
its target audience of PhD students and researchers, the
exposition is excellent.
