## Overview

*Market Microstructure Theory* is the first comprehensive textbook
on the theoretical foundations of market microstructure. Maureen
O'Hara surveys the major models that explain how prices are formed
in financial markets under different trading rules and information
conditions.

The book covers three generations of models: inventory-based models
(how market makers manage risk), information-based models (how
private information affects prices), and strategic trader models
(how informed traders optimize their trading behavior). It remains
the standard graduate-level reference for microstructure theory.

## Executive Summary

O'Hara's central contribution is to show that market structure
matters. The classical view of frictionless markets with efficient
prices is a useful abstraction but misses how the mechanics of
trading — order types, trading rules, dealer behavior — affect
prices. Microstructure theory fills this gap, explaining how the
rules of the game determine the outcomes.
