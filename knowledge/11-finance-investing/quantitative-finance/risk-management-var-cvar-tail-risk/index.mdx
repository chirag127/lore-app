# Risk Management — VaR, CVaR & Tail Risk

## Overview

Quantitative risk management uses statistical methods to measure and manage
financial risk. Value-at-Risk (VaR) and Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR /
Expected Shortfall) are the most widely used risk measures. This subcategory
also covers portfolio risk decomposition, stress testing, scenario analysis,
extreme value theory for tail risk, and the limitations of quantitative risk
models exposed during financial crises.
