## Overview

*The Big Short* tells the story of the 2008 financial crisis through
the eyes of the handful of investors who saw it coming and bet
against the housing market. Michael Lewis profiles four groups of
misfits: Steve Eisman, a cynical hedge fund manager; Michael Burry,
an autistic former doctor; Greg Lippmann, a Deutsche Bank trader; and
Jamie Mai and Charlie Ledley, two Berkeley dropouts running Cornell
Capital.

Each group independently concluded that the housing market was a
fraud and bought credit default swaps — insurance policies — on
subprime mortgage bonds. When the market collapsed, they made
billions. Lewis uses their stories to explain the complex financial
engineering — CDOs, synthetic CDOs, and credit default swaps — that
transformed bad loans into a global catastrophe.

## Executive Summary

The book argues that the crisis was not an accident but a predictable
consequence of incentives. Wall Street banks originated fraudulent
loans, packaged them into bonds, got AAA ratings from conflicted
rating agencies, and sold them to pension funds around the world.
The few people who tried to warn about the collapse were ignored,
ridiculed, or fired. Lewis turns the crisis into a story about human
nature — greed, denial, and the courage to be wrong when everyone
else is wrong.
