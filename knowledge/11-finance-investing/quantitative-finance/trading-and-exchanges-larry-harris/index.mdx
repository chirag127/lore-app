## Overview

*Trading and Exchanges* is the definitive textbook on market
microstructure for practitioners. Larry Harris, former Chief
Economist of the SEC, explains how markets work at the mechanical
level — how orders interact, how prices are formed, how liquidity
is provided, and how regulation shapes outcomes.

The book covers the entire trading ecosystem: the people who trade,
the instruments they trade, the marketplaces where they trade, and
the rules that govern trading. Harris writes for practitioners but
with academic rigor. The book is comprehensive, covering everything
from order types to market manipulation.

## Executive Summary

Harris's central insight is that trading is a zero-sum game minus
transaction costs. Most traders lose money because they compete
against better-informed or faster traders. The key to successful
trading is understanding your competitive advantage. The book
provides the conceptual tools to analyze any market and any trading
strategy.
