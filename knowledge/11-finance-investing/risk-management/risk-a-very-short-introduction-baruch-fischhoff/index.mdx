_Risk: A Very Short Introduction_ is a compact yet remarkably comprehensive survey of how risk is understood across disciplines. Baruch Fischhoff, a pioneer in risk perception research, and John Kadvany, a policy analyst, synthesize insights from decision theory, psychology, statistics, and philosophy into a practical framework for thinking about uncertainty. The book is part of Oxford's Very Short Introductions series, which aims to provide expert but accessible overviews of complex subjects.

<BookHeader />

The book addresses three fundamental questions: What is risk? How should we measure it? And how can we make better decisions under uncertainty? Fischhoff and Kadvany argue that risk is not a single objective quantity but a multidimensional concept that depends on measurement choices, cultural values, and cognitive processes. The book moves from the theoretical foundations of risk analysis to practical applications in health, environment, finance, and public policy.
