_Freakonomics: A Rogue Economist Explores the Hidden Side of Everything_ (2005) by Steven D. Levitt and Stephen J. Dubner is the book that made economics cool. It reframes economics not as the study of money but as the study of incentives — how people get what they want, especially when others want the same thing. Through a series of unexpected questions and data-driven answers, Levitt and Dubner argue that conventional wisdom is almost always wrong, and that the hidden side of everything is worth exploring.
