# Behavioral Economics

## Overview

Behavioral economics relaxes the traditional economic assumption of perfect
rationality. It draws on psychology to build more realistic models of how
people actually make choices — incorporating cognitive biases, limited
willpower, social preferences, and bounded rationality. This subcategory
covers heuristics and biases in economic behavior (prospect theory, framing,
mental accounting) and the experimental methods used to study economic
decision-making.
