# Development Economics

## Overview

Development economics studies the economic transformation of low-income
countries. It asks why some countries grow rich while others remain poor,
and what policies can accelerate development. This subcategory covers
economic growth theory (Solow model, endogenous growth, structural
transformation), the role of institutions (property rights, governance,
rule of law), and the microeconomics of poverty (health, education, credit,
insurance in developing contexts).
