# Externalities & Market Failure

## Overview

Externalities occur when the actions of one party impose costs or confer
benefits on others who are not part of the transaction (pollution is the
classic example). Market failure describes situations where the free market
does not produce an efficient outcome. This subcategory covers the theory
of externalities, public goods, the tragedy of the commons, and the policy
responses: Pigouvian taxes, subsidies, and regulation.
