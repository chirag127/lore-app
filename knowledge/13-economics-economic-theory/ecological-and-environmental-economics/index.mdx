# Ecological & Environmental Economics

## Overview

Environmental economics applies economic reasoning to environmental
problems. It asks how to efficiently allocate scarce environmental
resources and design policies to correct market failures like pollution.
This subcategory covers externalities and market failure (the fundamental
concept that private costs can diverge from social costs), carbon markets
and environmental pricing (cap-and-trade, carbon taxes, offsets), and
sustainable development economics (the challenge of meeting present needs
without compromising future generations).
