# Trade Theory

## Overview

Trade theory explains the patterns and benefits of international exchange.
It covers the classical theories (Ricardian comparative advantage,
Heckscher-Ohlin factor proportions), the new trade theory (increasing
returns, product differentiation, Krugman's model), and the firm-level
heterogeneity approach (Melitz model). Understanding these theories is
essential for analyzing trade policy and globalization.
