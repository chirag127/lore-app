# Microeconomics

## Overview

Microeconomics analyzes the behavior of individual economic agents —
consumers, firms, workers — and how they interact in markets. It provides
the tools for understanding prices, production, competition, and welfare.
This subcategory covers price theory and market structures (perfect
competition, monopoly, oligopoly, monopolistic competition) and the
foundations of supply and demand, elasticity, consumer choice, and
production theory.
