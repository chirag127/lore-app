# Political Economy

## Overview

Political economy examines how political forces — institutions, interests,
ideology — shape economic policy and outcomes, and conversely how economic
conditions affect political systems. This subcategory covers the role of
political institutions in economic development, public choice theory
(politicians and bureaucrats as self-interested actors), and the
relationship between democracy, property rights, and prosperity.
