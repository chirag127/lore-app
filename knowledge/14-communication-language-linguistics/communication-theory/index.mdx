# Communication Theory

## Overview

Communication theory provides the conceptual foundations for understanding how messages are created, transmitted, received, and interpreted. It spans Shannon and Weaver's mathematical model of information transmission, the Palo Alto school's interactional view, McLuhan's media ecology, and contemporary theories of networked communication, diffusion of innovations, and computer-mediated communication. This subcategory grounds the more applied areas of rhetoric, public speaking, and writing.
