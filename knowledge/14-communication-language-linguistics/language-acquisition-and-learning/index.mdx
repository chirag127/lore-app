# Language Acquisition & Learning

## Overview

Language acquisition studies how children acquire their native language without explicit instruction — a remarkable cognitive achievement that has been central to debates about innate knowledge (Chomsky's universal grammar, usage-based approaches). Second language acquisition (SLA) examines how learners acquire additional languages later in life, including the role of instruction, individual differences, and cross-linguistic influence. Language learning methodologies apply SLA research to classroom teaching.
