# Linguistics

## Overview

Linguistics is the systematic study of human language as a cognitive and social phenomenon. Its core subfields are phonetics (speech sounds), phonology (sound systems), morphology (word structure), syntax (sentence structure), semantics (meaning), and pragmatics (meaning in context). Sociolinguistics examines language variation across social groups and contexts. Historical linguistics traces language change and relationships. Cognitive linguistics studies the mental processes underlying language use.
