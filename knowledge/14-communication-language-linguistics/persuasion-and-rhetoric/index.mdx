# Persuasion & Rhetoric

## Overview

Rhetoric is the art of persuasive communication, originating in ancient Greece as the study of how speakers move audiences to belief or action. Classical rhetoric identified three appeals (ethos, pathos, logos) and five canons (invention, arrangement, style, memory, delivery). Modern persuasion theory draws on social psychology (Cialdini's principles, elaboration likelihood model) and communication studies to understand how attitudes are changed through messages. This subcategory also covers propaganda and strategic communication.
