# Public Speaking & Presentation

## Overview

Public speaking translates rhetorical theory into practice. This subcategory covers speech writing (structuring arguments, crafting openings and closings, using stories and examples), delivery (voice, gesture, eye contact, stage presence), audience analysis (adapting content and style to different listeners), and presentation design (slides, visuals, handouts). It draws on classical rhetoric, theater, and communication research to help speakers communicate with clarity and impact.
