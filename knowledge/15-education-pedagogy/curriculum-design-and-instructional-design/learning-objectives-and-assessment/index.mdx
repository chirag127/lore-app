# Learning Objectives and Assessment

## Why This Exists

If education is purposeful, we must be able to specify what learners should know and be able to do — and to determine whether they have in fact learned it. Learning objectives and assessment are the mechanisms through which educational intentions are made explicit and their achievement verified. Far from being a bureaucratic imposition, careful attention to objectives and assessment is a professional responsibility: it ensures alignment between what is taught, what is tested, and what students actually learn.

The modern approach to learning objectives originated with Benjamin Bloom's *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives* (1956), which classified cognitive outcomes from simple recall to evaluation. Lorin Anderson and David Krathwohl revised the taxonomy in 2001, adding a knowledge dimension and renaming the levels. Robert Mager popularized the practice of writing behavioral objectives in precise, measurable terms. Assessment design has evolved from traditional norm-referenced testing to criterion-referenced assessment, performance-based assessment, authentic assessment, portfolio assessment, and competency-based evaluation. Grant Wiggins and Jay McTighe's backward design framework — starting with desired results, then assessment evidence, then learning experiences — has become widely influential.

## What Belongs Here

Taxonomies of learning outcomes, methods for writing clear objectives, frameworks for aligning objectives with instruction and assessment, design principles for formative and summative assessment, research on assessment validity and reliability, performance assessment and authentic assessment, grading philosophies and practices, portfolio assessment, and the relationship between assessment and learning motivation.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Statistical psychometrics and test theory (belongs in quantitative methods), curriculum content decisions (belongs in Curriculum Theory), classroom management, and general instructional strategies not tied to assessment of specific objectives.

## Essential Books

- Benjamin S. Bloom et al., *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives: The Classification of Educational Goals* — The classic cognitive taxonomy
- Lorin W. Anderson & David R. Krathwohl, *A Taxonomy for Learning, Teaching, and Assessing* — The revised Bloom's taxonomy
- Grant Wiggins & Jay McTighe, *Understanding by Design* — Backward design from desired results through assessment
- Robert J. Marzano, *Designing and Assessing Educational Objectives* — A comprehensive framework aligned with the revised taxonomy
- Linda Suskie, *Assessing Student Learning: A Common Sense Guide* — Practical approaches to assessment in higher education
- Richard J. Stiggins, *Student-Involved Assessment FOR Learning* — Formative assessment and student involvement
- Dylan Wiliam, *Embedded Formative Assessment* — The research and practice of in-class assessment for learning
- Thomas R. Guskey, *Evaluating Professional Development* — Assessment at the teacher and program level
