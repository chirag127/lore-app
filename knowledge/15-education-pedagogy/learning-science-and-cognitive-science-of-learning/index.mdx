# Learning Science & Cognitive Science of Learning

## Overview

Learning science applies cognitive psychology and neuroscience to understand how learning works. It covers the mechanisms of memory (encoding, consolidation, retrieval), attention and focus, metacognition (thinking about one's own thinking), and the conditions that promote durable learning. Key findings include the superiority of retrieval practice over rereading, the benefits of spaced and interleaved practice, and the importance of desirable difficulties. This research provides the evidence base for effective teaching and instructional design.
