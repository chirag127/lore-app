# Critical Pedagogy

## Why This Exists

Education is never politically neutral, and critical pedagogy is the tradition that takes this claim most seriously. It asks not only what should be taught but whose knowledge is valued, whose history is told, whose interests are served by the curriculum, and how schools function as sites of cultural reproduction and resistance. Inspired by Paulo Freire's *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* and drawing on the Frankfurt School of critical theory, this tradition insists that the purpose of education is not merely to transmit skills but to develop critical consciousness (conscientization) that enables learners to recognize, analyze, and act against oppressive social structures.

Critical pedagogy gained prominence through Freire's literacy work with impoverished Brazilian peasants, expanded through Henry Giroux's analyses of schooling as a form of cultural politics, bell hooks's integration of feminist and anti-racist perspectives, and Michael Apple's examination of the relationships between education and economic power. It challenges the banking model of education (students as passive receptacles to be filled with knowledge) and advocates for problem-posing education, dialogue, and praxis — the continuous cycle of reflection and action. In the twenty-first century, it has been extended to address neoliberalism, globalization, digital media, and climate justice.

## What Belongs Here

Freire's pedagogy and its applications, neo-Marxist analyses of educational systems, feminist pedagogy, anti-racist and decolonial approaches to education, critical analysis of curriculum and textbooks, studies of teacher agency and resistance, examinations of how educational policy reflects class and power structures, and pedagogical strategies for developing critical consciousness.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

Generic progressive education without political analysis, classroom management techniques unconnected to social critique, policy analysis that does not address power and ideology, and versions of multicultural education that celebrate diversity without examining structural inequality.

## Essential Books

- Paulo Freire, *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* — The foundational text of critical pedagogy
- Henry A. Giroux, *Theory and Resistance in Education* — Extends critical pedagogy to American schooling
- bell hooks, *Teaching to Transgress* — Feminist and anti-racist critical pedagogy in practice
- Michael W. Apple, *Ideology and Curriculum* — How school knowledge reflects class interests
- Peter McLaren, *Life in Schools* — Critical pedagogy applied to urban education
- Ira Shor, *Empowering Education* — Practical strategies for critical teaching
- Paulo Freire & Donaldo Macedo, *Literacy: Reading the Word and the World* — Literacy as political action
- Antonia Darder, *Reinventing Paulo Freire* — Contemporary applications of Freirean pedagogy

## Key Concepts

Conscientization, banking model vs. problem-posing education, praxis, dialogue, oppression, liberation, cultural capital, hidden curriculum, voice, empowerment, hegemony, resistance, transformative intellectual, the political nature of literacy.
