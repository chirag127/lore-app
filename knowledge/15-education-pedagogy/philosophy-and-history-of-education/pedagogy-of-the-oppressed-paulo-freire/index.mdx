Paulo Freire's *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* is the foundational text of critical pedagogy, written during his political exile following the 1964 Brazilian military coup. Based on his experience teaching literacy to impoverished Brazilian peasants, the book argues that education is never politically neutral — it either functions as an instrument of oppression or becomes a practice of freedom. Freire's lasting contribution is the pairing of the "banking model" of education (students as passive receptacles) with its antidote: problem-posing pedagogy rooted in dialogue, critical consciousness (conscientization), and praxis — the cycle of reflection and action through which the oppressed transform their world.
