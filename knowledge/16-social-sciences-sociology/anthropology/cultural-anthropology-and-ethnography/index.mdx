# Cultural Anthropology & Ethnography

## Overview

Cultural anthropology studies the diversity of human cultures — their kinship systems, economic practices, religious beliefs, political organizations, and symbolic worlds. Its signature method is ethnography: long-term immersive fieldwork in which the anthropologist learns the language, participates in daily life, and seeks to understand the world from the native's point of view. Foundational figures include Franz Boas (historical particularism), Bronislaw Malinowski (functionalism), and Clifford Geertz (interpretive anthropology).
