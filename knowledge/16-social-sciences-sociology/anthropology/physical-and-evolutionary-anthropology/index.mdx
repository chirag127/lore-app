# Physical & Evolutionary Anthropology

## Overview

Physical anthropology examines humans as biological organisms. It covers human evolution (fossil record, hominin lineages), primatology (the study of non-human primates as models for understanding human evolution), human variation (genetics, adaptation to different environments), and osteology (the study of the skeleton for archaeological and forensic purposes). This subfield bridges anthropology with biology, paleontology, and genetics.
