# Social Anthropology & Kinship

## Overview

Social anthropology, particularly associated with British anthropology (Radcliffe-Brown, Evans-Pritchard, Leach), focuses on social structure — the patterned relationships that constitute society. Kinship is central: the study of descent, marriage, family structures, and the terminology systems that different cultures use to classify relatives. This subfield also examines political organization in non-state societies, legal anthropology, and the anthropology of religion and ritual.
