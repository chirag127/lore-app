description: "The study of interconnectedness, global inequality, and transnational social structures."
---

Globalization theory examines the intensification of worldwide social relations linking distant localities. World-systems theory (Wallerstein) analyzes the capitalist world economy as a core-periphery structure. Covers cultural homogenization, global civil society, transnational social movements, and the political economy of global integration and fragmentation.
