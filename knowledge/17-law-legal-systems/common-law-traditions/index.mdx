The common law tradition develops through judicial decisions rather than legislative codes. This subcategory covers the core doctrinal areas that define the common law: the law of obligations (contract and tort), property rights, criminal responsibility, and the procedural frameworks within which these disputes are adjudicated.

### Sub-subcategories

- **contract-law-tort-law-and-property-law** — Formation, breach, remedies; negligence, strict liability; estates, trusts, land law
- **criminal-law-and-criminal-procedure** — Elements of crimes, defenses, criminal procedure, evidence
- **civil-procedure-and-litigation** — Jurisdiction, pleadings, discovery, trial, appeals, class actions
