Corporate and commercial law governs business entities, their formation, governance, and dissolution. It also covers the regulation of markets — intellectual property rights that incentivize innovation, competition law that prevents monopolistic abuse, and securities regulation that protects investors in capital markets.

### Sub-subcategories

- **company-law-partnerships-and-llp-structures** — Corporate personality, directors' duties, shareholder rights, mergers and acquisitions
- **intellectual-property-law** — Patents, copyright, trademarks, trade secrets, IP licensing and enforcement
- **competition-law-and-antitrust** — Monopolization, cartels, mergers, abuse of dominance, market definition
- **securities-regulation-and-capital-markets-law** — Public offerings, insider trading, disclosure obligations, market manipulation
