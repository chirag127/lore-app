The foundations of law explore the nature of law itself — what it is, why it binds us, and how it should be interpreted. Jurisprudence examines theories of law from natural law to legal positivism to legal realism. Constitutional law studies the distribution and limitation of governmental power. Human rights law protects fundamental freedoms against state encroachment.

### Sub-subcategories

- **jurisprudence-and-philosophy-of-law** — Natural law, legal positivism, Dworkin's interpretivism, American legal realism
- **legal-reasoning-and-statutory-interpretation** — Canons of construction, precedent, analogical reasoning, purposive vs textual interpretation
- **constitutional-principles-and-human-rights** — Separation of powers, judicial review, fundamental rights, constitutional interpretation
