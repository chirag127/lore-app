International law governs the relations between sovereign states and other actors in the international system. It spans public international law (treaties, custom, jurisdiction), international trade law (WTO, trade agreements), and international humanitarian and human rights law (laws of war, accountability for atrocities).

### Sub-subcategories

- **public-international-law-and-treaty-law** — Sources of international law, statehood, jurisdiction, diplomatic immunity, law of the sea
- **international-trade-law-and-wto** — GATT, trade in services, TRIPS, dispute settlement, trade remedies
- **international-human-rights-and-humanitarian-law** — Geneva Conventions, ICC, human rights treaties, transitional justice
