Contemporary history covers the period after 1945, beginning with the Cold War and extending to the present. It examines the superpower rivalry between the US and Soviet Union, the dismantling of European empires, the revolution in information technology, and the integration of the global economy.

### Sub-subcategories

- **cold-war-and-superpower-competition** — Nuclear arms race, proxy wars, détente, collapse of the Soviet Union
- **decolonization-and-post-colonial-struggles** — Independence movements, nation-building, post-colonial challenges, North-South relations
- **digital-revolution-and-the-information-age** — Origins of the internet, personal computing, social media, AI
- **globalization-and-its-historical-origins** — Trade liberalization, financial integration, cultural exchange, backlash
