Economic history studies how material conditions, production, and exchange have evolved over time. Financial history focuses on the institutions of credit, banking, and investment — and the crises that periodically shake them. Together they explain the historical origins of modern capitalism and its periodic instabilities.

### Sub-subcategories

- **history-of-trade-commerce-and-global-markets** — Silk Road, mercantilism, free trade, trade blocs, global supply chains
- **history-of-banking-money-and-credit** — Origins of banking, gold standard, central banking, digital currencies
- **history-of-financial-crises-and-market-manias** — Tulip mania, South Sea Bubble, 1929, 2008, patterns of boom and bust
