Jack Weatherford's *Genghis Khan and the Making of the Modern World* (2004) is a landmark work of revisionist history that rescues the Mongol conqueror from centuries of Western demonization. Written by an anthropologist rather than a traditional historian, the book argues that Genghis Khan was not a barbarian destroyer of civilization but an architect of the modern world — a visionary leader who abolished torture, granted religious freedom, united the Silk Road, and sparked the technological and cultural exchange that made the Renaissance possible. The spent weeks on the *New York Times* bestseller list and reshaped popular understanding of Mongol history for a generation of readers.
