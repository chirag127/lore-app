Modern world history covers the period from roughly 1750 to 1945 — the age of empires, industrialization, and the catastrophic wars that reshaped the global order. It examines how the Industrial Revolution transformed economies and societies, how European empires expanded and then collapsed, and how totalitarian ideologies challenged liberal democracy.

### Sub-subcategories

- **age-of-empires-and-colonial-powers** — British, French, Dutch, and other empires; scramble for Africa; colonial administration and resistance
- **industrial-revolution** — Technological innovation, urbanization, labor movements, capitalism and its critics
- **world-war-i** — Causes, trench warfare, home fronts, Treaty of Versailles, legacy
- **the-interwar-period-and-the-rise-of-totalitarianism** — Great Depression, fascism, Nazism, Stalinism, League of Nations
- **world-war-ii-and-the-holocaust** — Causes, theaters of war, genocide, home fronts, postwar settlement
