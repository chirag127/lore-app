## Strengths

- **Unique perspective.** Fills a genuine gap in popular history
  writing — world history from a non-Western viewpoint.
- **Accessible narrative.** Unlike academic histories, this is a
  compelling story told by a gifted writer.
- **Balanced tone.** Ansary does not demonize the West or idealize
  Islamic civilization. He presents both with nuance.
- **Relevant framing.** Provides essential context for understanding
  contemporary Middle Eastern politics and conflicts.
- **The "Middle World" concept is genuinely useful.** Offers a
  framework for thinking about geography and culture that escapes
  Western-centric labels.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Selective coverage.** The book skips significant periods and
  regions. Southeast Asian Islam, African Islam, and Islamic Spain
  receive limited attention.
- **Light on analysis.** The narrative is engaging but does not dig
  deeply into economic, social, or institutional history.
- **Occasional oversimplification.** Complex civilizational dynamics
  are sometimes reduced to a clash of narratives.
- **Controversial in parts.** Some scholars have challenged factual
  claims, particularly about early Islamic history and Zionism.
