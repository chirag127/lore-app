Regional history examines the distinct trajectories of the world's major civilizations and regions. Each region has its own historiographical traditions, periodization debates, and interpretive frameworks. Understanding regional histories is essential before attempting global synthesis.

### Sub-subcategories

- **indian-history** — Indus Valley, Vedic period, Maurya and Gupta empires, Delhi Sultanate, Mughals, British Raj, independence and partition
- **chinese-history-and-east-asian-civilizations** — Dynastic cycles, Confucian statecraft, Silk Road, Opium Wars, Republican era, Maoist period, reform era
- **european-history** — From antiquity through the EU, with emphasis on state formation, revolution, and integration
- **history-of-the-islamic-world** — Rise of Islam, caliphates, Ottoman, Safavid, Mughal empires, colonial encounters, modern Middle East
- **african-history-and-the-transatlantic-slave-trade** — Pre-colonial kingdoms, slave trade, colonization, independence movements
- **history-of-the-united-states** — Colonial period, founding, Civil War, Reconstruction, Progressive era, Cold War, contemporary America
- **latin-american-history** — Pre-Columbian civilizations, Spanish conquest, independence, state formation, dictatorships, democratization
