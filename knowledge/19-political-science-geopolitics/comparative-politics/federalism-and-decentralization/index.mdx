# Federalism & Decentralization

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Federalism addresses one of the most fundamental questions in political organization: how to distribute power across territorial units. In an era of both centralizing pressures (globalization, security threats) and decentralizing demands (regional identities, local autonomy), the design of federal systems has profound consequences for governance quality, conflict management, and democratic accountability. William Riker's *Federalism: Origin, Operation, Significance* laid the modern positive theory of federalism as a bargain between central and peripheral elites. Arend Lijphart's work on consociationalism and consensus democracy identified federalism as a key institution for managing divided societies.

The field extends beyond formal federal constitutions to encompass decentralization, devolution, and fiscal transfers in unitary systems. Scholars such as Richard Simeon, Daniel Treisman, Jenna Bednar, and Jonathan Rodden have deepened our understanding of how intergovernmental bargaining, fiscal incentives, and institutional design shape the performance of multilevel governance.

## What Belongs Here

- Intergovernmental relations and executive federalism

- The theory of territorial sovereignty per se (see Geography, Power & National Interest)

## Key Books

1. William H. Riker, *Federalism: Origin, Operation, Significance* — The foundational positive theory of federalism as a political bargain.
2. Arend Lijphart, *Patterns of Democracy* — The consociational and consensus democracy framework in which federalism plays a central role.
3. Jenna Bednar, *The Robust Federation* — How institutional mechanisms maintain federal stability over time.
4. Jonathan Rodden, *Hamilton's Paradox* — The political economy of fiscal federalism and soft budget constraints.
5. Daniel Treisman, *The Architecture of Government* — A comprehensive analysis of decentralization and its effects.
6. Alfred Stepan, *Arguing Comparative Politics* — Seminal chapters on federalism and democracy in multinational polities.
7. Richard Simeon, *State–Societal Relations in the Canadian Federal System* — Classic analysis of federal-provincial dynamics.
8. K.C. Wheare, *Federal Government* — The original modern classification of federal systems.
9. Anand Menon & Martin Schain (eds.), *Comparative Federalism* — Comparison of federal systems across continents.
10. Louise Tillin, *Remapping India* — The politics of state reorganization in India's federal system.
