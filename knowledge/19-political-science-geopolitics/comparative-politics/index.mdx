# Comparative Politics

## Overview

Comparative politics uses the method of comparison to understand how political systems work. By examining similarities and differences across countries, it identifies causal patterns in institutional design, electoral behavior, party competition, and policy outcomes. Its core questions include: why do democracies emerge and survive? How do different constitutional arrangements shape governance? What explains variation in party systems and voter behavior?
