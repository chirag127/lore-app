# Electoral Integrity & Voting Systems

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

Elections are the central mechanism of democratic accountability, but their integrity cannot be assumed. Electoral integrity — the application of international standards and norms throughout the electoral cycle — is threatened by voter suppression, gerrymandering, campaign finance abuse, electoral violence, and manipulation of election administration. The field emerged as a distinct area of study in response to widespread concerns about the quality of elections in both established and new democracies.

Pippa Norris's *Why Electoral Integrity Matters* (2014) and her Electoral Integrity Project have been foundational in developing systematic cross-national measures of electoral quality. The field draws on earlier work by scholars such as Jørgen Elklit and Palle Svensson on the concept of "free and fair" elections and on the broader democratization literature (Diamond, Linz, Przeworski). It also addresses the practical institutional design of election management bodies, voter registration systems, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Contemporary concerns include the impact of campaign disinformation, foreign interference, and the erosion of trust in electoral outcomes.

## Key Books

1. Pippa Norris, *Why Electoral Integrity Matters* — The foundational cross-national framework and evidence for electoral integrity.
2. Jørgen Elklit & Palle Svensson, "What Makes Elections Free and Fair?" — The classic conceptual analysis of electoral quality.
3. Pippa Norris, *Why Elections Fail* — Analysis of the causes and consequences of electoral malpractice.
4. Guy-Uriel E. Charles, Luis Fuentes-Rohwer & Michael S. Kang (eds.), *Race, Representation, and the American Electoral System* — Race and equality in electoral access.
5. Richard L. Hasen, *The Voting Wars* — The partisan battle over election rules in the United States.
6. Nicholas O. Stephanopoulos, "The Consequences of Consequential Gerrymandering" — Empirical analysis of partisan redistricting effects.
7. Samuel Issacharoff, Pamela S. Karlan & Richard H. Pildes, *The Law of Democracy* — The leading legal casebook on election law and democratic process.
8. Larry Diamond, "Elections Without Democracy" — Analysis of competitive authoritarianism and electoral fraud.
9. Alberto Simpser, *Why Governments and Parties Manipulate Elections* — The strategic logic of electoral manipulation.
10. Sarah Birch, *Electoral Malpractice* — Comparative analysis of electoral fraud across regime types.
