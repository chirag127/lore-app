# India in the Global Order

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

India's emergence as a major power is one of the defining geopolitical developments of the 21st century. The world's most populous country, a nuclear-armed state with the third-largest military budget, and an economy projected to be among the top three globally by 2030, India's strategic choices will profoundly shape the international order. Yet India's foreign policy tradition is distinctive: it combines a civilizational identity, a post-colonial commitment to strategic autonomy, a tradition of non-alignment, and increasingly pragmatic great-power balancing.

The study of India's global role draws on scholars such as Stephen P. Cohen (the founding figure in modern Indian strategic studies), C. Raja Mohan (Indian foreign policy and grand strategy), Sumit Ganguly (India's neighborhood policy), and Shivshankar Menon (diplomatic practice and strategic thinking). Harsh V. Pant, S. Jaishankar (India's External Affairs Minister and a former diplomat), and David M. Malone have contributed to understanding India's evolving role in global governance. The literature examines India's relationships with the United States, China, Russia, its South Asian neighbors, and the broader Indo-Pacific region.

## What Belongs Here

- India's strategic culture and identity
- Non-alignment and strategic autonomy (multi-alignment, issue-based coalitions)
- India-US relations; India-China competition and border disputes
- India's neighborhood policy (Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Afghanistan)
- Act East Policy and Southeast Asia
- India in the Indo-Pacific and QUAD
- India-Russia and India-Europe relations
- India and global governance (UN Security Council reform, G20, BRICS, SCO)
- India's nuclear doctrine and deterrence
- India's maritime strategy in the Indian Ocean
- India's economic statecraft and diaspora diplomacy

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- Domestic Indian politics or political history (see that in Area Studies)
- Indian economic development per se (see Political Economy of Developing Nations)
- Pakistan or China's foreign policy as primary subjects (see US-China Competition)
- Theories of international relations generally (see Realism, Liberalism & Constructivism)
- South Asian history or the independence movement (see that in History)

## Key Books

1. Stephen P. Cohen, *India: Emerging Power* — The essential overview of India's strategic traditions and capabilities.
2. C. Raja Mohan, *Crossing the Rubicon: The Shaping of India's New Foreign Policy* — How post-Cold War India shed its strategic restraint.
3. S. Jaishankar, *The India Way: Strategies for an Uncertain World* — A practitioner's perspective on India's foreign policy approach.
4. Sumit Ganguly (ed.), *India's Foreign Policy: Retrospect and Prospect* — Comprehensive edited volume on the major relationships and issues.
5. Shivshankar Menon, *Choices: Inside the Making of India's Foreign Policy* — Memoir and analysis by a former National Security Advisor.
6. David M. Malone, *Does the Elephant Dance?* — India's contemporary engagement with the world, with a focus on global institutions.
7. Harsh V. Pant, *Indian Foreign Policy: The Modi Era* — Analysis of the transformation under the Modi government.
8. Sunil Khilnani, Rajiv Kumar et al., *NonAlignment 2.0* — Strategic vision for Indian foreign policy in the 21st century.
9. Manjeet S. Pardesi, "India's Grand Strategy" (in various journals) — Academic analysis of gaps and consistencies in Indian strategy.
10. T.V. Paul (ed.), *India in the World Order* — India's approach to international order and its reform.

## Key Concepts

Strategic autonomy, multi-alignment, non-alignment, Act East, Look East, neighborhood first, Gujral doctrine, Indo-Pacific, QUAD, Indian Ocean Region (IOR), SAGAR (Security and Growth for All in the Region), China containment, border standoff, nuclear triad, credible minimum deterrence, diaspora diplomacy, soft power, yoga diplomacy, South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC), Bay of Bengal Initiative (BIMSTEC), India-Africa Forum

## Connections

India's global role is a case study in **Geography, Power & National Interest** (Indian Ocean centrality, Himalayan buffer), **US-China Competition** (India is a key swing state in the US-China rivalry), and **Realism, Liberalism & Constructivism** (India's behavior tests theories of rising powers). India's role in global economic governance connects to **International Organizations** (UN, G20, BRICS, SCO) and **Political Economy of Developing Nations**.
