Jihad vs. McWorld is Benjamin Barber's influential and prescient analysis of the twin forces shaping the post-Cold War world. Published in 1995, the book predicted that globalization and tribal fundamentalism would become the dominant forces of the twenty-first century, both at the expense of democratic governance and the nation-state.

<BookHeader />

Barber's central insight is that Jihad (the forces of ethnic, religious, and tribal fundamentalism) and McWorld (the forces of global capitalism and consumer culture) are not opposites but two sides of the same coin. Both are hostile to democracy — McWorld through the commodification of politics and the reduction of citizens to consumers, Jihad through the rejection of pluralism and the imposition of monolithic identity. The book gained remarkably in relevance after 9/11, as the conflict between the two forces Barber identified came to define global politics.
