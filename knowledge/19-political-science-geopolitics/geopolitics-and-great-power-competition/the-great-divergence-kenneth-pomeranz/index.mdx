The Great Divergence is a landmark work of global economic history that fundamentally challenged conventional understandings of the rise of the West. Kenneth Pomeranz argues that the most advanced regions of China and Europe were at comparable levels of development as late as 1800, and that the subsequent divergence was driven by contingent geographic and historical factors — particularly coal deposits and colonial extraction — rather than by any inherent cultural or institutional superiority.

<BookHeader />

Pomeranz's work has reshaped the field of world history and provoked intense debate among historians of Europe, China, and the global economy. The book won the American Historical Association's John H. Dunning Prize and remains essential reading for understanding the origins of global inequality.
