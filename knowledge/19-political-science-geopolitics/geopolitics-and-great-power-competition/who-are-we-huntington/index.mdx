Who Are We? is Samuel Huntington's controversial examination of American national identity at a time of perceived crisis. Published post-9/11, the book argues that the United States faces a fundamental challenge to its identity from mass immigration — particularly from Mexico — and from elites who have abandoned traditional American culture for cosmopolitan globalism.

<BookHeader />

Huntington contends that the core of American identity has always been the Anglo-Protestant culture of the founding settlers: the English language, Protestant religious values, individualism, work ethic, and a belief in the rule of law. He warns that the unprecedented scale of Latino immigration, the persistence of Spanish-language use across generations, and the celebration of multiculturalism by elites threaten to bifurcate America into two cultures. The book sparked intense debate upon publication, with critics accusing Huntington of nativism while supporters praised him for raising uncomfortable questions about national cohesion.
