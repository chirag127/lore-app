*After Hegemony: Cooperation and Discord in the World Political Economy* (1984) is the foundational text of neoliberal institutionalism. Robert O. Keohane, one of the most influential American IR scholars of the late twentieth century, wrote the book to address a pressing practical and theoretical question: will international cooperation collapse as American hegemony declines? His answer, grounded in rational choice theory and game theory, is that cooperation can persist and even flourish after hegemony, sustained by international institutions and regimes.

The book combines theoretical rigor with historical case studies. Keohane develops a functional theory of international regimes, arguing that they emerge because they serve the interests of states by reducing transaction costs, facilitating information sharing, and enabling decentralized enforcement through reciprocity. The book directly challenges realist claims that institutions are mere reflections of power and that cooperation requires a hegemon.
