# International Relations

## Overview

International relations (IR) examines how states and non-state actors interact in an anarchic international system. Its major theoretical paradigms — realism, liberalism, and constructivism — offer competing explanations for war and peace, cooperation and conflict, the role of international institutions, and the influence of ideas and norms on global politics. IR also encompasses diplomatic history, foreign policy analysis, and the study of international organizations.
