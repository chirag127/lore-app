*The Great Delusion: Liberal Dreams and International Realities* (2018) is John Mearsheimer's most comprehensive critique of American grand strategy since the end of the Cold War. Building on his earlier work in offensive realism, Mearsheimer argues that the policy of "liberal hegemony"—the effort to spread democracy, open markets, and international institutions across the world—has been a catastrophic failure. The book is organized in three parts: first, a philosophical analysis of liberalism's internal contradictions; second, a historical account of how liberal hegemony failed from the Balkans to Iraq; and third, a case for restraint as the alternative.

The book was named a Financial Times Best Book of 2018 and drew praise from realists and critics of interventionism across the political spectrum. Robert D. Kaplan called it "a systematic tour de force" that both idealists and realists need to read.
