*The Israel Lobby and U.S. Foreign Policy* began as a 2006 *London Review of Books* article that provoked an extraordinary firestorm. Expanded into a book published by Farrar, Straus and Giroux in 2007, it became a New York Times bestseller and one of the most controversial works of political analysis in recent memory. Mearsheimer and Walt set out to answer a simple question: why does the United States provide such unwavering diplomatic, economic, and military support to Israel, when that support appears to conflict with America's strategic interests?

Their answer is that the special relationship is sustained primarily by the political influence of the Israel lobby—a loose coalition of individuals and organizations that work to shape U.S. foreign policy in a pro-Israel direction. The authors are careful to distinguish their criticism of the lobby from anti-Semitism, criticizing Israeli policy and lobbying activity while explicitly rejecting prejudicial motives. The book systematically examines the lobby's composition, its methods, and its impact on U.S. policy across the Middle East.
