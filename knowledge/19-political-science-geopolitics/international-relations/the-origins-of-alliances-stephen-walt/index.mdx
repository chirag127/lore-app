*The Origins of Alliances* (1987) is Stephen Walt's seminal contribution to international relations theory. The book challenges the traditional balance-of-power theory by arguing that states balance against threats, not against power alone. Walt introduces four factors that determine how threatening a state appears: aggregate power, geographic proximity, offensive military capability, and aggressive intentions. The book tests this "balance-of-threat" theory against the diplomatic history of the Middle East from 1955 to 1979.

Published as part of the Cornell Studies in Security Affairs, the book quickly became one of the most cited works in alliance theory. It has shaped subsequent research on alignment decisions, security cooperation, and the dynamics of balance-of-power politics. Walt's framework remains central to understanding why states choose to balance or bandwagon in response to emerging threats.
