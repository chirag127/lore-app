John Mearsheimer's *The Tragedy of Great Power Politics* (2001) is the foundational text of offensive realism, one of the most influential and controversial theories in modern international relations. Published just months before the September 11 attacks, the book systematically argues that the anarchic structure of the international system forces great powers to pursue hegemony relentlessly. Mearsheimer rejects the notion that states seek only to survive (defensive realism) or that institutions and commerce can transcend power politics (liberal internationalism).

The book is structured as an exercise in social science theory-building followed by extensive historical testing. Mearsheimer first lays out the assumptions and logic of offensive realism, then derives five empirical predictions about great power behavior. He tests these against the historical record of great power politics from Napoleon through the Cold War, and concludes with a bleak forecast for US-China relations in the twenty-first century. The updated edition (2014) adds a new chapter on the inevitable rivalry between the United States and China.
