# Industrial Policy & State Capitalism

## Why This Sub-subcategory Exists

The debate over the proper role of the state in the economy is one of the oldest in political economy. Industrial policy — deliberate government action to shape the sectoral composition of the economy — was long associated with East Asian developmental states (Japan, South Korea, Taiwan) and dismissed by Washington Consensus orthodoxy as prone to rent-seeking and failure. The global financial crisis of 2008 and the US-China technology competition of the 2020s have dramatically revived interest in industrial policy. The US CHIPS and Science Act and Inflation Reduction Act, Europe's Green Deal, and China's Made in China 2025 represent a new era of activist state intervention.

The intellectual foundations draw from Alexander Hamilton's *Report on Manufactures*, Friedrich List's national political economy, and more recently from Dani Rodrik, Ha-Joon Chang, Mariana Mazzucato, and the East Asian developmental state literature. The study of state capitalism — where the state acts as owner, investor, and strategic planner — has expanded to encompass China's state-owned enterprise system, sovereign wealth funds (Norway, UAE, Singapore, China), and state venture capital. Scholars such as Aldo Musacchio, Sergio Lazzarini, and Yuen Yuen Ang have developed frameworks for classifying and analyzing varieties of state capitalism.

## Key Books

1. Ha-Joon Chang, *Kicking Away the Ladder* — Argues that developed countries used protectionist industrial policy to grow, then denied the same tools to developing countries.
2. Dani Rodrik, *The Globalization Paradox* — How industrial policy can be reconciled with the global economy.
3. Mariana Mazzucato, *The Entrepreneurial State* — Debunks the myth of the lean state and documents the state's role in radical innovation.
4. Alice H. Amsden, *Asia's Next Giant* — The canonical study of South Korea's industrial transformation.
5. Robert H. Wade, *Governing the Market* — Taiwan's state-led industrialization strategy.
6. Aldo Musacchio & Sergio G. Lazzarini, *Reinventing State Capitalism* — The new forms of state capitalism in the 21st century.
7. Yuen Yuen Ang, *How China Escaped the Poverty Trap* — A framework for how China's state-led development evolved.
8. Chalmers Johnson, *MITI and the Japanese Miracle* — The original formulation of the developmental state concept.
9. Alexander Gerschenkron, *Economic Backwardness in Historical Perspective* — The classic theory of late industrialization and state-driven catching up.
10. Fred Block & Matthew R. Keller (eds.), *State of Innovation* — The US innovation system and the hidden developmental state.
