Democracy in America is the result of Alexis de Tocqueville's nine-month journey across the United States in 1831–1832, ostensibly to study the American prison system. What he produced was far more: a two-volume masterwork that anatomizes democratic society with a clarity and depth that no writer before or since has matched. Tocqueville, a French aristocrat born in 1805, approached America with the eye of an outsider and the mind of a philosopher, and his book remains the indispensable guide to the promises and perils of democracy.
