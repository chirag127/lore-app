Cosmos is the companion book to Carl Sagan's legendary 13-part PBS television series, first broadcast in 1980. It became the best-selling science book ever published in English at the time, spending 70 weeks on the New York Times bestseller list and selling over 5 million copies worldwide. Blending astronomy, biology, history, and philosophy, Sagan takes readers on a "spaceship of the imagination" across 15 billion years of cosmic evolution, from the Big Bang to the possible futures of humanity. The book won the Hugo Award for Best Non-Fiction Book in 1981 and was later designated by the Library of Congress as one of 88 books "that shaped America."
