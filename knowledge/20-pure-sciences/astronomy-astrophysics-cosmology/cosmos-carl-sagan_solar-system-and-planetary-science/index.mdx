Cosmos is the companion book to Carl Sagan's legendary 13-part PBS television series, first broadcast in 1980. It became the best-selling science book ever published in English at the time, spending 70 weeks on the New York Times bestseller list and selling over 5 million copies worldwide. Blending astronomy, planetary science, biology, history, and philosophy, Sagan takes readers on a "spaceship of the imagination" across 15 billion years of cosmic evolution — from the Big Bang to the formation of our solar system, the exploration of neighboring worlds, and the possible futures of humanity. The book won the Hugo Award for Best Non-Fiction Book in 1981 and was later designated by the Library of Congress as one of 88 books "that shaped America." From the hellish surface of Venus to the icy plains of Europa, from the Martian landscape to the rings of Saturn, Sagan reveals how the study of other worlds illuminates our own.
