Why did Napoleon's soldiers freeze to death in their uniforms during the Russian winter of 1812? The answer may lie in the tin buttons that crumbled to powder at subzero temperatures — a phenomenon called "tin disease." In *Napoleon's Buttons*, chemists Penny Le Couteur and Jay Burreson use this riveting anecdote to launch a journey through 17 molecules that have fundamentally altered the course of human history. From the pepper that drove European exploration to the quinine that made colonization of the tropics possible, each chapter reveals the hidden chemical stories behind pivotal events, showing that the fate of empires often hung on the properties of a single molecule.
