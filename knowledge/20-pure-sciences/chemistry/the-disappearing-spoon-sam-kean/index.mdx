Why did Gandhi hate iodine? How did a gallium spoon nearly ruin a dinner party? What does cadmium poisoning have to do with Godzilla? In *The Disappearing Spoon*, Sam Kean answers these questions and hundreds more, weaving the periodic table into a tapestry of human ambition, folly, and wonder. The book transforms a familiar wall-chart of 118 boxes into a rollicking adventure story, proving that science is never just about facts — it is about the passionate, flawed, and occasionally mad people who uncover them.
