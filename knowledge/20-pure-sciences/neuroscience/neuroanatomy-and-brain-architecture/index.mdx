# Neuroanatomy & Brain Architecture

## Overview

Neuroanatomy describes the structure of the nervous system at multiple
scales: the cellular (neurons, glia, synapses), the regional (cortical
areas, nuclei, pathways), and the systems level (sensory, motor, limbic
systems). Understanding brain architecture is essential for linking brain
structure to function and dysfunction.
