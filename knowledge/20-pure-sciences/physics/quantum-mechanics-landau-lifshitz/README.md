# Quantum Mechanics: Non-Relativistic Theory — L. D. Landau & E. M. Lifshitz

Volume 3 of the Course of Theoretical Physics. First English edition 1958, translated by J. B. Sykes and J. S. Bell. 3rd edition (1981, Butterworth-Heinemann): ISBN 9780750635394, 689 pp.

The definitive deductive treatment of non-relativistic quantum mechanics, covering the axiomatic foundations through atomic structure, molecular physics, and collision theory. Notable for its extraordinary compression, mathematical rigor, and encyclopedic coverage of angular momentum theory and atomic applications.

**Key features:** 18 chapters covering the full non-relativistic domain, with mathematical appendices that serve as a reference; rigorous deductive presentation from the uncertainty principle; comprehensive treatment of angular momentum addition and Racah algebra; definitive chapters on atomic structure (X) and collision theory (XVII–XVIII).

Used as a graduate text and reference for over 60 years worldwide. Lenin Prize 1962 (awarded to Landau and Lifshitz for the series).
