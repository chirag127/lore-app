Seven Brief Lessons on Physics, originally published in Italian in 2014 and translated into over 50 languages, is Carlo Rovelli's wildly successful introduction to the key ideas of modern physics. Born from a series of newspaper articles, the book condenses general relativity, quantum mechanics, elementary particles, cosmology, black holes, loop quantum gravity, and the nature of consciousness into seven concise, poetic essays. It became an international phenomenon, selling over a million copies and establishing Rovelli as one of the most prominent voices in popular science.
