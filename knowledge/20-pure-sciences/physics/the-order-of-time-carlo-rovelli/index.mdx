What is time? Why do we remember the past and not the future? Does time flow, or is that merely a subjective impression? In *The Order of Time*, theoretical physicist Carlo Rovelli takes readers on a profound journey through modern physics to dismantle our commonsense notions of time and rebuild them from the ground up.

Drawing on relativity, quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, and loop quantum gravity, Rovelli reveals that time as we experience it — unified, directional, present-centered, independent, continuous — is not a fundamental feature of reality. It is an emergent phenomenon arising from entropy, our coarse-grained perspective on the world, and the peculiar way heat interacts with matter. The result is a lyrical, philosophically rich exploration that asks not only what time is, but what it means to exist within it.
