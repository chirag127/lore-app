Natural history classics are the works that established and defined the tradition of writing about the natural world with scientific observation, personal reflection, and literary artistry. Henry David Thoreau's Walden (the foundational American text in the genre), Aldo Leopold's A Sand County Almanac (the land ethic), Rachel Carson's Silent Spring (the environmental call to arms), and Edward Abbey's Desert Solitaire are the canonical works. This subcategory collects these texts and the tradition they inaugurated.
