## Chapter 1: Foundations

The opening chapter reviews the essential preliminaries: divisibility, the division algorithm, greatest common divisors, the Euclidean algorithm, and the fundamental theorem of arithmetic. The review is concise — the authors assume readers have seen this material — and serves as a refresher before the problem sets begin. The chapter includes 10 foundational problems with complete solutions.

## Chapter 2: Divisibility

This chapter presents 14 problems focused on divisibility arguments. The problems test the ability to use the division algorithm, the properties of gcd and lcm, and the fundamental theorem. The solutions emphasize the logical structure of divisibility proofs, a skill essential for all competition number theory.

## Chapter 3: Primes

Fifteen problems on prime numbers: proving primality, finding prime factors, and working with prime distributions. The problems include both computational and theoretical exercises, testing the ability to reason about the properties of primes.

## Chapter 4: Congruences

This substantial chapter contains 20 problems on modular arithmetic. Topics covered include linear congruences, systems of congruences (Chinese remainder theorem), Fermat's little theorem, Euler's theorem, and Wilson's theorem. The problems are sequenced to build from routine applications to complex multi-step reasoning.

## Chapter 5: Quadratic Residues

Fifteen problems on the Legendre and Jacobi symbols, quadratic reciprocity, and the solution of quadratic congruences. The chapter emphasizes the computational aspects of quadratic reciprocity and its use in competition problems.

## Chapter 6: Diophantine Equations

Twenty problems covering the full range of Diophantine techniques: linear equations, Pythagorean triples, infinite descent, modular arguments, bounding, and factorization in rings of algebraic integers. This is the most challenging chapter.

## Chapter 7: Sums of Squares

Ten problems on the representation of integers as sums of squares, including Fermat's theorem on sums of two squares, Lagrange's theorem on sums of four squares, and related topics.

## Chapter 8: Advanced Topics

Ten problems on the Chinese remainder theorem, order of an element, primitive roots, and Hensel's lemma. These problems draw on multiple techniques and prepare the reader for the most difficult competition problems.

## Reading Guide

### Sufficiency Assessment

This summary captures the book's organization but cannot convey the quality of the solutions, which are the book's main value.

### Recommended Reading Path

| Reader Type | Time | What to Read |
|---|---|---|
| Casual | ~15 min | This summary |
| Competition prep | ~10-20 hr | All problems |
| Serious aspirant | ~20-35 hr | Full book, solving each problem before reading solutions |

### What You'll Miss by Not Reading the Full Book

The detailed solutions, the multiple solution approaches for many problems, and the strategic insights that are the book's main pedagogical value.
