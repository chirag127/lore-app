104 Number Theory Problems distills the essence of olympiad number theory into a compact problem-solving manual. Co-authored by three of the most prominent figures in competition mathematics, the book draws directly on materials developed for training the United States International Mathematical Olympiad team.

The problems are organized into eight chapters covering the full competition number theory syllabus: fundamentals, divisibility, primes, modular arithmetic, Diophantine equations, sums of squares, the Chinese remainder theorem, and advanced topics. Each chapter begins with a concise review of essential theory, followed by carefully sequenced problems that progress from routine to challenging, with complete solutions provided for every problem.
