A Course in Arithmetic is remarkably concise: Serre packs the essentials of quadratic forms, Dirichlets theorem, and modular forms into just over 100 pages. Originally published in French as Cours dArithmetique and translated into English as volume 7 of Springer's Graduate Texts in Mathematics, it belongs to every number theorist's library.

The book divides into two parts. The first develops the algebraic theory of quadratic forms over the rational numbers, culminating in the Hasse-Minkowski classification. The second introduces analytic methods — Dirichlet characters, L-functions, and modular forms — showing how complex analysis illuminates arithmetic questions. Serre's voice is unmistakable: precise, economical, and elegant, with not a word wasted.
