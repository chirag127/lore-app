Joseph Silverman's A Friendly Introduction to Number Theory is exactly what its title promises: the gentlest possible entry into a subject often perceived as difficult. Silverman, a leading researcher in elliptic curves and arithmetic geometry at Brown University, designed this book specifically for students who may never take another mathematics course.

The book starts with nothing more than high school algebra — Pythagorean triples and the Euclidean algorithm — and gradually builds to substantial topics: the RSA cryptosystem, quadratic reciprocity, Pell's equation, and even elliptic curves. Every new idea is introduced through numerical examples that the reader can compute by hand or with a calculator, and the exposition remains informal and conversational throughout.
