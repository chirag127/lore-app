Dummit and Foote's *Abstract Algebra* has become the standard graduate introduction to abstract algebra for a generation of mathematics students. First published in 1991 and now in its third edition, the book is prized for its encyclopedic coverage, its pedagogical clarity, and the remarkable breadth and depth of its exercises.

The book begins with group theory, moves through ring theory and module theory, and culminates in a thorough treatment of Galois theory with applications. Later chapters cover representation theory, commutative algebra, and homological algebra. What distinguishes Dummit and Foote is the extraordinary care taken with exposition: definitions are motivated, proofs are detailed without being pedantic, and the examples are plentiful and illuminating. The problem sets, which range from routine to research-level, are legendary in their scope.
