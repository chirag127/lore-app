Joseph Rotman's *Advanced Modern Algebra* is a substantial graduate text that takes the reader from the standard first-year curriculum into the advanced topics that connect algebra to the rest of modern mathematics. Published in 2002, the book reflects Rotman's belief that algebra should be taught as a living subject connected to geometry, topology, and number theory.

The book covers groups, rings, modules, and Galois theory at a brisk pace before moving into homological algebra, category theory, and commutative algebra. Later chapters introduce algebraic number theory and algebraic geometry. Rotman's style is engaging and discursive, with historical asides and extensive motivation. The book is particularly valued for its treatment of homological algebra, which occupies nearly a third of the text and provides a foundation for further study in algebraic topology and algebraic geometry.
