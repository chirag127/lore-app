I. Martin Isaacs's *Algebra: A Graduate Course* is a carefully crafted introduction to graduate-level algebra distinguished by its focus on non-commutative algebra and its extraordinary problem sets. Published in 1993, the book reflects Isaacs's philosophy that algebra is best learned through active problem-solving.

The book covers groups, rings, modules, fields, and Galois theory, with particular depth in group theory and the structure of non-commutative rings. Isaacs was a renowned group theorist, and his expertise shines in the treatment of solvable groups, nilpotent groups, and the representation theory of finite groups. The writing is clear and conversational, making difficult concepts accessible without sacrificing rigor. The exercises — over 800 of them — are legendary among students and instructors for their quality, ranging from routine to deeply challenging.
