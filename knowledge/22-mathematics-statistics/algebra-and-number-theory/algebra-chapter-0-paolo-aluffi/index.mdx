Paolo Aluffi's *Algebra: Chapter 0* is a singular entry in the graduate algebra textbook landscape. Published in 2009, it takes the radical approach of introducing category theory in the very first chapter and using it throughout as a organizing framework.

The book treats groups, rings, modules, fields, and Galois theory but does so through the lens of universal properties, functors, and natural transformations. This categorical perspective — normally reserved for advanced study — becomes the thread that ties everything together. Aluffi writes in a conversational yet precise style that has made the book unusually popular among self-taught students. The subtitle "Chapter 0" signals that this is preparatory material for what Aluffi considers the real subject: algebraic geometry and advanced algebra.
