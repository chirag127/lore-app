Serge Lang's *Algebra* is among the most influential graduate textbooks in mathematics. First published in 1965 and substantially revised through its third edition, it has trained generations of mathematicians. The book covers the full sweep of abstract algebra: group theory, ring theory, module theory, Galois theory, linear algebra, commutative algebra, and homological algebra.

Lang's approach is structural and concise. He emphasizes the internal logic of algebraic systems rather than computational technique, producing a text that rewards careful study. While infamous for its demanding style and occasionally elliptical proofs, the book remains a rite of passage for graduate students and an indispensable reference for working mathematicians. Its influence can be measured by its longevity — it remains in active classroom use more than half a century after its first appearance.
