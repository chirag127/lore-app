Nathan Jacobson's *Basic Algebra I* is the first volume of a two-part masterpiece by one of the preeminent algebraists of the twentieth century. Published originally in 1974 and later reissued by Dover, this book offers a systematic, rigorous treatment of the core areas of algebra at the graduate level.

Jacobson was renowned for his work in ring theory, Lie algebras, and Jordan algebras, and his expertise permeates every page. The book moves from set-theoretic foundations through groups, rings, modules, vector spaces, and culminates in a thorough treatment of Galois theory. Its hallmark is clarity of exposition combined with theoretical depth — each concept is motivated and developed with care, and the exercises are carefully chosen to extend the reader's understanding.
