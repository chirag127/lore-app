*Basic Algebra II* completes Nathan Jacobson's monumental two-volume survey of algebra. Published in 1980, it takes the reader from the foundations established in Volume I into the advanced topics that define modern algebraic research.

The book covers homological algebra, including derived functors and spectral sequences, the representation theory of finite groups and associative algebras, commutative algebra with connections to algebraic geometry, and the theory of algebraic groups. Jacobson's treatment is distinguished by its clarity, its historical perspective, and its choice of topics that have proven enduringly important. The second volume is especially notable for its treatment of representation theory, reflecting Jacobson's own deep contributions to the subject.
