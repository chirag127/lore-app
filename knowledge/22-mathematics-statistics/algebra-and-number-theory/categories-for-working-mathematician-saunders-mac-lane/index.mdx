*Categories for the Working Mathematician* (CWM) is the definitive introduction to category theory by its co-founder, Saunders Mac Lane. First published in 1971 as volume 5 of Springer's Graduate Texts in Mathematics series, it established category theory as a mature subject with systematic methods applicable across algebra, topology, and logic. The book's central achievement is demonstrating that the language of categories, functors, and natural transformations is not merely "general abstract nonsense" but a powerful tool for unifying mathematical structures, clarifying universal constructions, and revealing deep analogies between apparently disparate fields. A second edition in 1998 added chapters on braided monoidal categories and 2-categories.
