Joseph Gallian's *Contemporary Abstract Algebra* stands out among undergraduate algebra texts for its accessibility and breadth of applications. First published in 1986 and now in its seventh edition, it has become one of the most widely used undergraduate algebra textbooks in the United States.

Gallian's approach is notably warm and inviting. He introduces each major concept through concrete examples and gradually builds toward abstraction. The book is famous for its wide-ranging applications of algebra to cryptography, coding theory, polya counting, and combinatorial designs. Each chapter opens with historical vignettes that give faces and stories to the mathematicians behind the theory. The exercises are carefully graded and include both computational practice and proof-based problems, making the book suitable for a range of student abilities.
