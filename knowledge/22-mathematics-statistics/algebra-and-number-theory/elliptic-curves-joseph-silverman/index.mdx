First published in 1986 as volume 106 of Springer's Graduate Texts in Mathematics, The Arithmetic of Elliptic Curves remains the standard graduate-level introduction to the subject. Before Silverman's book, no single volume provided a systematic account of the arithmetic theory of elliptic curves — students had to piece together the theory from Cassels's lecture notes, Shimura's Introduction to the Arithmetic Theory of Automorphic Functions, and scattered research articles. Silverman changed that, and his book has educated a generation of number theorists.

The book develops the necessary algebraic geometry, then proceeds through the geometry of elliptic curves, their formal groups, and their arithmetic over finite fields, the complex numbers, local fields, and global fields. It culminates in proofs of the Mordell-Weil theorem (finite generation of rational points) and Siegel's theorem (finiteness of integral points). For its clarity and comprehensiveness, Silverman's two-volume treatment earned the 2008 AMS Steele Prize for Mathematical Exposition.
