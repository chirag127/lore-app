George E. Andrews' Number Theory stands apart from other introductory texts. Andrews, a world authority on partition theory and the discoverer of the Andrews-Gordon identities, brings a combinatorial sensibility to classical number theory. First published in 1971 by Saunders and later reprinted by Dover, the book remains fresh and distinctive.

The text moves from the basics of divisibility and congruences through quadratic reciprocity and Diophantine equations, culminating in a treatment of partitions that reflects Andrews' own research. His approach is problem-centered: each concept is introduced through examples and exercises, and the reader builds understanding by doing mathematics rather than merely reading about it.
