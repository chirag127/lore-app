## Part I: Fundamentals of Number Theory

### Chapter 1: Divisibility

The book opens with a systematic treatment of divisibility. The division algorithm, greatest common divisors, the Euclidean algorithm, and the fundamental theorem of arithmetic are presented with an emphasis on problem-solving applications. The theoretical content is concise — the authors assume basic familiarity — and the focus is on applying these concepts to problems of graded difficulty. The chapter includes problems on the gcd and lcm, representations of integers, and the order of operations in arithmetic.

### Chapter 2: Primes

Andreescu and Andrica cover prime numbers with a problem-solver's eye. Topics include the infinitude of primes (multiple proofs), the sieve of Eratosthenes, prime-counting functions, Mersenne and Fermat primes, and the distribution of primes. The problem set includes both computational exercises and theoretical extensions, with an emphasis on the techniques used to prove results about primes.

### Chapter 3: Congruences

Modular arithmetic is developed in detail. The chapter covers the basic properties of congruences, the Chinese remainder theorem, complete and reduced residue systems, Fermat's little theorem, Euler's theorem, and Wilson's theorem. The presentation is organized around problem types: linear congruences, systems of congruences, and congruences with unknown exponents. Each technique is illustrated with examples from competitions.

### Chapter 4: Quadratic Residues

The Legendre and Jacobi symbols are introduced, and the law of quadratic reciprocity is proved. The chapter includes applications to the determination of quadratic residues, the evaluation of sums of Legendre symbols, and the solution of quadratic congruences. The problems range from straightforward computations to deep applications of reciprocity.

### Chapter 5: Arithmetic Functions

The standard arithmetic functions are studied: the divisor function, the sum-of-divisors function, the Euler totient function, the Mobius function, and the Riemann zeta function (briefly). The Mobius inversion formula is proved and applied. The chapter includes a discussion of perfect numbers and amicable pairs, with computational problems.

### Chapter 6: Diophantine Equations

This substantial chapter covers linear Diophantine equations, Pythagorean triples, Pell's equation, and higher-degree equations. The treatment emphasizes the techniques most useful in competitions: infinite descent, factorization methods, use of congruences, and bounding arguments. The chapter includes problems from international olympiads.

## Part II: Special Topics

### Chapter 7: Irrational Numbers

The classical proofs of irrationality are presented: the irrationality of sqrt(2), the irrationality of sqrt(p) for prime p, and the irrationality of e and pi. The chapter includes more advanced topics such as the irrationality of zeta(3) (Apery's theorem) and transcendental numbers.

### Chapter 8: Sequences of Integers

This chapter studies integer sequences from a number-theoretic perspective: Fibonacci numbers, Lucas sequences, and linear recurrences. The connections between recurrence relations and number theory are explored through divisibility properties, congruences, and periodicity.

## Reading Guide

### Sufficiency Assessment

This summary captures the organizational structure but cannot convey the depth of the problem sets or the systematic approach to problem classification.

### Recommended Reading Path

| Reader Type | Time | What to Read |
|---|---|---|
| Casual | ~15 min | This summary |
| Olympiad aspirant | ~8-15 hr | Part I selectively + problems |
| Serious competitor | ~25-40 hr | Full book |

### Chapters to Read in Full

- **Chapter 3** — Congruences, the most used tool in competition problems
- **Chapter 6** — Diophantine equations, the hardest and most rewarding topic

### What You'll Miss by Not Reading the Full Book

The systematic problem classification, the hundreds of worked examples, and the progression from routine to highly challenging problems.
