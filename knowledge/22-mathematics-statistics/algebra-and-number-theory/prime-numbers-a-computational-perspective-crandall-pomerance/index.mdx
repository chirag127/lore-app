Prime Numbers: A Computational Perspective by Richard Crandall and Carl Pomerance is the definitive reference on the computational side of prime number theory. Crandall was a renowned applied number theorist known for his work on the Apple Integer Fast Fourier Transform and large-scale prime computation; Pomerance is the co-discoverer of the quadratic sieve factorization algorithm.

The book covers every major algorithmic topic in prime number theory: primality testing (from simple trial division through Miller-Rabin to the AKS algorithm), integer factorization (Pollard rho, quadratic sieve, number field sieve), prime generation, elliptic curve methods, and distributed computation. The treatment is mathematically rigorous while remaining grounded in practical algorithmic concerns.
