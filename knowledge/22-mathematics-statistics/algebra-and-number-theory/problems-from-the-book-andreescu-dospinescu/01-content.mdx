## Chapter 1: Divisibility and Primes

The opening chapter presents problems that test the deepest aspects of divisibility and prime number theory. The problems go well beyond standard competition material, requiring creative use of the Euclidean algorithm, the fundamental theorem, and properties of gcd and lcm. Several problems involve proving that certain expressions are integers, that certain numbers are prime, or that certain equations have no integer solutions. The solutions demonstrate the full range of elementary techniques.

## Chapter 2: Congruences

This chapter covers problems on modular arithmetic at an advanced level. The problems test the ability to use the Chinese remainder theorem, Fermat-Euler theorem, and Wilson's theorem in non-standard situations. Many problems involve finding all solutions to congruence systems with parameters, or proving the impossibility of certain congruences. The solutions emphasize the strategic thinking required for difficult problems.

## Chapter 3: Diophantine Equations

The heart of the book: 30+ problems on Diophantine equations, ranging from very difficult to brutally hard. The techniques employed include infinite descent, factorization in Z and Z[i], modular arguments, bounding, and the use of inequalities. Many problems are drawn from or inspired by the hardest problems ever posed at the IMO and other elite competitions. The solutions are detailed and often present multiple approaches.

## Chapter 4: Quadratic Reciprocity and Higher Reciprocity

Problems on the Legendre and Jacobi symbols, the law of quadratic reciprocity, and its higher generalizations. The chapter includes problems on the representation of integers by quadratic forms, the evaluation of Gauss sums, and the reciprocity laws for cubic and biquadratic residues. This is the most algebraically demanding chapter.

## Chapter 5: Inequalities and Estimates

A distinctive feature of this book is the inclusion of problems on inequalities in number theory. The chapter covers techniques for bounding arithmetic functions, estimating sums, and proving asymptotic results. The problems connect number theory with analysis and combinatorics.

## Chapter 6: Functional Equations and Polynomials

The final chapter covers problems on functional equations with number-theoretic content and polynomial problems with integer constraints. Topics include integer-valued polynomials, polynomial congruences, and functional equations over the integers.

## Reading Guide

### Sufficiency Assessment

This summary conveys the structure but cannot convey the difficulty and beauty of the problems themselves.

### Recommended Reading Path

| Reader Type | Time | What to Read |
|---|---|---|
| Casual | ~15 min | This summary |
| Enthusiast | ~10-20 hr | Selected problems with solutions |
| Elite competitor | ~30-50 hr | Full book |

### What You'll Miss by Not Reading the Full Book

The experience of struggling with and then solving (or reading the solution to) truly difficult problems, which is the book's entire purpose.
