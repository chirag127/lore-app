Problems from the Book is not for the faint of heart. Titu Andreescu and Gabriel Dospinescu have assembled a collection of problems that push far beyond the standard olympiad syllabus, drawing on number theory, algebra, analysis, and combinatorics in equal measure. The title's allusion to Paul Erdos' concept of "The Book" — the mythical volume containing the most elegant proofs — signals the authors' ambition.

The book is organized thematically, with chapters on divisibility, primes, congruences, Diophantine equations, inequalities, functional equations, and polynomials. What makes it extraordinary is the depth of the problems and the thoroughness of the solutions. Many problems are drawn from or inspired by the hardest competition problems ever posed, including problems from the IMO Shortlist, the Romanian Master of Mathematics, and various national olympiads.
