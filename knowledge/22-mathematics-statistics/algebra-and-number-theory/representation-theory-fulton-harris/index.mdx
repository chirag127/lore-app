*Representation Theory: A First Course* is the standard introductory text for finite-dimensional representations of Lie groups and Lie algebras, written by two masters of algebraic geometry. Published in 1991 as volume 129 of Springer's Graduate Texts in Mathematics, it takes a deliberately concrete, example-driven approach: general theory is developed sparingly, mainly as useful language to describe phenomena already encountered in explicit computations with the classical groups. The book begins with a compact tour through the representation theory of finite groups, then proceeds through Lie groups and Lie algebras, and culminates in a thorough treatment of the classical Lie algebras and their representations. Its defining characteristic is the conviction that beginners learn best by working through many concrete examples before confronting abstract classification theorems.
