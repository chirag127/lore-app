# Seminumerical Algorithms — Deep Dive

## Random Numbers

Knuth opens with an ancient joke: there is no such thing as a random number, only numbers generated by a method. Knuth establishes the field of pseudorandomness formally.

### Linear Congruential Generator

The LCG: X_{n+1} = (aX_n + c) mod m. Knuth shows how choice of parameters affects period and spectral quality.

| Parameter | Role |
|-----------|------|
| a | Multiplier |
| c | Increment |
| m | Modulus |
| X_0 | Seed |

A maximal-period LCG requires: c relatively prime to m; a = 1 mod 4; a = 1 mod all prime factors of m.

### Combined Generators

Better performance comes from combining two or three inferior generators to decorrelate artifacts. Knuth analyzes the spectral test as a measure of lattice structure.

### Statistical Tests

Randomness tests include frequency tests, serial tests, the autocorrelation test, runs test, gap test, poker test, and the chi-square test. No finite test guarantees randomness — each only exposes particular defects.

## Arithmetic

### Multiple-Precision Arithmetic

Knuth extends the MIX model to handle integers wider than one word. Multiple-precision addition, subtraction, and multiplication are explained with carry handling.

The grade-school multiplication algorithm is O(n^2). The Karatsuba method reduces to O(n^1.585), and the FFT-based Schönhage-Strassen achieves O(n log n log log n).

### Radix Conversion

Converting decimal fractions to binary is not trivial because 1/10 is a recurring binary fraction. Knuth shows the pitfalls and presents strategies to convert with explicit error bounds.

---

## Euclid's Algorithm

GCD is the cornerstone of number theory. Knuth reassures us that Euclid did not invent GCD; he merely stated the algorithm whose proof now bears his name.

The extended Euclidean algorithm also finds integers x, y such that ax + by = gcd(a, b).

---

## Fourier Transforms

Knuth covers the Fast Fourier Transform (FFT), allowing polynomial multiplication from O(n²) to O(n log n). The Cooley-Tukey algorithm and derivation of Boolean convolution complete the chapter.

---

## Prime Numbers

Prime generation via the Sieve of Eratosthenes, table construction, and primality tests (pseudoprimes, Lucas-Lehmer, Pollard's rho).

---

## Practical Takeaways

- Your generator's seed selection matters significantly.
- Statistical tests are a necessary maintenance step in any simulation code.
- Mod operator pitfalls: negative operands, overflow behavior vary by language.
- Hashing and PRNG share similar structure — both relate to permutations of a finite state space.
