# The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 2

## Overview

_Volume 2: Seminumerical Algorithms_ is the second installment of Knuth's life work. It covers algorithms that are "numeric" in the broad sense: not purely discrete symbolic operations (those of Volume 1) but algorithms for approximating real numbers, generating randomness, and computing with quantities like integer fractions and modular arithmetic.

Topics: random number generation (linear congruential, combined generators, statistical tests), arithmetic (multi-precision, radix conversion), polynomial arithmetic, Fourier transforms (FFT), Euclid's GCD and continued fractions, factoring large numbers, primality testing, and the Euclidean algorithm.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. True randomness cannot be produced by deterministic machines — best we get is pseudorandomness.
2. Linear congruential generators are simple, fast, and surprisingly effective.
3. Statistical testing (spectral test, chi-square) is essential to reject weak generators.
4. Multi-precision arithmetic requires care with carry and alignment.
5. The Euclidean algorithm is both ancient and ideal for computers.
6. Fourier transforms provide the butterfly-shift between time and frequency domains.
7. Primes are the atoms of arithmetic; prime tables predate computers by millennia.
8. Complexity of arithmetic grows more quickly than its elegant simplicity suggests.
