## Chapter 1: Divisibility

The book opens with a rigorous treatment of divisibility. The division algorithm is proved carefully, the Euclidean algorithm is presented as both a computational tool and a theoretical device, and the fundamental theorem of arithmetic is proved using the method of infinite descent. The chapter includes the standard Diophantine equation ax + by = c, with complete characterization of solvability. The exercises include many classical problems.

## Chapter 2: Congruences

Niven and Zuckerman develop the theory of congruences with characteristic rigor. The basic properties are proved, the Chinese remainder theorem is given a constructive proof, and Fermat's and Wilson's theorems are proved. The chapter includes a discussion of polynomial congruences and Hensel's lemma. The treatment is systematic and complete, leaving no step unverified.

## Chapter 3: Quadratic Reciprocity

The Legendre symbol is introduced, and the law of quadratic reciprocity is proved. The authors give a proof based on Gauss's lemma, with all cases checked carefully. The Jacobi symbol extends the Legendre symbol to composite moduli. Applications include the solution of quadratic congruences and the representation of primes by quadratic forms.

## Chapter 4: Quadratic Forms

This chapter studies binary quadratic forms, their reduction theory, and the representation of integers by forms. The class number is introduced, and Dirichlet's formula for the class number is derived. The treatment is more advanced than in most undergraduate texts, preparing the reader for algebraic number theory.

## Chapter 5: Diophantine Equations

The classical Diophantine equations are treated: the Pythagorean equation, Pell's equation, and the equation x^4 + y^4 = z^4. The solution of Pell's equation uses continued fractions, which are developed in the next chapter. The chapter includes a discussion of Fermat's method of infinite descent.

## Chapter 6: Continued Fractions

A thorough treatment of continued fractions, including finite and infinite continued fractions, periodic continued fractions, and their application to Pell's equation. The authors prove that the continued fraction expansion of a quadratic irrational is periodic and show how to find all solutions of Pell's equation.

## Chapter 7: Analytic Number Theory

The final chapter introduces analytic methods: arithmetic functions, the Mobius inversion formula, Chebyshev's theorem on the distribution of primes, and a proof of the prime number theorem (in later editions). The treatment is necessarily brief but gives the flavor of analytic methods.

## Reading Guide

### Sufficiency Assessment

This summary captures the main topics but cannot convey the precision of the proofs, which is the book's distinguishing feature.

### Recommended Reading Path

| Reader Type | Time | What to Read |
|---|---|---|
| Casual | ~15 min | This summary |
| Interested | ~3-5 hr | Summary + Chapters 1-3, 5 |
| Mathematics major | ~15-30 hr | Full book |

### Chapters to Read in Full

- **Chapter 2** — The rigorous development of congruence theory
- **Chapter 3** — The complete proof of quadratic reciprocity
- **Chapter 6** — Continued fractions, masterfully presented

### What You'll Miss by Not Reading the Full Book

The precision of the proofs, the carefully designed exercise sets, and the gradual development from elementary to advanced topics.
