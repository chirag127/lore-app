Niven and Zuckerman's The Theory of Numbers has been the gold standard for rigorous undergraduate number theory since its first publication in 1960. Hugh Montgomery, a leading figure in analytic number theory, joined the fifth edition, updating the text while preserving its renowned precision.

The book is distinguished by its clarity and rigor. Every theorem is proved carefully, every definition is precise, and the exercises range from computational to deeply theoretical. The authors assume mathematical maturity but minimal specific background — a solid grasp of calculus and basic algebra suffices. Topics covered include divisibility, congruences, quadratic reciprocity, continued fractions, and an introduction to algebraic numbers.
