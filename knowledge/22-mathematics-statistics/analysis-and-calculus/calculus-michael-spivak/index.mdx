**Calculus** by Michael Spivak is widely regarded as the finest proof-based calculus textbook ever written. First published in 1967, it transformed how rigorous calculus is taught to undergraduates. Unlike standard calculus texts that emphasize computation and application, Spivak's *Calculus* builds the entire subject from first principles — beginning with the properties of real numbers and ending with complex power series and the construction of the real numbers themselves.

The book is famous for its clarity, wit, and uncompromising intellectual honesty. Spivak proves every theorem, gives counterexamples that show why each hypothesis is necessary, and includes exercises that range from routine to devastatingly difficult. Fernando Q. Gouvêa, reviewing the book for the MAA, called it simply "the best Calculus textbook ever written."

This is a book for students who want to *understand* calculus — not just compute with it. It has been used for decades in honors calculus programs at Harvard, MIT, Columbia, and many other leading universities, and it remains the gold standard for the transition from computational to proof-based mathematics.
