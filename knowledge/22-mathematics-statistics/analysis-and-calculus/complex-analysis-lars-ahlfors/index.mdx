First published in 1953, Lars Ahlfors's *Complex Analysis* is one of the most celebrated mathematics textbooks of the twentieth century. Written by the only Fields Medalist ever to produce an introductory textbook, it has shaped how generations of mathematicians learn and understand the theory of analytic functions of one complex variable. Ahlfors's approach is fundamentally geometric: power series are not introduced until Chapter 4, and the emphasis throughout is on conformal mapping, the Riemann mapping theorem, and the beautiful interplay between complex analysis and topology.

The book is renowned for its concision and rigor. Every sentence carries meaning; no page is wasted. This density makes it challenging for self-study but deeply rewarding for those who work through it carefully. The third edition (1979, reprinted by AMS Chelsea in 2021) added improved proofs in Chapter 4 (Cauchy's theorem) and introduced sheaf-theoretic language in Chapter 8, while preserving the original's distinctive geometric spirit.
