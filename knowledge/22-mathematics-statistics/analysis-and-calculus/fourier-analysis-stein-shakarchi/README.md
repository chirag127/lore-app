# Fourier Analysis: An Introduction

**Authors:** Elias M. Stein & Rami Shakarchi  
**Year:** 2003  
**ISBN:** 9780691113845  
**Pages:** 328  
**Series:** Princeton Lectures in Analysis, Volume 1  
**Publisher:** Princeton University Press

## About

The first volume of the celebrated Princeton Lectures in Analysis series, arising from Stein's radical reorganization of the advanced undergraduate analysis curriculum at Princeton. The book covers Fourier series (Chapters 1--4), the Fourier transform on R and R^d (Chapters 5--6), and finite Fourier analysis with applications to Dirichlet's theorem on arithmetic progressions (Chapters 7--8). It uses Riemann integration throughout (Lebesgue integration is deferred to Volume 3).

## Key Features

- Physical motivation via vibrating string and heat equation
- Rigorous treatment of Fourier series convergence (Fejér, Cesàro, Abel)
- Fourier transform on Schwartz space with Plancherel and inversion
- Applications: isoperimetric inequality, Weyl equidistribution, nowhere differentiable function, PDEs
- Finite Fourier analysis on abelian groups, fast Fourier transform
- Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions
