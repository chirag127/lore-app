Vladimir Arnold's *Ordinary Differential Equations* (1973) is a landmark textbook that fundamentally reimagines how ODEs are taught. Rather than the traditional cookbook of integration tricks, Arnold builds the subject around two central geometric ideas: the rectification theorem for vector fields and the theory of one-parameter groups of linear transformations. The pendulum equation appears on page one and returns throughout as a unifying thread — each new concept tested against it. Translated from Russian by Richard A. Silverman and published by MIT Press, this concise 280-page volume influenced generations of mathematicians with its emphasis on phase spaces, phase flows, and qualitative geometric reasoning over algebraic manipulation.
