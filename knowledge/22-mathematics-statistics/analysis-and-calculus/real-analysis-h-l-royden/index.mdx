Royden's *Real Analysis* is the canonical graduate text that taught generations of mathematicians the modern theory of measure and integration. First published in 1963, it established a three-part architecture that became the gold standard: concrete Lebesgue theory on the real line, abstract topological and Banach spaces, and general measure and integration theory. Its concise, no-nonsense style demands active reading but rewards with a deep, permanent understanding of the tools that underpin modern analysis, probability, and PDEs.
