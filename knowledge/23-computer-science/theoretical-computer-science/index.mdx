# Theoretical Computer Science

## Overview

Theoretical computer science is the branch of computer science that deals with the mathematical foundations of computation. It draws heavily on mathematical logic, formal language theory, and the philosophy of mathematics. Questions addressed include: What are the fundamental limits of formal proof? How do we define and reason about computation itself? What does it mean for a statement to be true but unprovable?

This subcategory covers the deep logical and mathematical underpinnings of computation: Gödel's incompleteness theorems, the nature of formal systems, the connections between logic and computability, and the foundational results that define what computers can and cannot do.
