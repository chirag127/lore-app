## About the Author

Hussein Nasser is a software engineer and tech educator known for building **asyncpg**, the popular async PostgreSQL driver that ships as part of Python's standard library. He spent years at Google working on large-scale infrastructure before dedicating himself to education through his YouTube channel and writing. His teaching style prioritises pragmatic, battlefield-tested advice over textbook theory.

## Why This Book Matters

Most engineering books teach you to write code. This one teaches you what happens **after** the code ships. Nasser makes the explicit and often-unspoken transition that separates junior from senior engineers: from solving assigned problems to defining which problems are worth solving; from writing features to owning systems; from being evaluated on output to being evaluated on outcomes.

## What You'll Learn

- **What a senior engineer actually is** — the role definition nobody gives you
- **Ownership** as a technical and organisational concept, not just a buzzword
- **System design** from the perspective of someone who will have to operate what they build
- **Writing production-ready code** — the difference between "it works" and "it ships"
- **The non-coding skills** that determine how far your technical ability can take you: meetings, design documents, incident response, and stakeholder communication
- **Building competence at every level** from IC2 through Staff and beyond
