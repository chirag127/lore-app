A landmark study of why great companies fail — not because they make bad decisions, but because they follow good management practices in the face of disruptive innovation. Clayton Christensen's theory of disruptive innovation explains how successful incumbents are systematically blindsided by technologies that initially underperform in mainstream markets but eventually topple industry leaders.
