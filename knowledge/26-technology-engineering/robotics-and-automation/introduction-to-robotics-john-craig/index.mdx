*Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control* by John J. Craig is the most widely adopted textbook for introductory robotics courses at the university level, first published in 1986 and now in its fourth edition (Pearson, 2018). The book blends mechanical engineering, computer science, and control theory to present an intuitive yet mathematically rigorous treatment of robotic manipulation. Its thirteen chapters progress from spatial descriptions and transformations through forward and inverse kinematics, velocities and Jacobians, dynamics, trajectory planning, mechanical design, linear and nonlinear control, force control, and programming languages for robotics.
