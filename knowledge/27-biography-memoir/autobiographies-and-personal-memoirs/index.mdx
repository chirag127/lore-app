Autobiography and memoir are acts of self-construction: the author shapes their own story, choosing what to reveal and what to conceal. This subcategory collects first-person narratives from all walks of life — spiritual autobiographies (Augustine, Malcolm X), political memoirs (Churchill, Obama), survival narratives (Elie Wiesel, Viktor Frankl), and memoirs of ordinary lives rendered extraordinary through storytelling.
