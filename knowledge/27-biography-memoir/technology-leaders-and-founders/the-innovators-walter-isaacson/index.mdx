*The Innovators: How a Group of Hackers, Geniuses, and Geeks Created the Digital Revolution* by Walter Isaacson is a masterwork of narrative nonfiction that traces the 170-year history of the digital revolution from Ada Lovelace's visionary insights in 1843 to the rise of Google and Wikipedia. Published in 2014 by Simon & Schuster, the book synthesizes the lives and work of dozens of inventors, scientists, engineers, and entrepreneurs — from Charles Babbage and Alan Turing to Bill Gates, Steve Jobs, and Tim Berners-Lee — into a single, propulsive narrative. Its central thesis, argued through every chapter, is that innovation is fundamentally collaborative: the great breakthroughs of the digital age emerged from teams, institutions, and communities that built on each other's work, not from solitary geniuses working in isolation.
