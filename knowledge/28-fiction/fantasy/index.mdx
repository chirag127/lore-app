Fantasy creates worlds that could not exist — and in doing so, reveals truths about the one that does. From Tolkien's Middle-earth to Le Guin's Earthsea, from epic quest narratives to urban fantasy set in our own world, the genre explores themes of power, destiny, good and evil, and the hero's journey through the lens of the supernatural and magical.

## Sub-Subcategories

- **[Epic Fantasy & Secondary World-Building](epic-fantasy-and-secondary-world-building)** — Fully realized invented worlds and grand quest narratives.
- **[Mythopoeic Fantasy](mythopoeic-fantasy)** — Fantasy that creates or draws on mythological frameworks.
- **[Urban Fantasy & Contemporary Fantasy](urban-fantasy-and-contemporary-fantasy)** — Magic intruding into the modern everyday world.
