title: 'Mythopoeic Fantasy'
parent: '28-fiction/fantasy'

description: 'Fantasy that creates or draws on mythological frameworks to explore archetypal themes.'
---

Mythopoeic (myth-making) fantasy deliberately invokes the power of myth, often drawing on existing mythological traditions or creating new ones. The term was coined by Tolkien to describe a literature that creates a sense of numinous depth — stories that feel as though they emerge from an ancient oral tradition. Includes the works of Tolkien, Lewis, Le Guin, and more recent mythopoeic writers.
