Historical fiction reconstructs past eras through imagined characters and events, blending historical research with narrative art. From the novels of Walter Scott and Hilary Mantel to the genre's many contemporary practitioners, historical fiction offers a way of entering the past — not as history (which explains) but as experience (which makes us feel what it was like to be there).
