Horror literature aims to provoke fear, dread, or disgust — but the best of it does so in service of exploring deeper human anxieties about death, the unknown, the monstrous, and the limits of the self. From Gothic fiction's haunted castles and Byronic villains to contemporary horror's psychological and cosmic dimensions, this genre peers into the dark to understand the light.
