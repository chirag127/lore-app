British and Irish literature comprises the oldest continuous tradition of the English novel, from its eighteenth-century origins (Defoe, Richardson, Fielding) through the Victorian period (Dickens, Eliot, the Brontës, Hardy), modernism (Woolf, Joyce, Lawrence), and the rich diversity of contemporary British and Irish fiction. This tradition is defined by a deep engagement with social class, the rural and the urban, empire and its aftermath, and the evolution of consciousness itself.
