Literary fiction is distinguished from genre fiction not by subject matter but by its primary commitment to aesthetic ambition, psychological depth, and language itself — it is fiction that asks to be read not only for story but for style, meaning, and the expansion of what the novel can do. This subcategory covers the major traditions and periods of literary fiction: the British and Irish novel, the American tradition, European literature, Latin American magical realism, Japanese and Indian literary fiction, and the contemporary global novel.
