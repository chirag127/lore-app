Detective fiction follows a distinct structure: crime, investigation, revelation. The genre's golden age (Poe's Dupin, Doyle's Holmes, Christie's Poirot, Sayers's Wimsey) established the rules of the fair-play puzzle. Hard-boiled detective fiction (Hammett, Chandler) brought realism and moral ambiguity. Contemporary detective fiction continues to evolve, experimenting with form while honoring the conventions.
