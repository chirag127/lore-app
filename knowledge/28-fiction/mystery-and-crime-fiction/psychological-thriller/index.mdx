The psychological thriller shifts focus from external detection to internal tension: what is real, who can be trusted, what lies beneath the surface of ordinary life? Rooted in the Gothic tradition and refined by Patricia Highsmith, Ruth Rendell, and their successors, the genre exploits the gap between appearance and reality, often through the device of the unreliable narrator.
