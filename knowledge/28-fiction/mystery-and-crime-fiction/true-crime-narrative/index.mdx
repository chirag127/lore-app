True crime occupies the border between journalism and literature, reporting real crimes with the narrative craft of a novel. Truman Capote's In Cold Blood created the genre, and it has since produced masterworks by Norman Mailer, Janet Malcolm, and Michelle McNamara. The genre raises distinctive ethical questions about the representation of real suffering for readerly pleasure.
