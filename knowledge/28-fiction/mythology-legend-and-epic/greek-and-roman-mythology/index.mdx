The myths of ancient Greece and Rome are the bedrock of Western literary culture. The Homeric epics, the tragedies of Aeschylus, Sophocles, and Euripides, the Metamorphoses of Ovid — these texts established patterns of narrative, character, and theme that continue to shape storytelling. This subcategory collects both the primary texts and the scholarly and popular retellings.
