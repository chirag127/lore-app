Myths are not merely false stories; they are stories that carry profound cultural and psychological truth. Epic poems — from Gilgamesh and the Iliad to the Mahabharata, Ramayana, and Beowulf — are the oldest sustained narratives we possess, establishing the archetypes and themes that all subsequent fiction draws on. This subcategory collects the primary texts and scholarly studies of world mythology and epic traditions.

## Sub-Subcategories

- **[Greek & Roman Mythology](greek-and-roman-mythology)** — The classical myths of Greece and Rome.
- **[Indian Epics](indian-epics)** — The Mahabharata, Ramayana, and the puranic traditions.
- **[Norse, Celtic & World Mythology](norse-celtic-and-world-mythology)** — Mythological traditions from Northern Europe and around the globe.
