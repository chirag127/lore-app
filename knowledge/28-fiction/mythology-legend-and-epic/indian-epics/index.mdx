The great epics of India — the Mahabharata (the longest epic poem ever written) and the Ramayana — are foundational texts of Indian civilization, containing history, philosophy, theology, and moral instruction within their narrative frames. This subcategory covers the primary epics, their major translations, retellings, and the scholarly tradition that interprets them.
