Philosophical fiction uses narrative to explore ideas — not as abstract arguments but as lived questions. From Dostoevsky's Underground Man and Camus's Sisyphus to Borges's labyrinths and Murdoch's moral philosophy in novel form, this tradition puts philosophy into motion. Existential fiction specifically confronts questions of freedom, responsibility, meaning, and death in a universe without inherent purpose.
