Romance fiction is the most commercially successful literary genre, and among the oldest — its core structure (obstacle, growth, resolution) traces back to the ancient Greek novel. The genre explores the full range of human intimacy: how people meet, what keeps them apart, and what brings them together. Though often dismissed by critics, the best romance fiction offers deep insight into character, emotion, and the social structures that shape personal relationships.
