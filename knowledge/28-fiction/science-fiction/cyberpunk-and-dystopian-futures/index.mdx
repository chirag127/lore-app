Cyberpunk emerged in the 1980s as a gritty, rebellious response to utopian visions of technology. William Gibson's Neuromancer defined the aesthetic: hackers, AI, megacorporations, and the fusion of human and machine. Dystopian fiction (Zamyatin, Huxley, Orwell, Atwood) shares the critical impulse, projecting present anxieties into dark futures. Together they form the genre's cautionary wing.
