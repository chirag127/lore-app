Science fiction is the literature of cognitive estrangement — it imagines worlds different from our own to reflect on what it means to be human. From the technological extrapolations of hard SF to the social critiques of cyberpunk, from space opera's galactic canvases to the philosophical questions raised by AI, this genre uses the tools of science and speculation to explore the present through the lens of the possible.

## Sub-Subcategories

- **[AI & Technology-Centered Sci-Fi](ai-and-technology-centered-scifi)** — Artificial intelligence, robotics, and technological transformation.
- **[Cyberpunk & Dystopian Futures](cyberpunk-and-dystopian-futures)** — High-tech, low-life futures and cautionary societies.
- **[Hard Science Fiction](hard-science-fiction)** — Scientifically rigorous extrapolation grounded in real physics and biology.
- **[Space Opera & Galactic Civilization](space-opera-and-galactic-civilization)** — Epic spacefaring adventures and interstellar politics.
