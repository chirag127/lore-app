The short story is not a novel cut short but a distinct art form with its own techniques, traditions, and masters. Where the novel accumulates, the short story compresses; where the novel explains, the short story implies. This subcategory covers the classic short story tradition (Chekhov, Joyce, Hemingway, Mansfield), the modern and postmodern masters (Borges, Carver, Munro, Saunders), and the contemporary flourishing of flash fiction and micro-fiction.
