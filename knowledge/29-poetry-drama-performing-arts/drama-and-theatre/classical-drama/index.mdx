---

Covers the dramatic literature of ancient Greece (Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides, Aristophanes) and the Sanskrit theatrical tradition (Kalidasa, the Natyashastra). These works established the conventions of tragedy, comedy, and dramatic theory that shaped millennia of theatre.
