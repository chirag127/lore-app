*The Song Machine* is John Seabrook's riveting investigation of the global hit-making apparatus that produces the pop songs dominating radio, streaming services, and cultural consciousness. Tracing the rise of the "track and hook" method pioneered by Swedish producers, Seabrook reveals a world where hit songs are engineered by specialized teams -- melody writers, beat makers, topliners -- each contributing a piece to the final product. The book is both a celebration of pop craftsmanship and a critical examination of its cultural implications.
