*This Is Your Brain on Music* bridges two worlds that rarely speak to each other: the concert hall and the neuroscience lab. Daniel Levitin, a neuroscientist and former record producer, draws on his dual background to explain how the brain transforms acoustic vibrations into the profound emotional and aesthetic experiences we call music. The book synthesizes decades of cognitive neuroscience research into an accessible account of pitch perception, rhythm processing, memory for music, and the neural basis of musical emotion.
