In *Emotional Design*, Don Norman revisits and revises his earlier functionalist framework. While *The Design of Everyday Things* emphasized usability above all, this book argues that beauty, pleasure, and emotion are equally critical. Norman shows that our emotional state fundamentally changes how we think — anxious people focus narrowly, while happy people think more creatively and tolerate minor frustrations.

This means attractive products literally work better for users because positive affect enhances cognitive performance. The book introduces a three-level model of emotional processing — visceral, behavioral, reflective — that explains why we form deep attachments to certain objects and feel nothing for others.
