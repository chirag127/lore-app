*Living with Complexity* challenges the dominant design mantra that simplicity is always better. Don Norman argues that we conflate two very different things: complexity (inherent in powerful systems) and complication (unnecessary difficulty caused by poor design). A professional camera is necessarily complex, and that complexity enables creative power. A poorly designed television remote that makes basic functions hard to find is merely complicated.

The real design challenge is not eliminating complexity but making it manageable through good conceptual models, sensible organization, and appropriate training. Norman extends this argument beyond products to social systems, services, and everyday life.
