Alan Cooper's *The Inmates Are Running the Asylum* introduced the concept of personas and goal-directed design to the software industry. His provocative title makes the argument explicit: programmers — the "inmates" — are designing software for other programmers, creating products that are technically impressive but practically unusable for normal people.

Cooper argues that the software industry suffers from a structural problem: engineers control product decisions because they build the product, but their cognitive style (logical, systematic, detail-oriented) is profoundly different from typical users' (goal-oriented, impatient, context-driven). The solution is to put interaction designers in charge of product behavior.
