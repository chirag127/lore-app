description: "The origins of literary criticism in Plato, Aristotle, and Longinus."
---

The Western critical tradition begins with Plato's ambivalence toward poetry and Aristotle's systematic defense in the *Poetics*. Longinus introduced the concept of the sublime, expanding criticism beyond structure to the experience of elevation. These texts remain essential reference points for all subsequent literary theory.
