description: "The foundational debates on imitation, poetry, and truth in Greek philosophy."
---

Plato's critique of poetry (as mere imitation that appeals to emotion rather than reason) and Aristotle's defense (as a form of understanding that reveals universal truths) established the terms of literary criticism for two millennia. Covers the *Republic*, *Ion*, and *Poetics*.
