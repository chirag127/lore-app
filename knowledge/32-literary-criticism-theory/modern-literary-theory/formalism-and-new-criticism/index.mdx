Russian Formalism (Shklovsky, Jakobson) and Anglo-American New Criticism (Richards, Brooks, Wimsatt) shifted attention from author and context to the text itself. Covers defamiliarization, the intentional and affective fallacies, and close reading as the central critical practice.
