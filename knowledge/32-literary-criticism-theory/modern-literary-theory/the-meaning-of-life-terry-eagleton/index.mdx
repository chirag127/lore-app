*The Meaning of Life: A Very Short Introduction* (2007) is one of Terry Eagleton's most personal and accessible books. Part of Oxford University Press's "Very Short Introductions" series, the book addresses what Eagleton calls the "ultimate question" — the question that cannot be dismissed either as meaningless or as answerable by any single discipline. Drawing on philosophy, theology, literature, and personal reflection, Eagleton argues that the meaning of life is not a secret to be discovered but a quality of our engagement with the world.

The book is not a work of literary theory, but it is continuous with Eagleton's broader project: the critique of a social order that systematically undermines human flourishing. The meaning of life, for Eagleton, is found in love, friendship, solidarity, and creative work — precisely the values that capitalism subordinates to profit and competition. Written with Eagleton's characteristic blend of wit, erudition, and moral seriousness, *The Meaning of Life* is the work of a critic who has spent his career asking what makes life worth living.
