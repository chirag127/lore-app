# 01 — Mind, Behavior and Human Performance

This is the **foundational category** of BookAtlas. Every other category — finance, software, business, philosophy, health, relationships, and creativity — depends on understanding the mind. Before you can master any domain, you must first understand how you learn, think, decide, feel, relate, and grow.

## Scope

This category covers the full spectrum of human psychology and performance:

- **How we learn** — the science of skill acquisition, memory, and expertise
- **How we think** — cognitive processes, biases, heuristics, and rationality
- **How we decide** — judgment, decision-making frameworks, and mental models
- **How we change** — habit formation, behavior design, and productivity systems
- **How we relate** — social influence, attachment, emotional intelligence, and relationships
- **How we struggle** — mental health, stress, resilience, and psychiatry
- **How we thrive** — motivation, purpose, creativity, wisdom, and flourishing

## What Belongs Here

Books that explain the **mechanisms of the mind** — cognitive science, behavioral psychology, neuroscience (at the behavioral level), social psychology, personality theory, developmental psychology, evolutionary psychology, clinical psychology, and positive psychology.

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- **Pure neuroscience** (cellular/molecular level) — belongs in Body/Health
- **Philosophy of mind** (metaphysical) — belongs in Philosophy
- **Self-help without evidence** — only included books with research backing or genuine practical value
- **Domain-specific psychology** (e.g., investor psychology, consumer behavior) — belongs in their respective categories (Money, Business)

This category is organized into a **progressive path**:

| Phase | Subcategories | Purpose |

## How to Use This Category

1. Start with **Subcategory 01** (Learning Science) — it teaches you how to learn, which helps you read the rest
2. Read each subcategory **in order** — the sequence is deliberate
3. Within each subcategory, read books **in numbered order** — foundational first, specialized later
4. Skip books that don't match your current needs, but understand what you're skipping
5. Return to subcategories when you need them — this is a reference library, not a one-time reading list
