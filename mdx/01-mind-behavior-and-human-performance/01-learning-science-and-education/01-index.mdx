## Overview

Learning Science and Education is the foundational shelf for understanding **how human beings learn effectively**. It bridges cognitive psychology, neuroscience, and practical pedagogy to answer a single question: given what we know about how the brain works, what are the most effective ways to acquire, retain, and apply knowledge?

This shelf is positioned as the **first subcategory** in Mind, Behavior and Human Performance because learning is the meta-skill that underpins all others. Before you can master any domain—whether finance, philosophy, or software engineering—you must understand how learning itself works.

## What's In This Shelf

This shelf contains books that address:

- **Evidence-based learning techniques**: retrieval practice, spaced repetition, interleaving, and desirable difficulties
- **The neuroscience of memory formation**: how the brain encodes, consolidates, and retrieves information
- **Skill acquisition and mastery**: the process of moving from novice to expert in any domain
- **Reading and comprehension**: how to extract maximum value from written material
- **Mental performance optimization**: techniques to enhance focus, memory, and cognitive capacity

## What's Not In This Shelf

Books that focus on **what to learn** rather than **how to learn** belong in their respective domain shelves. Books about specific educational systems or teaching methodologies belong elsewhere. The focus here is on the universal principles of learning that apply across domains.

## Reading Order

1. **Make It Stick** — The definitive evidence-based primer on how learning actually works. Start here to understand why most study techniques are ineffective and what cognitive science recommends instead.
2. **A Mind for Numbers** — Applies the principles from Make It Stick to the specific challenge of learning math and science. Oakley's work (co-creator of the most popular online course ever) makes abstract learning science concrete.
3. **How We Learn** — A more accessible, narrative-driven tour of the same territory. Carey reads like a long-form magazine article and provides an excellent second pass at the material.
4. **Ultralearning** — Moves from theory to intensive practice. Young's self-directed projects demonstrate how aggressive, self-designed learning programs can compress years of learning into months.
5. **Peak** — The definitive work on deliberate practice. Ericsson shows that expertise is not innate but developed through specific, structured practice methods.
6. **The First 20 Hours** — A practical counterpoint to the 10,000-hour framing. Kaufman demonstrates how to achieve proficiency in any skill with just 20 hours of focused practice.
7. **Limitless** — A modern brain-optimization toolkit. Kwik provides practical techniques for speed reading, memory enhancement, and overcoming digital distraction.
8. **How to Read a Book** — The capstone that teaches you how to engage with the books in this system at the highest level. Adler's framework for analytical and syntopical reading is the key to getting maximum value from every other book.
