Most people study by re-reading textbooks, highlighting passages, and reviewing notes. These methods feel productive—they create fluency and familiarity—but cognitive science has shown they produce almost no long-term learning. *Make It Stick* brings together three leading cognitive psychologists to explain why our most cherished study habits are ineffective and what actually works.

The book translates decades of research on memory and learning into actionable principles. Its central message is that **effortful learning is the most durable learning**. Techniques that make studying feel harder—retrieving information from memory, spacing study sessions, mixing topics—are precisely the techniques that produce strong, lasting retention. The authors call these "desirable difficulties."

Drawing on real-world examples from pilots, surgeons, and classroom teachers, the book shows how these principles apply across domains. It challenges the dominance of learning styles, the myth of multitasking, and the widespread belief that fluency during study predicts later performance. For anyone serious about learning effectively, this is the essential starting point.
