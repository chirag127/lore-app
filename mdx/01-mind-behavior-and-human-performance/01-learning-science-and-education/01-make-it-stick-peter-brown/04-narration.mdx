## Narration

### Writing Style

The book is co-authored by a professional writer and two academic researchers, which produces a voice that is authoritative without being academic. Sentences are clear and direct. Technical concepts like "retrieval practice" and "spaced repetition" are introduced with vivid examples before being named.

### Structure

The book alternates between explaining research findings and illustrating them through case studies—pilots learning to fly, surgeons practicing techniques, classroom teachers applying the principles. This structure keeps abstract concepts grounded in real application.

### Rhetorical Techniques

The book's most powerful rhetorical device is the contrast between intuition and evidence. Each chapter typically begins with a common belief (re-reading is effective studying), then systematically dismantles it with research findings. This creates productive cognitive dissonance that motivates behavior change.

### Readability

Accessible to a general audience. The concepts are inherently interesting, and the stakes—learning more effectively—are universally relevant. Some readers may find the level of research detail excessive, but the key insights are repeated enough to survive skimming.
