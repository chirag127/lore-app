## Analysis

### Strengths

Carey is an excellent science writer who makes complex research accessible and entertaining. The narrative approach—telling the stories behind the studies—makes the material memorable. The book covers a broad range of topics including sleep, perceptual learning, and the role of emotion in memory, going beyond standard study-skill advice.

### Weaknesses

The book sometimes prioritizes narrative over precision. Some studies are described with more confidence than their methodology warrants. Readers looking for a systematic, prescriptive framework may find the book too anecdotal. It lacks the structured implementation guidance of Make It Stick.

### Context

Published in the same year as Make It Stick, this book represents the more journalistic end of the learning-science popularization spectrum. It's an excellent complement: read Make It Stick for the science and system, read How We Learn for the stories and breadth.
