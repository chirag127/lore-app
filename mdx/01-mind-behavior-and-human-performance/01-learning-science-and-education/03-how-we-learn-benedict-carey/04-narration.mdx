## Narration

### Writing Style

Carey writes with the voice of an experienced journalist—engaging, clear, and slightly playful. He opens chapters with vivid scenes and personal anecdotes before diving into the research. The prose is accessible to any reader with high school science background.

### Structure

The book is organized thematically rather than prescriptively: chapters on forgetting, testing, spacing, sleep, and perception. Each chapter tells the story of the research and its implications. The lack of a rigid framework makes the book feel exploratory rather than instructional.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Carey excels at the "revelation" structure: presenting a common belief, then revealing the surprising research that contradicts it. He uses metaphor effectively—comparing memory to a muscle that strengthens through use, or forgetting to a filter that clarifies what matters.

### Readability

Very high. This is the most accessible book on learning science for a general audience. It can be read in a weekend and the key insights are memorable. Perfect for readers who find Make It Stick too dense.
