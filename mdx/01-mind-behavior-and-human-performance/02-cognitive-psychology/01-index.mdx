## Overview

Cognitive Psychology explores the fundamental architecture of the human mind. It addresses how we perceive the world, how attention filters the flood of sensory input, how memory encodes and reconstructs the past, and how unconscious processes shape conscious experience. This shelf is the **basic science** of the mind—understanding its operating principles before we attempt to improve, optimize, or override them.

It sits second in the category because learning science assumes a basic understanding of how the mind works. Before you can learn effectively or make better decisions, you need to understand the cognitive machinery you're working with.

## What's In This Shelf

- **Dual-process theory**: System 1 (fast, intuitive) and System 2 (slow, analytical) thinking
- **Attention and perception**: how we filter reality and what we miss
- **Memory systems**: how memories are formed, stored, and reconstructed
- **The unconscious mind**: neural processes that operate below awareness
- **Cognitive neuroscience**: the brain basis of mental processes
- **Clinical case studies**: neurological conditions that illuminate normal function

## What's Not In This Shelf

Books focused primarily on cognitive **biases** and **judgment errors** belong in Decision Making and Judgment (subcategory 03). Books about **neuroscience** as a biological science (neuroanatomy, neurochemistry) belong in the Body and Life Sciences category. The focus here is on cognitive processes as psychological phenomena.

## Reading Order

1. **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — The essential overview of the mind's two-system architecture. Kahneman's masterwork introduces the fundamental framework for understanding judgment, decision-making, and the hidden forces shaping thought.
2. **The Invisible Gorilla** — A vivid demonstration of the limits of attention and awareness. Chabris and Simons show how much of the world we miss and why we're confident we see everything.
3. **The Organized Mind** — Applies cognitive science to the practical challenge of information management. Levitin shows how understanding attention and memory constraints can help us navigate information overload.
4. **Incognito** — A tour of the unconscious brain. Eagleman reveals how much of our mental life happens below the radar of awareness.
5. **The Undoing Project** — The human story behind the science. Lewis's narrative biography of Kahneman and Tversky illuminates how the modern understanding of cognitive biases came to be.
6. **The Mind's Eye** — Oliver Sacks explores the extraordinary plasticity of visual perception through unforgettable clinical cases.
7. **Phantoms in the Brain** — Ramachandran uses bizarre neurological conditions to probe the deepest questions about consciousness and the self.

## Contribution to the Knowledge Tree

Cognitive psychology provides the **foundational understanding** that every other shelf in this category builds upon. Behavior change requires understanding automaticity and habit loops. Decision making requires understanding biases and heuristics. Productivity requires understanding attention and cognitive load. Without this foundation, the applied shelves lack context and depth.
