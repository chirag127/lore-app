You've probably heard of the famous experiment where a person in a gorilla suit walks through a basketball game and half the viewers don't see it. That experiment was conducted by Christopher Chabris and Daniel Simons, and this book is the full exploration of what it means for how we perceive the world.

The book identifies six "illusions" that distort our experience: the illusions of attention, memory, confidence, knowledge, cause, and potential. Each illusion reveals a gap between how we think our minds work and how they actually work. The famous gorilla experiment demonstrates the illusion of attention: we see far less of the world than we think we do, and we're startlingly unaware of what we're missing.
