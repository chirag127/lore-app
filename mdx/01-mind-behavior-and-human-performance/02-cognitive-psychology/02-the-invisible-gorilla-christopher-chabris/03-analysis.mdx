## Analysis

### Strengths

The book's great strength is its vivid demonstration of how wrong our intuitions about our own minds can be. The six-illusions framework is clear and memorable. The research is rigorous and well-explained. The gorilla experiment has become one of the most famous in psychology for good reason.

### Weaknesses

The book can feel repetitive, hammering the same point across multiple chapters. Some readers find the tone dismissive of alternative views. The practical implications section is weaker than the research presentation.

### Reception

Well-received by both academic and popular audiences. The gorilla experiment papers are among the most cited in psychology. The book was a New York Times bestseller.
