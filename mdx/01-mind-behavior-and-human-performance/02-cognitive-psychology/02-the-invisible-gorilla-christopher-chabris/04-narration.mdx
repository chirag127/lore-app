## Narration

### Writing Style

Chabris and Simons write in a clear, engaging style suitable for a general audience. They explain complex experiments with vivid prose and memorable examples. The book manages to be both scientifically rigorous and genuinely entertaining.

### Structure

Each chapter addresses one illusion, opening with a striking demonstration or case study before diving into the research. The gorilla experiment appears early as an anchor for the rest of the book.

### Rhetorical Techniques

The authors use surprise effectively—each chapter begins by revealing the gap between intuition and reality. They employ irony (our confidence in flawed perceptions) to keep readers engaged.

### Readability

High. Accessible to any reader with basic scientific literacy. The vivid examples and demonstrations make the concepts stick.
