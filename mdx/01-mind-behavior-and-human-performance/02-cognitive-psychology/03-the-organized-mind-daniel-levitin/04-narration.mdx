## Narration

### Writing Style

Levitin writes with the clarity of an experienced science communicator. He explains complex neuroscience concepts in accessible language without dumbing them down. The tone is warm and mildly humorous.

### Structure

The book opens with the neuroscience of attention and memory, then moves through applications: organizing physical spaces, time, social networks, and decision-making. The later sections cover specialized topics like medical decision-making and financial organization.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Levitin uses metaphor effectively—comparing attention to a spotlight, memory to a filing system. He frequently grounds abstract concepts in concrete examples from everyday life.

### Readability

Good. The neuroscience sections require some concentration but are well-explained. The practical sections are accessible to any reader. The book works best when read in chunks rather than all at once.
