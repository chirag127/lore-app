## Narration

### Writing Style

Eagleman writes with clarity, energy, and a sense of wonder. He has a gift for making cutting-edge neuroscience feel both accessible and profound. The prose is vivid and occasionally poetic.

### Structure

The book is organized thematically: the unconscious brain, neural competition, perception, social cognition, and the implications for law and responsibility. Each chapter opens with a striking case study or thought experiment.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Eagleman uses the "revelation" structure effectively—each chapter reveals something surprising about how the brain works that challenges common assumptions. He employs thought experiments and "what if" scenarios to explore philosophical implications.

### Readability

High. The concepts are intrinsically fascinating and Eagleman explains them clearly. Some readers may find the philosophical discussions challenging, but the scientific content is accessible to all.
