## Analysis

### Strengths

Lewis is a phenomenal storyteller, and the human drama of the Kahneman-Tversky partnership is inherently compelling. The book makes the science accessible without oversimplifying. It provides context for Thinking, Fast and Slow that deepens understanding of the material.

### Weaknesses

The book is light on the actual science—readers who want to learn about heuristics and biases should read Kahneman directly. The focus on biography and relationship sometimes overwhelms the intellectual content. Some critics note that Lewis soft-pedals the more controversial aspects of the collaboration.

### Reception

Critically acclaimed and commercially successful. Nominated for the National Book Critics Circle Award. Some psychologists and historians of science noted inaccuracies in the portrayal of certain events.
