## Narration

### Writing Style

Pure Michael Lewis: vivid scenes, memorable characters, and a narrative drive that makes a book about academic psychologists feel like a thriller. Lewis has an extraordinary ability to make complex ideas feel alive through the people who created them.

### Structure

The book alternates between the story of Kahneman and Tversky's collaboration and the scientific ideas they developed. The narrative structure is chronological but with frequent flashbacks that provide context.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Lewis uses character development as his primary tool. Kahneman and Tversky are drawn with such vivid detail that their disagreements, triumphs, and eventual estrangement feel deeply personal. The science emerges naturally from the story of the friendship.

### Readability

Very high. This is one of the most accessible books on the list. It reads like a novel. If you find Thinking, Fast and Slow too dense, start here.
