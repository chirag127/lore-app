In *The Mind's Eye*, Oliver Sacks explores the extraordinary world of visual perception and the brain's capacity to adapt when vision fails. Drawing on his characteristic mix of clinical case studies and personal experience, Sacks examines how we see, how we represent the world internally, and what happens when these systems break down.

The book covers a remarkable range of conditions: a musician who loses the ability to read music (and then text), a woman who can no longer navigate three-dimensional space, people who cannot recognize faces (prosopagnosia), and Sacks's own experience with a face-recognition deficit and a cancer that threatened his vision. Throughout, Sacks reveals the brain's extraordinary plasticity and the many creative ways it compensates for loss.
