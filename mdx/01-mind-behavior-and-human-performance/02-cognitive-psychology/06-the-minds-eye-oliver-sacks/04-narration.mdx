## Narration

### Writing Style

Sacks's prose is distinctive—scientifically precise yet deeply humane. He writes with the eye of a novelist and the rigor of a neurologist. His descriptions of patients are never clinical or detached but always respectful and deeply curious.

### Structure

The book is organized as a series of essays, each focusing on a different aspect of visual perception. Sacks's own experience provides a personal thread that connects the cases.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Sacks uses narrative case studies as his primary form. Each case is a story with a beginning, middle, and end. He lets the remarkable details of each case carry the scientific point, trusting readers to draw their own conclusions.

### Readability

High. Sacks is one of the most readable science writers ever. His books are enjoyed by readers with no scientific background. This is one of his more accessible works.
