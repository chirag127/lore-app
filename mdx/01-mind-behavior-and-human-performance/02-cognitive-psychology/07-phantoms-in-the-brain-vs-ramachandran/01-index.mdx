V. S. Ramachandran is one of the most creative neuroscientists of our time, known for designing elegant, low-cost experiments that reveal profound truths about the brain. In *Phantoms in the Brain*, he takes readers through a gallery of neurological oddities: phantom limbs that feel paralyzed, people who deny their own paralysis, patients who believe their mother is an impostor, and individuals who experience touch synesthesia.

Each case becomes a window into how the brain constructs our experience of reality, our sense of self, and our connections to others. Ramachandran's approach—using simple experiments to answer deep questions about the mind—makes this one of the most exciting books in neuroscience.
