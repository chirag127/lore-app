## Analysis

### Strengths

Ramachandran's creativity in experimental design is extraordinary. He shows that neuroscience doesn't require expensive equipment—just creative thinking. The case studies are fascinating and memorable. The mirror-box therapy for phantom limb pain has become a standard clinical intervention.

### Weaknesses

Some of Ramachandran's speculations—particularly about mirror neurons and the evolution of language—are more speculative than the book acknowledges. His theories have been criticized for outrunning the evidence. The book is now somewhat dated (1998) given advances in neuroimaging.

### Reception

Highly praised for its creativity and accessibility. The mirror box is one of the most celebrated discoveries in modern neuroscience. Some of Ramachandran's broader claims have been controversial within the field.
