## Narration

### Writing Style

Ramachandran writes with infectious enthusiasm. His excitement about his discoveries is palpable. The book is co-authored with science writer Sandra Blakeslee, which gives it a polished narrative quality while preserving Ramachandran's distinctive voice.

### Structure

Each chapter focuses on a different neurological syndrome, opening with a case study before exploring the implications for understanding normal brain function.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Ramachandran excels at the "detective" structure: presenting a puzzling symptom, describing the investigative process, and revealing the underlying mechanism. Each chapter feels like solving a mystery.

### Readability

Good. Some of the neuroscience concepts require concentration, but the vivid case studies make the book inherently engaging. For readers new to neuroscience, this is an excellent starting point.
