## Analysis

### Strengths

The Good Judgment Project is one of the most important social science experiments of recent decades, and the results are genuinely surprising. The book translates rigorous research into practical guidance without oversimplifying. The distinction between foxes and hedgehogs is memorable and useful.

### Weaknesses

Some readers find the book too long for its core insights. The practical guidance for becoming a superforecaster could be more prescriptive. The emphasis on geopolitical forecasting makes the book less directly applicable to everyday predictions.

### Reception

Widely praised. Winner of the 2015 Best Book Award from the American Society of Association Executives. Influenced intelligence agencies, forecasting markets, and strategic planning in business.
