## Narration

### Writing Style

Tetlock (academic) and Gardner (journalist) combine authority with readability. The academic concepts are clearly explained without jargon. The narrative sections about the tournament and individual superforecasters are engaging and accessible.

### Structure

The book opens with the story of the Good Judgment Project, introduces the superforecasters, then explores the habits of mind that make them effective, and concludes with implications for organizations and society.

### Rhetorical Techniques

The central mystery structure—"Some people can predict the future, and here's how"—drives the narrative. Character portraits of individual superforecasters make the abstract concepts concrete.

### Readability

High for a book based on academic research. Accessible to any motivated reader. Key insights are clearly stated and reinforced.
