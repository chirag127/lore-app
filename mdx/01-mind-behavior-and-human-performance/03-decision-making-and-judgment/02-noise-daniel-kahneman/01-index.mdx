If you've read *Thinking, Fast and Slow*, you know about cognitive biases—the systematic errors that distort human judgment. But there's another kind of error that is just as damaging and far less recognized: noise, the unwanted variability in judgments that should be identical.

*Noise* reveals that two doctors, two judges, two underwriters, or two hiring managers faced with the same case will often reach wildly different conclusions. This inconsistency is noise, and it permeates every domain where human judgment is involved. The book provides a framework for understanding noise, measuring it, and reducing it through "decision hygiene"—without eliminating the beneficial diversity of perspectives.
