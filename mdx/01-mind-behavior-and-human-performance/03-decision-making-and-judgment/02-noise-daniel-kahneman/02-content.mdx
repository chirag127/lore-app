## Core Concepts

### Noise vs. Bias

Bias is the average error—the systematic tendency to be wrong in a particular direction. Noise is the variability in error—the scatter of judgments around the average. Both reduce accuracy, but organizations focus almost exclusively on bias while ignoring noise.

### The Noise Audit

The authors develop a method for measuring noise: present the same case to multiple judges and measure the variability in their decisions. The results are usually shocking—far more noise than anyone expected.

### Decision Hygiene

A set of practices to reduce noise without eliminating valuable discretion: using structured checklists, aggregating independent judgments, sequencing information carefully, and resisting the urge to make intuitive judgments.

### The Paradox of Judgment

Noise exists because people are different—they have different experiences, personalities, and cognitive styles. But in many contexts, we want consistency. The book explores this tension and how to manage it.

## Key Domains Affected by Noise

- Medicine: different doctors give different diagnoses for the same patient
- Law: different judges give different sentences for the same crime
- Hiring: different interviewers evaluate the same candidate differently
- Business: different executives make different forecasts for the same market
