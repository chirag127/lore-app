## Analysis

### Strengths

The concept of noise is genuinely important and underappreciated. The noise audit methodology is practical and powerful. The book provides a comprehensive framework that complements rather than duplicates the bias-focused literature. The collaboration between Kahneman (cognitive psychologist), Sibony (business strategist), and Sunstein (legal scholar) produces rich, multidisciplinary analysis.

### Weaknesses

The book is long—some readers find it could be half the length. The emphasis on organizational decision-making means less practical advice for individuals. The solutions (decision hygiene) are less developed than the problem diagnosis.

### Reception

Critically acclaimed and commercially successful. Widely discussed in legal, medical, and business communities. Some critics argued the book overstates the prevalence of noise and understates the difficulty of reducing it.
