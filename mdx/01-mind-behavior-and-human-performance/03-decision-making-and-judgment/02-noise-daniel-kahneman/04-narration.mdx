## Narration

### Writing Style

Kahneman's characteristic clarity and precision, enriched by Sibony's business perspective and Sunstein's legal examples. The writing is dense but clear—every sentence carries weight. This is a serious book that demands attention.

### Structure

Organized in six parts: introducing noise, measuring noise, the psychology of noise, how noise happens in specific domains, reducing noise, and a concluding synthesis. The structure is logical and cumulative.

### Rhetorical Techniques

The authors use the "seeing the invisible" structure—making visible a problem that was always there but unnoticed. The noise audit examples are designed to surprise and alarm readers, creating motivation for change.

### Readability

Moderate. This is a challenging read that requires concentration. The concepts are clear but the book is long and detailed. Best read in sections rather than all at once.
