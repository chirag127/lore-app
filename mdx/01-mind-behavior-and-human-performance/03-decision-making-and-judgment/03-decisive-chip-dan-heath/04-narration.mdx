## Writing Style & Voice

The Heath brothers write in a collaborative, accessible style that disguises the contributions of two authors. The voice is consistent: curious, enthusiastic, and relentlessly practical. Each chapter opens with a compelling story, develops a conceptual insight, and closes with specific takeaways.

## Narrative Structure

The book follows a clear four-part structure — one part per villain and countermeasure. Within each part, chapters alternate between story and analysis. The pacing is brisk; chapters rarely exceed 20 pages.

## Rhetorical Techniques

The brothers rely heavily on contrast: before/after, villain/countermeasure, intuitive/effective. The WRAP acronym itself is a mnemonic device that makes the framework sticky. They use research findings as authority anchors but always translate them into concrete recommendations.

## Readability

The writing targets a business audience but is accessible to general readers. Reading level is approximately 9th grade. Technical terms are introduced with clear definitions and vivid examples. The book includes "try this" sidebars and summary boxes throughout.

## Comparative Context

*Decisive* is the Heath brothers' most structurally ambitious book. It is less emotional than *Switch* (which focused on change) and less communication-focused than *Made to Stick*. It represents their most complete engagement with academic research, with endnotes comprising nearly 40 pages.
