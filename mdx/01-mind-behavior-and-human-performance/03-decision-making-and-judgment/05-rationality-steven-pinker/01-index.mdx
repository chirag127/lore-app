Steven Pinker, one of our most influential public intellectuals, takes on the topic of rationality: what it is, why it seems scarce, and why it matters more than ever. The book is both a defense of reason against its modern critics and a practical guide to the tools of rational thought.

Pinker argues that rationality is not a natural human endowment but a set of cognitive tools developed over centuries—from formal logic to probability theory to critical thinking. These tools are not intuitive; they must be learned and practiced. The book provides a tour of these tools, explaining how they work and why they're essential for making good decisions, understanding the world, and navigating the complexities of modern life.
