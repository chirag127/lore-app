## Core Concepts

### The Rationality Toolkit

Pinker identifies several tools of rationality: logic (deductive reasoning), probability (Bayesian reasoning), critical thinking (evaluating claims and evidence), and decision theory (making choices under uncertainty). Each tool is explained with historical context and practical examples.

### Logic and Fallacies

The book covers the basics of formal logic—syllogisms, modus ponens, modus tollens—and common fallacies like affirming the consequent and denying the antecedent. Pinker shows why understanding logical structure matters for clear thinking.

### Bayes' Rule

Pinker provides an accessible introduction to Bayesian reasoning: how to update beliefs in proportion to the strength of evidence. He argues that Bayesian thinking is the foundation of scientific reasoning and rational belief formation.

### The Rationality of Groups

Individual rationality does not automatically scale to groups. Pinker explores collective rationality, the tragedy of the commons, and how institutions can align individual incentives with collective welfare.

### Why Irrationality Persists

Pinker acknowledges that people often seem irrational. He argues that much apparent irrationality is actually rationality applied to the wrong context—social reasoning is different from scientific reasoning. The book also addresses motivated reasoning and tribalism.
