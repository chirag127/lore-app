## Narration

### Writing Style

Classic Pinker: erudite, clear, slightly combative, and occasionally funny. He writes with authority and confidence. The prose is dense with ideas but never obscure. Pinker assumes an intelligent reader willing to engage with challenging material.

### Structure

The book moves from definition (what is rationality?), through tools (logic, probability, critical thinking), to applications (rationality in science, politics, and everyday life), and finally to the defense of reason against its critics.

### Rhetorical Techniques

Pinker uses the "common sense vs. science" structure: showing that intuitions about reasoning are often wrong and that formal tools yield better results. He frequently illustrates concepts with examples from history, science, and current events.

### Readability

Moderate. This is a demanding book that rewards careful reading. The logic and probability sections require attention, but Pinker's explanations are as clear as any available.
