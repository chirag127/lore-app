## Overview

Behavior Change and Habits addresses the science of why we do what we do—and how to change it. Drawing on behavioral psychology, neuroscience, and practical behavior design, this shelf explores the mechanisms of automaticity, the structure of habit loops, and the environmental and cognitive factors that shape our daily actions.

It sits fourth in the category, leveraging understanding from cognitive psychology (subcategory 02) and decision science (subcategory 03) to tackle the applied challenge of behavior change. Knowing how the mind works and how to make good decisions is incomplete without the ability to translate that knowledge into consistent action.

## What's In This Shelf

- **Habit formation theory**: the cue-routine-reward loop and its variations
- **Behavior design**: how to engineer environments that make good habits easy
- **Motivation and willpower**: the limits of self-control and alternatives to it
- **Change management**: frameworks for personal and organizational transformation
- **Distraction and focus**: overcoming the forces that pull us away from intended behaviors

## What's Not In This Shelf

Books about **productivity systems** (task management, workflow design) belong in Productivity and Execution (subcategory 05). Books about **motivation** as a general psychological concept may belong in the Motivation and Purpose shelf. The focus here is specifically on the mechanics of behavior change and habit formation.

## Reading Order

1. **Atomic Habits** — The most practical and complete modern guide to habit formation. Clear's four laws of behavior change provide a comprehensive framework for building good habits and breaking bad ones.
2. **Tiny Habits** — Fogg's behavior design approach offers a complementary, even more granular method. Start here if you struggle with the discipline required by Atomic Habits.
3. **The Power of Habit** — The foundational popular work on habit science. Duhigg's reporting provides the context and evidence that underpins the practical books.
4. **Mini Habits** — The simplest possible approach: make habits so small they can't fail. Guise's method is an elegant solution to the willpower problem.
5. **Switch** — The Heath brothers apply their signature framework to the challenge of change, showing how to mobilize the Rider (rational mind), motivate the Elephant (emotion), and shape the Path (environment).
6. **Indistractable** — A focused treatment of the specific challenge of distraction. Eyal provides a four-step model for managing attention in a world designed to fragment it.
