*Mini Habits* offers the simplest possible approach to habit formation: make your habits so small they require almost no willpower to execute. One push-up. One page of reading. One minute of meditation. The idea is that starting is the hardest part—once you begin, you almost always do more. But by reducing the barrier to entry to near zero, you eliminate the willpower problem entirely.

Guise developed this system after his own failed attempts at building habits. He noticed that forcing himself to do just one push-up per day was sustainable in a way that thirty push-ups never was. From that single insight, he built a complete philosophy of behavior change centered on "stupid small" commitments and the power of consistent, tiny actions.
