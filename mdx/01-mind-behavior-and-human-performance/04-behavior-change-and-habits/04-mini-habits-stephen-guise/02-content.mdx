## Core Concepts

### The Mini Habit Principle

A mini habit is a habit reduced to its minimum viable version: one push-up, one paragraph, one minute of focus. The key insight is that the barrier to starting must be so low that you can't rationalize skipping it. Once you start, you'll often continue—but even if you don't, you've still succeeded.

### Willpower vs. Motivation

Guise argues that motivation is unreliable (it fluctuates with mood, energy, and circumstances). Willpower is more reliable but limited. Mini habits bypass both by eliminating the need for significant willpower or motivation.

### The One-Push Rule

Do one repetition of your habit, then you're allowed to stop. This removes the pressure of meeting a quota while preserving the option to do more. The psychological effect is powerful: the smallest possible action counts as success.

### Cognitive Restructuring

By consistently meeting mini habit goals, you rewire your self-perception. You begin to see yourself as someone who exercises, reads, or writes daily. This identity shift is more important than any single habit.

## Practical Applications

- Choose one or two mini habits to start
- Define the "stupid small" version (one push-up, one sentence, one minute)
- Schedule a regular time to execute
- Track your streaks
- Celebrate every success, no matter how small
