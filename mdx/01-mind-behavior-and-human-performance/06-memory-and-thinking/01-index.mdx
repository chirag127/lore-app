## Overview

This subcategory bridges two deeply connected faculties: **memory** (how we encode, store, and retrieve information) and **thinking** (how we reason, decide, and reflect on our own cognitive processes). Memory is not just a repository — it is the substrate of all thinking. How we remember shapes how we think, and how we think shapes what we remember.

## Why This Subcategory Exists

Memory and thinking are the foundational cognitive skills that underpin every other subcategory. Before you can learn effectively, build habits, or make good decisions, you need to understand how your mind encodes, stores, and retrieves information — and how it can trick you. This shelf combines two literatures that are often separated: the ancient art of memory (mnemonics, memory palaces) and the modern science of judgment and decision-making.

## Reading Order

| Order | Book | Why |
|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | *Moonwalking with Einstein* — Joshua Foer | The most engaging introduction to memory techniques. Read this first to get motivated and see what's possible. |
| 2 | *Remember It!* — Nelson Dellis | A practical, step-by-step guide to the same techniques. Read this second to actually build the skills. |
| 3 | *Unlimited Memory* — Kevin Horsley | A complementary perspective focused on speed and efficiency. Read third for additional techniques. |
| 4 | *Thinking in Bets* — Annie Duke | Transitions from memory to thinking. Uses poker as a metaphor for probabilistic decision-making under uncertainty. |
| 5 | *The Art of Thinking Clearly* — Rolf Dobelli | A catalog of cognitive biases and mental errors. Read last as a diagnostic toolkit for thinking traps. |
