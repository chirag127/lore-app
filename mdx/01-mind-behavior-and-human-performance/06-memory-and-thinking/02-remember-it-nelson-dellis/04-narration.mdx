## Writing Style

Dellis writes in a direct, instructional voice — clear, encouraging, and free of jargon. He writes like a coach: "Here's what to do, here's why it works, now practice." The tone is warm but efficient, wasting no words on literary flourishes.

## Structure

The book is organized by use case rather than by technique, which makes it easy to navigate. Each chapter follows a consistent pattern: explain the problem, introduce the technique, provide examples, give exercises. This structure is effective for learning but can feel formulaic when read cover to cover.

## Readability

Very accessible. The reading level is approximately 8th grade. Dellis assumes no background knowledge and defines every term on first use. The book is designed for dipping in and out rather than linear reading.

## Best Use

This is a reference book, not a read-once-and-shelve book. The techniques require practice. Dellis recommends reading one chapter at a time, practicing the technique for a week, then moving on. Used this way, the book can genuinely improve your memory.
