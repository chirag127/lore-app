## Strengths

- **Concise and efficient.** At 168 pages, it respects the reader's time.
- **Focuses on fundamentals.** Concentration and visualization are genuinely the foundation of all memory techniques.
- **Practical exercises.** Each chapter ends with actionable practice routines.
- **Low barrier to entry.** Requires no background knowledge and no expensive tools.

## Weaknesses

- **Production quality.** Self-published; editing, formatting, and cover design are noticeably lower quality than the other books in this shelf.
- **Limited depth.** The brevity means some techniques are introduced but not fully developed.
- **No spaced repetition coverage.** The science of forgetting and optimal review is absent.
- **Light on applications.** Fewer real-world examples than *Remember It!*.

## Comparison

| Dimension | Remember It! | Unlimited Memory |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 224 pages | 168 pages |
| Practicality | High | Medium-high |
| Production quality | High | Low |
| Name memorization | Excellent | Brief |
| Price-value | Better | Good |

## Overall

A solid supplementary text. Read it third, after Foer (motivation) and Dellis (practical instruction), for a faster, more concise perspective on the same techniques.
