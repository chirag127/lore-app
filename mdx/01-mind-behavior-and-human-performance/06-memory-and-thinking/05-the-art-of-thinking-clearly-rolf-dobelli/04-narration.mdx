## Writing Style

Dobelli writes in a crisp, essayistic style reminiscent of Nassim Taleb — declarative, slightly arrogant, and occasionally funny. Chapters open with a provocative claim, illustrate it with an example, and close with a blunt prescription. The tone is that of a wise uncle who has seen through the illusions of everyday thinking.

## Structure

The alphabetical organization by bias name is both the book's greatest strength and greatest weakness. It makes for easy browsing but prevents any narrative arc or cumulative learning. There is no build, no climax, no resolution — just 99 independent essays.

## Readability

Very high. Each chapter is self-contained and can be read in 3-5 minutes. The reading level is approximately 10th grade. Technical terms are avoided or explained on first use.

## Best Use

Keep this book on your desk or bedside table. Read one or two entries per day. Use it as a diagnostic reference when you catch yourself making a questionable decision. The book is designed for dipping, not linear reading.
