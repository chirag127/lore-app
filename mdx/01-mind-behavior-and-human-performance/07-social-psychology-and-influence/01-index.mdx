## Overview

Social psychology is the scientific study of how people's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are shaped by the actual, imagined, or implied presence of others. This subcategory spans the full spectrum: from the micro-mechanics of persuasion (what makes someone say yes) to the macro-patterns of social cognition (how we justify our beliefs and actions to ourselves and others).

## Why This Subcategory Exists

Social psychology is the bridge between individual cognition (how one mind works) and collective behavior (how minds interact). Understanding influence is essential for every domain of human interaction — from sales and marketing to leadership, relationships, and public policy. This shelf is placed here because social influence is a behavior of the human mind in context, requiring the cognitive foundations from earlier subcategories.
