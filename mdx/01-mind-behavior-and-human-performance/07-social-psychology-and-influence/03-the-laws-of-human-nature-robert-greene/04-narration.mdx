## Writing Style

Greene writes in a dense, authoritative style reminiscent of Machiavelli and Nietzsche. Sentences are long and complex. The vocabulary is sophisticated but not inaccessible. The tone is magisterial — Greene speaks as one who has uncovered the hidden truths of human behavior.

## Structure

Each law follows a consistent template: historical case study (5-10 pages), psychological analysis (3-5 pages), practical application (3-5 pages), and "keys to human nature" exercises (1-2 pages). This structure is effective for learning but repetitive when read continuously.

## Readability

Moderate difficulty. The reading level is approximately college level. The density of case studies and analysis requires sustained attention. Not a book to skim.

## Best Use

Read one law per day rather than attempting to consume the whole book quickly. The chapter structure supports this approach well. Keep a notebook to track personal insights and observations.
