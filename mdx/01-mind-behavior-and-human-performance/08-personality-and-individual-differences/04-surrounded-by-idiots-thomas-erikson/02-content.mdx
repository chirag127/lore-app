## The Four Color Types

### Red (Dominant)

**Core drive**: Results, challenge, control.
**Strengths**: Decisive, efficient, courageous, visionary.
**Weaknesses**: Impatient, arrogant, insensitive to others' feelings.
**Communication**: Direct, brief, focused on outcomes.
**Under stress**: Becomes autocratic and demanding.
**How to communicate**: Be brief, focus on results, respect their time, do not micromanage.

### Yellow (Influential)

**Core drive**: Social connection, recognition, fun.
**Strengths**: Enthusiastic, persuasive, creative, optimistic.
**Weaknesses**: Disorganized, over-promising, poor follow-through.
**Communication**: Talkative, energetic, big-picture.
**Under stress**: Becomes dramatic and unfocused.
**How to communicate**: Be warm and friendly, acknowledge their ideas, give them social time.

### Green (Stable)

**Core drive**: Harmony, security, relationships.
**Strengths**: Patient, supportive, good listener, reliable.
**Weaknesses**: Indecisive, resistant to change, avoids conflict.
**Communication**: Gentle, consensus-seeking, non-confrontational.
**Under stress**: Becomes withdrawn and passive-aggressive.
**How to communicate**: Show genuine interest in them, explain changes in advance, give them time to adapt.

### Blue (Analytical)

**Core drive**: Accuracy, order, competence.
**Strengths**: Precise, systematic, thorough, logical.
**Weaknesses**: Perfectionistic, critical, emotionally distant.
**Communication**: Detailed, data-driven, precise.
**Under stress**: Becomes rigid and withdrawn.
**How to communicate**: Provide data, be precise, respect their need for details, do not rush them.

## Combining Types

Most people are a blend of two colors, with one dominant. Erikson provides guidance for communicating across all type combinations — e.g., a Red-Blue speaking to a Yellow-Green must slow down and add warmth.

## Applications

| Context | Key Insight |
|---|---|
| Hiring | Hire for style diversity, not similarity |
| Team building | Assign roles based on natural style strengths |
| Conflict resolution | Frame solutions in the other person's language |
| Sales | Match the customer's style for rapport |
| Leadership | Flex your style to what the situation demands |
