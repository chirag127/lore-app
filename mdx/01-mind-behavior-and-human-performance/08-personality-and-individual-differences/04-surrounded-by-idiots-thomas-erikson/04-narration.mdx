## Writing Style

Erikson writes in a conversational, humorous style with a self-deprecating tone. He opens by admitting that he himself is a "difficult" personality type (Red) and uses his own communication failures as examples. The writing is clear and engaging but occasionally reads like a corporate training manual.

## Structure

The book is organized by color type — one section per type, each covering strengths, weaknesses, communication preferences, and stress responses. The final section covers combinations and practical applications. The structure is clear but repetitive.

## Readability

Very easy. Reading level is approximately 8th grade. Short chapters, bullet points, and summary boxes make this a quick read.

## Best Use

Use this as a reference for workplace communication. The framework is easy to remember and apply in meetings, negotiations, and difficult conversations. Read it as the last book in this subcategory for a practical (if imperfect) toolkit.
