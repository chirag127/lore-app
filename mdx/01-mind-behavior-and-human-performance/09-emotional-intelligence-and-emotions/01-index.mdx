## Overview

Emotions are not the enemy of rational thought — they are a parallel information system that evolved to guide behavior. This subcategory covers the science and practice of emotional intelligence: what emotions are, how they work, how to recognize and regulate them, and how emotional skills contribute to success in relationships, work, and life satisfaction. It spans from Goleman's foundational EQ framework to more modern approaches like emotional agility and acceptance-based models.

## Why This Subcategory Exists

Emotional intelligence is the skill that connects cognition to action. Knowing what you know (cognitive intelligence) is useless if you cannot manage the emotional states that derail good decisions, damage relationships, and undermine well-being. This subcategory provides the theoretical framework (Goleman), practical skills (David, Brackett), and a map of the emotional landscape (Brown). It sits between Personality (stable traits) and Relationships (applied social skills) as the bridge between individual psychology and interpersonal effectiveness.
