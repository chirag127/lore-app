## Strengths

- **Strong evidence base.** David is a practicing psychologist and researcher. The framework is grounded in ACT, CBT, and emotion regulation research.
- **Nuanced.** Unlike many self-help books, David acknowledges that emotions are complex and there are no simple solutions.
- **Practical.** The four-step framework is easy to remember and apply.
- **Well-written.** David's prose is clear, warm, and occasionally elegant.

## Weaknesses

- **Repetitive.** The core concept is introduced early; later chapters sometimes rephrase rather than deepen.
- **Light on neuroscience.** Readers wanting the brain science behind emotions should look elsewhere.
- **Individual focus.** The book focuses on personal emotional agility without much attention to social or systemic factors.

## Comparison with Emotional Intelligence (Goleman)

| Dimension | Emotional Intelligence | Emotional Agility |
|---|---|---|
| Year | 1995 | 2016 |
| Framework | Five domains of EQ | Four-step process |
| Evidence base | Mixed (pop science) | Strong (ACT/CBT) |
| Focus | What EQ is | How to develop it |
| Best for | Understanding the concept | Practical skill building |

## Overall

*Emotional Agility* is the best book on *applied* emotional intelligence. Read it immediately after Goleman for the practical framework that Goleman's book lacks.
