## Key Emotional Distinctions

### Empathy vs. Sympathy
- **Empathy**: Feeling *with* someone. "I know something of what you are feeling." Requires vulnerability.
- **Sympathy**: Feeling *for* someone. "I feel sorry for you." Creates distance.
- The research confirms: empathy connects; sympathy isolates.

### Shame vs. Guilt vs. Humiliation
- **Shame**: "I am bad." Focus on self. Corrosive to self-worth.
- **Guilt**: "I did something bad." Focus on behavior. Can motivate repair.
- **Humiliation**: "Something bad was done to me." The person feels undeserving of the treatment.

### Envy vs. Jealousy
- **Envy**: "I want what you have." Two-person dynamic.
- **Jealousy**: "I fear losing what we have." Three-person dynamic.

### Compassion vs. Pity
- **Compassion**: Acknowledging suffering with a desire to help. Equal relationship.
- **Pity**: Acknowledging suffering from a position of superiority. Unequal relationship.

### Disappointment vs. Regret
- **Disappointment**: About outcomes you cannot control.
- **Regret**: About choices you made. Requires self-compassion to process productively.

## Emotional Categories in Detail

| Category | Key Emotions | Brown's Insight |
|---|---|---|
| Uncertainty | Anxiety, fear, vulnerability | Anxiety is anticipation of threat; fear is in the moment |
| Comparison | Envy, jealousy, schadenfreude, resentment | Comparison is the thief of joy |
| Hurt | Shame, guilt, humiliation, embarrassment | Shame is the most powerful master emotion |
| Connection | Belonging, fitting in, acceptance | Belonging requires being seen; fitting in requires hiding |
| Openness | Curiosity, surprise, wonder | Curiosity is the antidote to judgment |
| Difficulty | Grief, heartbreak, pain, despair | Grief is not just about death — it is about any loss |
