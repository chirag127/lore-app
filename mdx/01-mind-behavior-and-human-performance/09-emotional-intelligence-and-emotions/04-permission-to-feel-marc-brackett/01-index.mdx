## Overview

*Permission to Feel* is the most practical book on developing emotional skills. Marc Brackett, the founding director of the Yale Center for Emotional Intelligence, has spent 25 years researching how emotions affect learning, decision-making, and well-being. The result is the RULER system, now used in over 3,000 schools worldwide.

The book's central insight is simple but profound: emotional intelligence is not about controlling or suppressing emotions — it is about giving yourself and others permission to feel, and then developing the skills to work with those feelings effectively.

## Executive Summary

**The RULER Skills:**
- **R**ecognize emotions in yourself and others
- **U**nderstand the causes and consequences of emotions
- **L**abel emotions with precise vocabulary
- **E**xpress emotions appropriately in different contexts
- **R**egulate emotions using effective strategies

**The Mood Meter**: A tool for building emotional awareness — plotting emotions on axes of pleasantness and energy.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Emotions are not optional.** They influence every decision, relationship, and learning moment.

2. **Labeling emotions reduces their intensity.** The RULER skill of precise labeling is a powerful regulation tool.

3. **There are no bad emotions.** All emotions are information. What matters is how you respond.

4. **Emotional skills must be taught.** They are not automatically developed through life experience.
