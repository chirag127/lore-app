## Writing Style

Brackett writes in a warm, personal style. He opens with his own childhood story — growing up as a sensitive, emotionally intense kid in a family that did not talk about feelings — which establishes his personal stake in the work. The prose is clear and engaging, less academic than one might expect from a Yale professor.

## Structure

The book is organized around the RULER framework, with one section per skill. Each chapter combines research, personal story, and practical exercises. The Mood Meter chapter is the highlight.

## Readability

Very easy. Reading level is approximately 9th grade. Brackett explains all terms and avoids jargon.

## Best Use

Use this as the practical handbook for the EQ subcategory. Implement the Mood Meter in your daily life and practice the RULER skills one at a time.
