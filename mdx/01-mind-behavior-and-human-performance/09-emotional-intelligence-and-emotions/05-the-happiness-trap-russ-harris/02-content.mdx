## Part 1: The Happiness Trap

Harris exposes six myths of happiness:
1. **Happiness is the natural state** — No, humans evolved negative emotions as survival mechanisms.
2. **If you are not happy, you are defective** — The multi-billion-dollar self-help industry profits from this belief.
3. **To live a better life, get rid of negative emotions** — The more you avoid, the more you suffer.
4. **You can control your thoughts and feelings** — Research shows the opposite: suppression amplifies.
5. **Positive thinking works** — Positive affirmations often make people feel *worse*.
6. **You should be able to think your way out of problems** — Some problems cannot be solved by thinking; they must be lived through.

Harris introduces the "control agenda" — the belief that we can and should control our inner experience. This agenda is the root of the happiness trap.

## Part 2: Transforming Your Inner World

**Mindfulness**: Present-moment awareness without judgment. Not a relaxation technique — a clarity technique.

**Acceptance**: Making room for difficult feelings. Instead of fighting anxiety, say "I notice anxiety is here."

**Defusion**: Separating thoughts from the reality they describe. Instead of "I am a failure," notice "I am having the thought that I am a failure."

**The Observing Self**: The part of you that can notice thoughts and feelings without being defined by them.

## Part 3: Creating a Life Worth Living

**Values clarification**: What do you want your life to stand for? Harris distinguishes values (ongoing directions) from goals (achievable outcomes).

**Committed action**: Action guided by values, not feelings. Harris uses the "sweet spot" metaphor: the intersection of values, strengths, and what matters.

## Key ACT Techniques

| Technique | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Expansion | Making room for emotions | "I can feel this and still take action" |
| Defusion | Creating distance from thoughts | "I notice I am thinking that..." |
| Connection | Staying present | Grounding in the five senses |
| Values clarification | Finding direction | Identifying what matters most |
| Committed action | Taking steps | A small action aligned with values |
