## Strengths

- **Evidence-based.** ACT is one of the most well-researched therapeutic approaches, with hundreds of clinical trials.
- **Extremely accessible.** Harris translates complex therapy concepts into simple, memorable language.
- **Practical exercises.** Every chapter includes specific practices to try.
- **Counterintuitive and thought-provoking.** The argument that happiness-seeking is self-defeating is genuinely paradigm-shifting.

## Weaknesses

- **Dense in spots.** Some ACT concepts (self-as-context, fusion vs. defusion) take time to grasp.
- **Repetitive structure.** Each chapter follows the same pattern: concept, exercise, example.
- **Not for severe mental health issues.** Harris cautions that the book is not a replacement for therapy.
- **Self-help trappings.** Despite the anti-positive-thinking message, the book uses many standard self-help conventions.

## Overall

Read this last in the EQ subcategory. It integrates the key insights from all the other books: emotional awareness (Goleman), acceptance (David), vocabulary (Brown), and practical skills (Brackett). ACT ties everything together.
