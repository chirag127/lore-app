## Writing Style

Harris writes in a clear, direct, and compassionate style. He uses simple language, plenty of examples, and a touch of humor. The tone is that of a warm, experienced therapist — non-judgmental, patient, and encouraging.

## Structure

The book follows a logical arc: diagnose the problem (the happiness trap), introduce the solution (ACT), teach the skills (mindfulness, acceptance, defusion), and apply to life (values, committed action). Each chapter is 10-15 pages with clear exercises.

## Readability

Very accessible. Reading level is approximately 8th grade. Harris explains all concepts with everyday examples.

## Best Use

Read this last in the EQ subcategory. Use the ACT framework as your daily operating system for handling difficult emotions and making values-aligned choices.
