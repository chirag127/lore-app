## Overview

*Attached* is the most accessible and practical introduction to attachment theory for general readers. Attachment theory, developed by John Bowlby and Mary Ainsworth, is one of the most extensively researched frameworks in psychology. It explains how the bonds we form with our earliest caregivers shape our expectations, behaviors, and patterns in adult romantic relationships.

Levine and Heller identify three primary attachment styles: Anxious (craving closeness, fearing abandonment), Avoidant (valuing independence, discomfort with closeness), and Secure (comfortable with intimacy and independence). About 50% of adults are Secure, 20% Anxious, 25% Avoidant, and 5% a combination (Anxious-Avoidant).

## Executive Summary

**Part 1: The Attachment System** — What attachment theory is and how it explains adult relationships.

**Part 2: The Three Styles** — Detailed profiles of Anxious, Avoidant, and Secure attachment.

**Part 3: Finding Love** — How to identify attachment styles in potential partners.

**Part 4: Building Security** — How to move toward secure attachment, regardless of your starting style.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Attachment is not a choice — it is a biological system.** Your attachment style is not a character flaw.

2. **The Anxious-Avoidant trap is the most common relationship disaster.** Anxious and Avoidant types are drawn to each other — and drive each other crazy.

3. **Secure attachment is learnable.** With awareness and effort, even Anxious and Avoidant people can develop more secure patterns.

4. **Dependency is not a weakness.** In attachment theory, the ability to depend on a partner is a sign of health.
