## Strengths

- **Science-based.** Attachment theory is one of the most researched frameworks in psychology.
- **Practical.** The book provides concrete guidance for different attachment styles.
- **Validating.** Many readers find their lifelong relationship patterns explained for the first time.
- **The Anxious-Avoidant trap concept is transformative.** This insight alone can save years of therapy.

## Weaknesses

- **Over-simplifies.** Three categories cannot capture the full complexity of adult attachment.
- **Deterministic tone.** Some readers feel labeled rather than liberated.
- **Heteronormative focus.** Most examples assume traditional romantic relationships.

## Comparison with Other Relationship Books

| Dimension | Attached | Gottman's Principles |
|---|---|---|
| Framework | Attachment theory | Observational research |
| Focus | Why you pick certain partners | How to make relationships work |
| Best for | Self-understanding | Skill-building |
| Blame | Attachment patterns | Behaviors (not personality) |

## Overall

Read this first in the relationship shelf. It provides the foundational framework — attachment theory — that explains why we choose the partners we do and why relationships follow predictable patterns.
