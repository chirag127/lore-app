## Overview

*The Seven Principles for Making Marriage Work* is the most research-grounded relationship book ever written. John Gottman's pioneering research — which involved observing thousands of couples in a "love lab" apartment and tracking their relationship outcomes for years — identified the specific behaviors that predict relationship success or failure.

What makes Gottman's work unique: he does not ask couples what they think about their relationship. He observes them. He codes their interactions frame by frame. He measures physiological responses. The result is an evidence-based understanding of what actually works — not what people say works.

## Executive Summary

**Principle 1: Enhance Your Love Maps** — Know your partner's inner world: their stresses, hopes, joys, and fears.

**Principle 2: Nurture Your Fondness and Admiration** — Actively maintain a culture of appreciation and respect.

**Principle 3: Turn Toward Each Other Instead of Away** — Respond to bids for connection.

**Principle 4: Let Your Partner Influence You** — Accept influence, especially if you are a man.

**Principle 5: Solve Your Solvable Problems** — Use the Six-Step process for resolvable conflicts.

**Principle 6: Overcome Gridlock** — Perpetual problems require dialogue about dreams, not solutions.

**Principle 7: Create Shared Meaning** — Build rituals, traditions, and shared goals.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Contempt is the single best predictor of divorce.** Criticism, defensiveness, and stonewalling are harmful, but contempt is lethal.

2. **The 5:1 ratio matters.** Happy couples have 5 positive interactions for every 1 negative.

3. **69% of problems are perpetual.** They will never be solved — the goal is dialogue, not resolution.

4. **Emotional bids are the currency of relationships.** Turning toward bids builds emotional bank accounts.
