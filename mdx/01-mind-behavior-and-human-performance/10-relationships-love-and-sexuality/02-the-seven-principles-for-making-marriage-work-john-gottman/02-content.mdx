## The Four Horsemen

Gottman's most famous concept: four communication patterns that predict divorce with remarkable accuracy.

**1. Criticism**: Attacking character rather than behavior. "You are so inconsiderate" vs. "I felt hurt when you were late."

**2. Contempt**: Insults, name-calling, eye-rolling, mockery. The single best predictor of divorce. Contempt says "I am better than you."

**3. Defensiveness**: Playing the victim, making excuses, denying responsibility. Defensiveness escalates conflict because it blames the partner.

**4. Stonewalling**: Emotional withdrawal and disengagement. More common in men. The final step before relationship collapse.

The antidote: gentle startup (for criticism), building a culture of appreciation (for contempt), taking responsibility (for defensiveness), and physiological self-soothing (for stonewalling).

## The Seven Principles

### 1. Enhance Your Love Maps

A "love map" is the part of your brain where you store information about your partner. Happy couples know each other's inner worlds — current stresses, past experiences, future dreams, favorite things. Gottman provides the "Love Map Card Game" exercise.

### 2. Nurture Your Fondness and Admiration

Actively maintain a mental catalog of your partner's positive qualities. This is the antidote to contempt. The "I Appreciate" exercise: write down specific things you appreciate and share them.

### 3. Turn Toward Each Other

Bids for connection are the fundamental unit of emotional connection. A bid can be a question, a touch, a look, a joke. Partners who turn toward bids build emotional bank accounts. Partners who turn away drain them.

### 4. Accept Influence

Gottman's research shows that men who accept influence from their wives have happier marriages. Accepting influence means sharing power, considering your partner's perspective, and compromising.

### 5-6. Solve Solvable Problems / Overcome Gridlock

Solvable problems can be resolved through the Six-Step process. Perpetual problems (69% of conflicts) require "dreams within conflict" dialogue — understanding the underlying values, not finding a solution.

### 7. Create Shared Meaning

Build rituals of connection, shared goals, and a sense of purpose as a couple. This transforms a marriage from a functional partnership into a source of meaning.
