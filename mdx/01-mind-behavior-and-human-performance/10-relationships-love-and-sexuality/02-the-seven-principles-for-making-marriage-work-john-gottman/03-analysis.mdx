## Strengths

- **Unparalleled research basis.** Gottman's observational research is the most rigorous in relationship science.
- **Predictive power.** 91% accuracy in predicting divorce is extraordinary for any psychological measure.
- **Practical.** Specific exercises, scripts, and assessment tools throughout.
- **Non-blaming.** Focuses on behaviors and patterns, not character defects.

## Weaknesses

- **Marriage-focused.** Singles, non-married couples, and non-traditional relationships get less attention.
- **Gender essentialism.** Some advice (particularly about male influence) relies on traditional gender roles.
- **Overemphasis on de-escalation.** Not all conflict should be de-escalated — some issues need passionate engagement.

## Comparison with Attached

| Dimension | Attached | Gottman |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Why we choose partners | How to make relationships work |
| Framework | Attachment theory | Behavioral observation |
| Best for | Self-understanding | Skill-building |
| Research | Developmental psychology | Direct observation |
| Problems addressed | Partner selection | Relationship maintenance |

## Overall

Read this immediately after *Attached*. Together they provide the complete foundation: *Attached* explains why you are drawn to certain partners; Gottman shows you what to do once you are in a relationship.
