## Overview

*Come As You Are* is the most important book on female sexuality written in the last generation. Emily Nagoski debunks the myths that have made generations of women feel broken — the idea that there is a "normal" way to experience desire, arousal, and orgasm — and replaces them with a science-based understanding that emphasizes individual variation and context.

The book is built around the dual control model: everyone has a Sexual Excitation System (SES) and a Sexual Inhibition System (SIS). These systems interact with context to produce sexual response. Nagoski's central insight: there is enormous natural variation in how these systems work, and "normal" is a much wider range than most people think.

## Executive Summary

**Part 1: The Dual Control Model** — How the sexual accelerator and brakes work.

**Part 2: Desire** — Spontaneous vs. responsive desire, and why the difference matters.

**Part 3: Arousal and Orgasm** — The science of arousal non-concordance and the orgasm gap.

**Part 4: Context** — Stress, body image, and relationship factors that shape sexuality.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Your sexuality is not broken.** The range of normal sexual response is enormous.

2. **Context is everything.** Stress, mood, and relationship quality affect female sexuality more than any biological factor.

3. **Responsive desire is normal.** Wanting sex after you start — not before — is how many women's desire works.

4. **The brakes matter more than the accelerator.** Inhibition is as important as excitation.
