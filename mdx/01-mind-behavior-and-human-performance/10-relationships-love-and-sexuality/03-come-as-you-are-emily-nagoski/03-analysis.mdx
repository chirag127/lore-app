## Strengths

- **Scientifically rigorous.** Nagoski is a PhD researcher who presents complex science accessibly.
- **Validating and compassionate.** The book's central message — "you are normal" — is genuinely healing.
- **The dual control model is a powerful framework.** Explains more than any single-factor theory.
- **Practical without being prescriptive.** Nagoski gives tools, not rules.

## Weaknesses

- **Heteronormative assumption.** Most examples assume partners of different genders.
- **Cisgender focus.** Does not fully address trans and non-binary experiences.
- **Very long.** At 400+ pages, it is the longest book in this subcategory.

## Comparison with Mating in Captivity (Perel)

| Dimension | Come As You Are | Mating in Captivity |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Female sexual response | Desire in long-term relationships |
| Framework | Dual control model | Psychoanalytic/Existential |
| Tone | Warm, educational | Provocative, intellectual |
| Evidence | Research-based | Clinical observation |
| Best for | Understanding sexuality | Understanding desire |

## Overall

Read this third, after the relationship foundations. Nagoski provides the sexual science context for the desire dynamics that Perel explores.
