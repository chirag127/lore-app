# Psychiatry & Mental Health

## Overview

Psychiatry and mental health is the clinical arm of the mind sciences. Where other subcategories in this category explore how the mind *normally* works — learning, memory, decision-making, social behavior — this shelf confronts what happens when it breaks. Depression, anxiety, trauma, ADHD, bipolar disorder: these are not edge cases. They are part of the human condition, and understanding them is essential to understanding the mind.

This shelf covers both the **clinical science** (diagnosis, treatment, mechanism) and the **human experience** (what it feels like to live with mental illness, and what it means to heal).

## Why This Exists as a Separate Shelf

Mental health is distinct from other mind sciences in several ways:

- **Clinical vs. descriptive:** This is the only subcategory focused on *pathology* and *treatment*, not normal function.
- **Bridging mind and body:** Psychiatry sits at the intersection of psychology (talk therapy, cognition, emotion) and medicine (neurochemistry, pharmacology, brain structure).
- **Personal stakes:** These books are often read not for intellectual curiosity but for survival — understanding oneself, helping a loved one, or choosing treatment.
- **Historical transformation:** The field has undergone revolutions — from Freudian psychoanalysis to the psychopharmacology revolution of the 1980s to the contemporary biopsychosocial model.

## What Belongs Here

- Clinical psychology and psychopathology
- Evidence-based treatments (CBT, DBT, ACT, exposure therapy)
- Trauma and PTSD (including developmental trauma)
- Mood disorders (depression, bipolar)
- Anxiety disorders and OCD
- ADHD and neurodevelopmental conditions
- Psychopharmacology — how medications work
- The therapy relationship and therapeutic process
- The lived experience of mental illness (memoirs)
- Cultural and systemic factors in mental health
- The history of psychiatry and its critiques

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- General cognitive or behavioral psychology (→ Subcategories 02–07)
- Neuroscience of brain function in healthy adults (→ Subcategory 13)
- Stress, resilience, and wellbeing (→ Subcategory 17)
- Addiction and substance use (cross-listed with Body & Health)
- Personality psychology as a descriptive system (→ Subcategory 08)
- Positive psychology and flourishing (→ Subcategory 20)
- Self-help that is not evidence-based or clinically grounded

## Reading Order

| Step | Book | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| 1 | **Feeling Good** — David Burns | The most practical entry point. Burns gives you the tools of CBT in an immediately usable form. Read this first if you are approaching this shelf for personal reasons. |
| 2 | **Lost Connections** — Johann Hari | A broader frame. Hari asks *why* depression and anxiety are epidemic — and critiques the purely biomedical model. Read after you know what CBT looks like. |
| 3 | **The Body Keeps the Score** — Bessel van der Kolk | The definitive book on trauma. More scientific and clinical. Read after you understand mood disorders; trauma rewrites the rules. |
| 4 | **Maybe You Should Talk to Someone** — Lori Gottlieb | A therapist's memoir that demystifies the therapy process. Read after the science to see what therapy actually looks like from both sides of the couch. |
| 5 | **An Unquiet Mind** — Kay Redfield Jamison | A memoir of bipolar disorder by a clinical psychologist. The most personal book in the shelf — shows that clinicians are not immune. |
| 6 | **Driven to Distraction** — Hallowell & Ratey | ADHD in adults. Read last — ADHD connects to many of the cognitive and productivity themes in other subcategories. |

## How This Contributes to the Knowledge Tree

This shelf connects to almost every other subcategory:

- **Neuroscience of the Mind (13):** Mental disorders are brain disorders. Sapolsky, Doidge, and Eagleman all inform the biological basis of psychopathology.
- **Consciousness and Cognition (14):** Altered states of consciousness — including pathological ones — illuminate the nature of normal cognition.
- **Human Development (15):** Developmental trauma, early attachment, and lifespan mental health.
- **Resilience and Wellbeing (17):** The flip side — what protects against mental illness.
- **Emotional Intelligence (09):** Emotion dysregulation is central to many disorders.
- **Body & Health (Category 02):** The mind-body connection in mental health.
