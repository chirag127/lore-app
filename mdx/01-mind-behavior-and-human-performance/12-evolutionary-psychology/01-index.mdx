# Evolutionary Psychology

## Overview

Evolutionary psychology is the application of evolutionary biology to the human mind. Its central premise: the brain, like every other organ, was shaped by natural selection. The cognitive and emotional programs that make up the human mind were designed by evolutionary pressures operating over hundreds of thousands of years in ancestral environments.

This subcategory covers the foundational texts of evolutionary psychology and its intellectual precursors. These books explore how evolutionary thinking illuminates human nature — our mating strategies, moral intuitions, social instincts, and the deep structure of the human mind.

## Why This Exists as a Separate Shelf

Evolutionary psychology is distinct from other mind sciences:

- **Ultimate vs. proximate explanations:** While other psychology subcategories focus on *how* the mind works (mechanisms, development), evolutionary psychology focuses on *why* — what evolutionary pressures shaped these mechanisms.
- **Consilience:** It connects psychology to biology, anthropology, and economics, providing a unifying framework for understanding human behavior.
- **Controversial:** EP is one of the most contested fields in the social sciences. The books in this shelf engage with both the science and the debate.
- **Foundational:** Understanding the evolved architecture of the mind provides context for every other subcategory in this category.

## Reading Order

| Step | Book | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| 1 | **The Selfish Gene** — Richard Dawkins | The essential foundation. Understand the gene's-eye view of evolution before you apply it to human behavior. |
| 2 | **The Moral Animal** — Robert Wright | The best application of the gene's-eye view to human nature. Wright shows how evolutionary thinking illuminates morality, love, family, and social status. |
| 3 | **The Red Queen** — Matt Ridley | Focuses on sexual selection and the evolution of human sexuality. Read after you understand the basics of gene-level thinking. |
| 4 | **The Evolution of Desire** — David Buss | The scientific evidence for evolved mating strategies. More empirical than Ridley, more focused than Wright. |
