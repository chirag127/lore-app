## The Information Theory of Aging

The traditional view: aging is caused by accumulated damage — DNA mutations, protein aggregation, cellular wear and tear. This view predicts that aging is inevitable and irreversible.

Sinclair's alternative: aging is primarily caused by loss of *information*, not accumulation of damage.

The DNA in every cell contains the same genetic information. But a liver cell is different from a neuron because of epigenetic information — which genes are turned on and off. This epigenetic information degrades over time. Cells lose their identity. They forget what kind of cell they are supposed to be.

This is the Information Theory of Aging. Aging is an information problem.

## Sirtuins and NAD+

The key players in the longevity network:

**Sirtuins:** A family of proteins that regulate cellular health. They repair damaged DNA, control inflammation, and manage cellular energy. There are seven sirtuins in humans (SIRT1-7). They require NAD+ to function.

**NAD+:** A molecule that is essential for sirtuin activity. NAD+ levels decline with age. Boosting NAD+ (through supplementation with NMN or NR) activates sirtuins and slows aging in animal studies.

**mTOR:** A nutrient-sensing pathway. When food is abundant, mTOR promotes growth and reproduction. When food is scarce, mTOR is suppressed and the cell shifts to repair and maintenance mode. Suppressing mTOR (through fasting or drugs like rapamycin) extends lifespan in every species tested.

**AMPK:** An energy sensor. When energy is low, AMPK activates catabolic processes that generate energy and activates longevity pathways.

## The Longevity Interventions

### Lifestyle
- **Caloric restriction/intermittent fasting:** Lowers mTOR, activates AMPK and sirtuins
- **Exercise:** Activates AMPK, improves mitochondrial function
- **Cold exposure:** Activates brown fat and mitochondrial biogenesis
- **Heat exposure (sauna):** Activates heat shock proteins and cellular repair

### Molecules
- **NMN/NR:** NAD+ precursors
- **Resveratrol:** Activates sirtuins (controversial bioavailability)
- **Metformin:** Activates AMPK, lowers glucose
- **Rapamycin:** Inhibits mTOR

### Emerging Technologies
- **Epigenetic reprogramming:** Using Yamanaka factors (OSK) to reset epigenetic age
- **Senolytics:** Drugs that clear senescent ("zombie") cells
- **Young blood factors:** Identifying molecules in young blood that rejuvenate old tissues

## The Experiments

Sinclair's lab has demonstrated:

- Reversing vision loss in old mice by reprogramming retinal cells
- Restoring muscle function in aged mice
- Extending lifespan in mice by 30-50% using various interventions
- Resetting the epigenetic clock

The key: these are not slowing aging — they are *reversing* it. Cells that had aged are being returned to a younger state.

## Practical Applications

- Exercise is the single most effective longevity intervention. Do it.
- Intermittent fasting or time-restricted eating activates longevity pathways.
- Cold exposure (cold showers, ice baths) has measurable benefits.
- NMN or NR supplements may boost NAD+ — the evidence is promising but not definitive.
- Metformin may extend healthspan (discuss with your doctor).
- The biggest risk factors for aging are the ones you can control: diet, exercise, sleep, stress, social connection.
- Do not smoke. Limit alcohol. Manage your weight.
- The science of rejuvenation is advancing rapidly. Stay informed.
- Do not count on being saved by future technology. Do the lifestyle work now.
