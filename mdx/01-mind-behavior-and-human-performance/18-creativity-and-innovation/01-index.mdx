## Overview

This shelf collects books about the psychology, practice, and culture of
creativity and innovation — how new ideas emerge, why they are blocked,
and how individuals and organizations can reliably produce original work.

Creativity sits at the intersection of cognitive psychology (divergent
thinking, insight), motivational psychology (intrinsic drive, flow),
and social psychology (creative environments, collaboration).
Innovation extends creativity into execution — turning novel ideas into
products, services, or works that change how people think or live.

## Why This Shelf Exists

Creativity is not merely a sub-skill of learning or cognition — it has
its own psychology, its own blockers (Resistance, fear, creative
anxiety), and its own enabling conditions (psychological safety,
autonomy, play). Earlier shelves covered *how* the mind works; this
shelf covers *how to make something new* with that mind.

This shelf also serves as a bridge to Subcategory 05 (Productivity
and Execution) — creativity without execution is hobbyism. The books
here emphasize both the inner game (overcoming Resistance) and the
outer game (showing your work, building creative confidence).

## What Belongs Here

- The psychology of creative blocks and how to overcome them
- Design thinking and human-centered innovation
- Daily practices and routines of creative professionals
- The relationship between creativity, intrinsic motivation, and flow
- Sharing work, building an audience, and the creative career
- Organizational innovation culture and creative confidence

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- **Expertise and deliberate practice** (Subcategory 19) — skill
  acquisition, not creative production
- **Flow** as a general phenomenon (Subcategory 16) — flow in creativity
  is an application, not the core concept
- **Entrepreneurship** (Business category) — business innovation is a
  downstream application of creative principles
- **Artistic technique** — the craft skills belong to their respective
  domains; this shelf covers the universal psychology

## Reading Order

| Order | Book | Justification |
|-------|------|---------------|
| 1 | **Creative Confidence** | The mindset foundation. Most people believe they aren't creative — this book debunks that and builds the confidence to start. |
| 2 | **Steal Like an Artist** | The methodology. A playful, practical manifesto for how creative work actually happens — by remixing, not inventing from nothing. |
| 3 | **The War of Art** | The inner game. The most important book on creative blocks ever written. Read this when you know what to make but can't make yourself start. |
| 4 | **Show Your Work!** | The outer game. Creativity isn't complete until it's shared. A guide to building an audience and putting your work into the world. |

## Contribution to the Knowledge Tree

Creativity draws on cognitive flexibility (Subcategory 02), intrinsic
motivation (Subcategory 16), and emotional resilience (Subcategory 17).
This shelf integrates those threads into a practical creative practice.
It is the upstream of innovation in the Business category and the
applied counterpart of the cognitive psychology of insight.
