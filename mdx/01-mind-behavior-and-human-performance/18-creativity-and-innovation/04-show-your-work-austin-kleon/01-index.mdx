## Overview

*Show Your Work! 10 Ways to Share Your Creativity and Get Discovered*
(2014) by Austin Kleon is the companion volume to *Steal Like an
Artist*. While the first book was about the creative *process*, this
one is about the creative *career* — how to share what you make, build
an audience, and find opportunities without selling out.

Kleon argues that the internet has democratized distribution but
created a new problem: obscurity. The solution is not self-promotion
in the traditional sense but generosity — sharing your process, your
sources, and your unfinished work so that people can follow along and
connect with you and your work.

## Key Takeaways

- You don't need to be a genius — you need to be findable
- Share your process, not just your finished products
- "So what do you do?" is the wrong question; show, don't tell
- Document your work as you go — the process is as interesting as the
  product
- Tell good stories about your work — narrative context creates meaning
- Teach what you know — teaching is the best way to learn and build
  authority
- Don't worry about people stealing your ideas — if they're good, you'll
  have more
- Keep a "flow" (the process) and a "stock" (the archive) — share from
  both
- Build a community, not an audience — the relationship is reciprocal

## Who Should Read

| Reader Profile | Why |
|---|---|
| Creatives building an audience | A generous, sustainable approach to self-promotion |
| Anyone who says "I'm not good at self-promotion" | This reframes promotion as sharing and teaching |
| Freelancers and independent creators | The blueprint for getting found |
| Students entering creative fields | How to start building a career while you learn |
| People who feel invisible | Permission to put your work into the world |

## Who Should Skip

- Those looking for social media growth hacks — this is a philosophy,
  not a tactics manual
- People who already have a strong public presence — the advice is
  beginner-focused
- Anyone who believes in starving artist mystique — Kleon is pro-career

| Build Community | Relationships, not followers |

## Why This Book Matters

*Show Your Work!* solved a problem that creatives had been struggling
with since the internet eliminated traditional gatekeeping: how do you
get noticed without being obnoxious? Kleon's answer — share generously,
teach what you know, document your process — is both ethical and
effective. It's the book that made "building in public" a mainstream
creative practice.

## Final Verdict

*Show Your Work!* is the essential companion to *Steal Like an Artist*.
Together they form a complete creative practice: how to make things
and how to share them. Kleon's approach — generosity over self-promotion,
process over product, teaching over selling — is both more sustainable
and more effective than traditional marketing approaches.

**Rating: 8/10** — A modern classic for creators. Pairs perfectly with
*Steal Like an Artist*.
