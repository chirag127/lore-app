Anatomy and Physiology 2e by OpenStax is a peer-reviewed textbook that covers the structure and function of the human body across all major organ systems. Published by Rice University in 2022, this second edition runs over thirteen hundred pages and is designed for two-semester introductory courses in life sciences and allied health programs. The book is authored by a team of ten anatomy and physiology educators led by J. Gordon Betts and is available completely free of charge as part of the OpenStax open educational resource initiative.

The textbook is organized around a few central principles. First, structure and function are inseparable at every level of biological organization from cells to organ systems. Every anatomical feature exists because it supports a specific physiological role. Second, homeostasis is the unifying concept that connects all body systems. The body uses negative and positive feedback loops to maintain a stable internal environment, and each chapter revisits this theme to show how its system contributes to or is regulated by homeostasis. Third, the body operates as an integrated network rather than a collection of independent parts.

The twenty-eight chapters follow a logical progression. The opening sections cover the foundations of anatomy and physiology, the chemical and cellular levels of organization, and tissue histology. The remaining chapters proceed system by system covering the integumentary, skeletal, muscular, nervous, endocrine, cardiovascular, lymphatic, respiratory, digestive, urinary, and reproductive systems. A final chapter on development and inheritance closes the volume. Notable strengths include the detailed treatment of the nervous system across four chapters and the cardiovascular system across three chapters.

Each chapter opens with clear learning objectives and closes with key terms, review questions, critical thinking exercises, and clinical correlations. Clinical Connections boxes link basic science to real medical scenarios such as bone fractures, cardiovascular disease, asthma, and kidney stones. Interactive Links direct readers to online animations and simulations that reinforce dynamic processes like muscle contraction and nerve impulse propagation.

The second edition incorporates a decade of classroom use and reviewer feedback. Errata from the first edition have been corrected and clinical content updated. The illustrations are medically accurate though the text relies entirely on diagrams and model photographs rather than cadaveric images. The molecular and cellular mechanisms are simplified compared to advanced textbooks, which makes the material accessible to beginners but may require supplementation for students pursuing upper-division coursework.

The ancillary materials are a major strength. Free downloadable PowerPoint slides, test banks, answer keys, and a course cartridge compatible with major learning management systems make adoption by instructors practical. More than two thousand glossary terms provide continuous reference support throughout the text.

As an open educational resource, this book eliminates the financial barrier that traditional textbooks impose. It is one of the most widely adopted OER textbooks in higher education and serves as the primary text for countless anatomy and physiology courses across the United States. For students beginning their study of the human body, allied health professionals building their foundational knowledge, or anyone seeking a thorough yet accessible introduction to human anatomy and physiology, this textbook represents an extraordinary resource that combines academic rigor with universal accessibility.
