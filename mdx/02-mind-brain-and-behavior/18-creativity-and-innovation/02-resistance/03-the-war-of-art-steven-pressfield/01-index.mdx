## Overview

*The War of Art* (2002) by Steven Pressfield names the universal force that
keeps creative work undone: **Resistance**. It manifests as procrastination,
rationalization, perfectionism, self-doubt, and a hundred other small
betrayals. Pressfield's argument is that the work itself is straightforward;
the work *of doing the work* is where every artist, entrepreneur, and athlete
is tested.

The cure is **turning pro** — treating creative practice as a job rather
than an inspiration-driven hobby. Showing up at the desk, regardless of mood;
finishing what you start; ignoring the result. The professional, in
Pressfield's framing, is not someone with talent — they're someone who has
learned to override Resistance daily.
