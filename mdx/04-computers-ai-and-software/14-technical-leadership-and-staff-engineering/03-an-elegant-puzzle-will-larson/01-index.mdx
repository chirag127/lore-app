## Overview

*An Elegant Puzzle* (2019) by Will Larson is a systems-oriented approach
to engineering management. Rather than offering anecdotal advice, Larson
applies engineering first principles to organizational problems —
designing team structures, managing technical debt, sizing organizations,
and building effective engineering cultures.

The book is organized around four domains: organizations, tools,
approaches, and culture. Each chapter presents a specific problem and
a structured framework for solving it.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Teams are the fundamental unit of engineering organizations.**
   Design teams to be small (6-8 people), have clear missions, and
   minimize dependencies.

2. **Technical debt must be explicitly managed.** Track it as a
   percentage of engineering investment — typically 10-40% depending
   on the stage of the product.

3. **Hiring is a system, not an event.** Build a repeatable process
   for sourcing, interviewing, and closing candidates.

4. **Onboarding determines retention.** The first 90 days are critical.
   Invest in structured onboarding programs.

5. **Size teams by the Dunbar number.** Teams larger than 8 struggle
   with communication overhead. Split teams when they reach 10-12.

6. **Run incident retrospectives without blame.** Focus on systems and
   processes, not individual mistakes.

7. **Engineering velocity is about outcomes, not output.** Measure
   shipped value, not story points.

8. **Leadership requires context, not control.** Provide clear context
   and constraints, then delegate execution authority.

9. **Career ladders must be concrete.** Vague expectations lead to
   unfair evaluations and demotivated engineers.

10. **Culture is the sum of your systems.** If you want a better
    culture, change your systems — not your values statement.

## Final Verdict

A unique, systems-thinking approach to engineering management. Larson's
engineering mindset brings clarity to organizational problems that are
often treated as mysteries. Dense with actionable frameworks.

**Rating: 8.5/10** — A fresh, analytically rigorous take on
engineering management. Best paired with The Manager's Path for
the people-management side.
