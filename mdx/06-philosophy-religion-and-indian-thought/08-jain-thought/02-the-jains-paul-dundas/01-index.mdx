# The Jains

## Executive Summary

Paul Dundas's The Jains is the standard scholarly introduction to one of the world's oldest but least understood religions. The book provides a comprehensive overview of Jain history, from its origins with the tirthankaras through its medieval flourishing to its modern transformations. Dundas covers Jain philosophy, ethics, ritual, literature, and social organization with scholarly rigor and clarity. The book is essential reading for anyone who wants to understand Jainism on its own terms.



## Key Takeaways

- **Jainism is a non-theistic religion** centered on individual spiritual liberation through ascetic practice
- **Ahimsa (non-violence)** is the central ethical principle, extended to all living beings
- **The Jain cosmology** describes an eternal, uncreated universe governed by karmic law
- **The path to liberation** requires rigorous asceticism and the destruction of karmic matter
- **Jainism has adapted** to modernity while maintaining its core principles

## Who Should Read

- **Scholars and students**: The definitive academic introduction to Jainism
- **Comparative religion readers**: Understand a non-theistic, ascetic tradition
- **Ahimsa practitioners**: The philosophical foundations of non-violence
- **Indian culture enthusiasts**: An essential element of India's religious diversity

## Who Should Skip

- Those wanting a **popular or devotional** introduction
- Readers seeking **simple overviews** — this is scholarly
- People looking for **comparative analysis** rather than focused study

## Difficulty

**Hard** — Scholarly treatment with technical terminology. Assumes some background in Indian religious studies.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: 10-12 hours

## Related Books

## Final Verdict

**Essential** for anyone seriously studying Jainism. Dundas's scholarship is authoritative, his coverage comprehensive, and his treatment balanced. The book is the gold standard for academic introductions to Jainism.
