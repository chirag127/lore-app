The writing style is scholarly, dense, and precise. Paul Dundas writes for an academic audience, using technical terminology with care and expecting readers to have some familiarity with Indian religious categories. His prose is clear within the conventions of academic writing but can be demanding for general readers. He defines terms when they are first introduced but assumes a level of background knowledge. The tone is neutral and scholarly, presenting Jain traditions with respect while maintaining academic distance.

The narrative structure is thematic rather than strictly chronological. The book opens with an overview of Jain history, then proceeds through doctrine, scripture, monastic practice, lay life, and modern developments. Each chapter is a self-contained treatment of its topic, but themes recur across chapters, building a cumulative portrait of Jainism. Dundas frequently notes debates among scholars and uncertainties in the historical record, giving the reader a sense of the field's current state of knowledge.

The rhetorical approach is expository and analytical. Dundas explains Jain doctrines as they are understood within the tradition, then offers scholarly analysis and contextualization. He is careful to distinguish between what Jains believe and what scholars can verify, and he acknowledges areas of disagreement within both the tradition and the academy. The book is designed as a reference work that can be consulted on specific topics as well as read through.

Readability is moderate for those with some background in religious studies and low for general readers. The prose is dense and packed with information. Technical terms in Sanskrit and Prakrit appear frequently, though they are explained. The book rewards careful reading but is not accessible as a casual introduction.

The book belongs to the Routledge series of introductions to world religions and reflects the series' commitment to scholarly rigor. Dundas's contribution stands out for its comprehensive treatment of the Jain scriptural tradition and its balanced presentation of both Digambara and Svetambara perspectives. The book has become the standard academic reference on Jainism, cited by scholars and used as a textbook in university courses on Indian religions. Its unique contribution is providing a complete, authoritative overview of Jainism that does justice to its complexity and historical depth.
