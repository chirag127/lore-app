keyIdeas:
  - "System dynamics models use stocks (accumulations), flows (rates of change), and feedback loops to capture the behavior of complex environmental systems over time"
  - "Causal loop diagrams reveal the feedback structure of a system — balancing loops stabilize, reinforcing loops amplify"
  - "Environmental policy often fails because it ignores feedback delays — the time between action and response can cause oscillation and overshoot"
  - "Model validity is not about predicting the future but about understanding system behavior and testing policy robustness"
  - "The best models are simple enough to understand, complex enough to capture the essential dynamics, and transparent enough to build consensus"
keyTakeaways:
  - "Always sketch the causal loop diagram before building the quantitative model — the feedback structure determines the behavior"
  - "Delays in environmental systems are the most common cause of policy failure: act too late, and the system has already crossed a threshold"
  - "Sensitivity analysis reveals which parameters matter most — focus data collection and policy effort there"
  - "The model is not reality: validation is about building confidence, not proving correctness"
  - "Participatory modeling — involving stakeholders in model building — increases trust and improves policy decisions"
