# Strengths

## Strength 1: Definitive Scholarship

Chernow's research is exhaustive. He has combed through every available source, and the book rests on a foundation of archival research that will not soon be superseded. For anyone wanting the complete story of Hamilton's life, this is the book.

## Strength 2: Rehabilitation of Reputation

Before Chernow, Hamilton was often treated as a minor founder or a villain. Chernow restores him to his rightful place alongside Washington, Jefferson, and Madison as one of the most consequential founders.

## Strength 3: Narrative Power

Despite its length and detail, the book is extraordinarily readable. Chernow has a journalist's gift for storytelling and character, and the narrative never flags.

# Weaknesses

## Weakness 1: Partisanship

Chernow is clearly on Hamilton's side. Jefferson is treated critically, sometimes unfairly. The book is a rehabilitation, and it sometimes overcorrects.

## Weakness 2: Length

At 832 pages, the book is longer than it needs to be. Some readers will find the level of detail excessive, particularly in the middle sections.

## Weakness 3: Limited Context

The book focuses tightly on Hamilton and sometimes neglects broader historical context. Readers wanting to understand the full Revolutionary and early Republic period should supplement with other books.

# Criticism

## Critic 1: *The New York Times*

"Magnificent. Chernow has written the definitive biography of the most brilliant and enigmatic of the founding fathers. The book is a triumph of research and narrative."

## Critic 2: *The Washington Post*

"Chernow's Hamilton is a masterpiece of the biographer's art — deeply researched, elegantly written, and passionately argued. It restores Hamilton to his proper place in American history."

## Critic 3: *The New Yorker*

"Chernow makes a powerful case for Hamilton's centrality to the American founding. The book is perhaps too sympathetic to its subject, but the scholarship is impeccable and the narrative gripping."

# Historical Context

## The Hamilton Revival

Chernow's biography was part of a broader scholarly reassessment of Hamilton that had been building for decades. The musical Hamilton by Lin-Manuel Miranda, inspired by this book, completed the rehabilitation and made Hamilton a popular culture icon.

## The Founders Debate

The book contributed to ongoing debates about the founding fathers' legacies. Chernow's Hamilton is a hero of American capitalism and federal power, a figure who appeals to those who believe in strong national institutions.

# Final Assessment

**9/10** — The definitive biography of Alexander Hamilton and one of the finest American biographies ever written. Chernow's research is exhaustive, his prose engaging, and his rehabilitation of Hamilton's reputation both convincing and compelling. The length and pro-Hamilton bias are minor flaws in an otherwise magnificent work.
