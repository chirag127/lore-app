# 🎓 Philosophy & History of Education

## 📚 Overview

What is education for? How should we teach? Who decides what is taught? This shelf covers the philosophical and historical foundations of education: from classical theories and critical pedagogy to the history of formal schooling and the modern research university.

These books are essential for anyone who cares about education — which is everyone.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

This category focuses on the **philosophy and history** of education.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Classical theories of education: Plato, Rousseau, Dewey
- Critical pedagogy: Paulo Freire and the politics of education
- History of formal schooling
- The research university and knowledge production
- University systems and academic culture

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Classical Theories of Education** — The philosophical foundations of education from Plato to Dewey.
- **Critical Pedagogy** — Paulo Freire's approach to education as liberation.
- **History of Formal Schooling** — How formal education evolved from ancient times to the present.
- **Research University and Knowledge Production** — The role of universities in creating and disseminating knowledge.
- **University Systems and Academic Culture** — How universities work and why they matter.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Education is the most powerful tool for individual and societal transformation. Understanding its philosophy and history helps us build better educational systems and make more informed choices about our own learning.
