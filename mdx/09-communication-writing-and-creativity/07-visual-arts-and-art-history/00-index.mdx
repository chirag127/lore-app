# Visual Arts & Art History

## Overview

Visual art shapes how we see the world. This shelf covers three essential perspectives: Gombrich's narrative history of Western art, Berger's political critique of visual culture, and Arnheim's psychological analysis of visual perception. Together, these books provide a complete foundation for understanding how images work.

## Essential Books

- **The Story of Art** — E.H. Gombrich: The world's best-selling art history book, from cave paintings to the 20th century.
- **Ways of Seeing** — John Berger: A radical critique of visual culture, the male gaze, and the politics of art.
- **Art and Visual Perception** — Rudolf Arnheim: A Gestalt-psychology approach to understanding composition, form, and expression.
