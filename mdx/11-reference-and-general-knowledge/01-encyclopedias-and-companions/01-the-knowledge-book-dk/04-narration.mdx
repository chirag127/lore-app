The writing style is clear, concise, and designed for quick comprehension. DK uses short paragraphs, bullet points, and labeled diagrams to maximize information density while maintaining readability. The tone is authoritative but friendly, assuming curiosity rather than prior knowledge. Technical terms are bolded and defined in context, and the visual layout guides the eye from main concepts to supporting details.

The narrative structure is modular rather than linear. Each two-page spread is a self-contained unit of knowledge, designed to be understood independently. This modularity makes the book ideal for browsing and reference use, though it means there is less narrative momentum than in a traditional textbook. The book rewards both sequential reading and random access.

The rhetorical approach is explanatory rather than argumentative. The book aims to convey established knowledge, not to advance new theories or challenge existing frameworks. This makes it a reliable reference but not a provocative or thought-provoking read in the way that a book with a strong thesis can be. The voice is that of a knowledgeable guide showing you around the museum of human knowledge.

Readability is excellent for the intended audience. The visual layout, clear section headings, and modular design make the book easy to navigate and digest. The text itself is written at a level appropriate for high school students and above, with technical concepts explained in plain language before any terminology is introduced.

This book fits within DK's long tradition of illustrated reference works, including their acclaimed children's encyclopedias and subject-specific visual guides. Its unique contribution is attempting a comprehensive general knowledge reference in DK's signature visual style, aimed at a family audience rather than children or specialists exclusively. It represents a bet that visual learning can make general knowledge accessible and enjoyable for readers of all ages.
