# Wikipedia: The Missing Manual

## Executive Summary

John Broughton's *Wikipedia: The Missing Manual* is a practical, how-to guide for editing and contributing to Wikipedia. Published by O'Reilly as part of their Missing Manual series, it covers the technical aspects of wiki markup, the social norms of the Wikipedia community, and the policies and guidelines that govern content creation. It is the definitive guide for anyone who wants to move from Wikipedia consumer to Wikipedia contributor.



## Key Takeaways

- **Anyone can edit Wikipedia**, but effective editing requires understanding community norms
- **Wikipedia's five pillars** form the foundation of all editorial policy
- **Dispute resolution** is a structured process with multiple stages
- **Reliable sources** are the backbone of Wikipedia content
- **Wikipedia's quality** has improved dramatically since its early years

## Who Should Read

- **New editors**: Learn to contribute effectively from day one
- **Educators**: Teach information literacy and digital citizenship
- **Experienced editors**: Deepen understanding of policies and best practices
- **Researchers**: Study online collaboration and knowledge production

## Who Should Skip

- Readers who only want to **consume** Wikipedia content
- Those seeking a **history** of Wikipedia rather than a practical guide
- Users who prefer **video tutorials** to written manuals

## Difficulty

**Easy** — Accessible to any reader with basic computer skills. Technical concepts are explained from first principles.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: 8-10 hours
- **Reference Use**: Variable

**Highly Recommended** for anyone who wants to contribute to Wikipedia. It remains the best practical guide available, even though some technical details are now dated. The social and policy guidance is still largely current and invaluable.
