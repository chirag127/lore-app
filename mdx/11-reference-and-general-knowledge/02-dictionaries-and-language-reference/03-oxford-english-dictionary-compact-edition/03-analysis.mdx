# Strengths

## Strength 1: Unmatched Comprehensiveness

The OED is the most comprehensive dictionary of English ever created. It covers not just standard vocabulary but also technical terms, slang, dialect words, and obsolete words. No other dictionary approaches its coverage of the English language.

## Strength 2: Historical Depth

The quotation system provides an unparalleled record of how English has been used across more than a millennium. Each word entry is a mini-history of that word's life in the language, showing when meanings emerged, flourished, and sometimes disappeared.

## Strength 3: Scholarly Reliability

The OED's research process, editorial standards, and system of revisions ensure that its content is as accurate and reliable as humanly possible. It is the gold standard for lexical authority.

## Strength 4: Cultural Significance

The OED is more than a reference work — it is a cultural monument. It represents the collective effort of thousands of scholars over more than a century to document the English language in its full richness.

# Weaknesses

## Weakness 1: The Cost

The Compact Edition is expensive, and a full OED subscription costs hundreds of dollars per year. This puts the work beyond the reach of many potential users.

## Weakness 2: Physical Impracticality

The Compact Edition requires a magnifying glass and a desk. It is not portable or convenient for quick reference. The online version solves this problem but lacks the physical beauty of the books.

## Weakness 3: Lag in New Words

Even with continuous updates, there is inevitably a lag between a word entering the language and its inclusion in the OED. Very recent vocabulary is better served by online dictionaries.

# Historical Context

## The Making of the OED

The first edition was conceived in 1857 and published in fascicles from 1884 to 1928 — over 70 years of work. The editor James Murray oversaw the project from 1879 until his death in 1915, coordinating a network of volunteer readers who submitted quotation slips. The second edition, published in 1989, integrated the original OED with its supplements into a single work of over 20,000 pages.

## The Compact Edition's Place

The Compact Edition, first published in 1971 and updated for the second edition in 1991, made the OED accessible to individual owners for the first time. Before the Compact Edition, the multi-volume set was primarily owned by libraries and institutions. The online version, launched in 2000, has now largely superseded the print edition for everyday use.

# Final Assessment

**10/10** — The greatest dictionary ever created and one of the supreme achievements of human scholarship. The Compact Edition makes this monument physically accessible, though challenging to read. For the depth, accuracy, and historical scope of its content, it receives the highest possible rating.
