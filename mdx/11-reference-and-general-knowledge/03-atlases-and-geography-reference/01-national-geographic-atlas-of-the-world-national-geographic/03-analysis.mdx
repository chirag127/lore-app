# Strengths

## Strength 1: Cartographic Excellence

National Geographic's maps are the finest available in print. The topographic relief shading, color palettes, typography, and level of detail are all outstanding. The maps are both scientifically accurate and aesthetically beautiful.

## Strength 2: Thematic Depth

The atlas goes beyond mere location maps to include thematic spreads on population, climate, energy, health, and other global issues. This thematic content transforms the atlas from a reference work into an educational resource that helps readers understand the world.

## Strength 3: Currency

The 11th edition (2019) incorporates recent political changes, new geographic data, and updated satellite imagery. South Sudan, the most recent independent state, is included. The thematic maps reflect the most current available data.

## Strength 4: Physical Quality

The atlas is beautifully produced with high-quality paper, binding, and printing. It is designed to last for decades of use. The large format allows maps to be shown at scales that reveal significant detail.

# Weaknesses

## Weakness 1: Size and Weight

At 448 pages in a large format, the atlas is heavy and not portable. It is a desk reference, not something you carry with you.

## Weakness 2: Cost

The atlas is expensive, reflecting its production values and National Geographic's brand. This puts it out of reach for some potential buyers.

## Weakness 3: Digital Competition

For many users, online maps like Google Maps or interactive atlases like ArcGIS provide more functionality, especially for finding specific locations or getting driving directions. The print atlas serves a different purpose but faces competition from free digital alternatives.

# Criticism

## Critic 1: *Library Journal*

"The National Geographic Atlas of the World maintains its position as the premier English-language world atlas. The 11th edition features excellent thematic maps that provide valuable context for understanding global issues. Essential for reference collections."

## Critic 2: *Geographical Magazine*

"National Geographic continues to set the standard for print cartography. The thematic section on climate change and human geography is particularly strong. While digital maps are more practical for daily navigation, this atlas offers a perspective and beauty that no screen can match."

# Historical Context

## National Geographic's Cartographic Tradition

National Geographic has been producing maps and atlases for over a century. The Society's cartographic division was established in 1915 and has produced some of the most famous maps in history, including the first accurate map of the ocean floor and the first maps of many remote regions.

## The Atlas in the Digital Age

The 11th edition represents National Geographic's response to the digital transformation of cartography. Rather than competing with digital maps for locational queries, the atlas focuses on what print does well: providing a comprehensive, curated view of the world that encourages exploration and understanding.

# Final Assessment

**9/10** — The finest print atlas available, combining cartographic excellence with thematic depth and beautiful production. It is expensive and heavy, but for those who value the experience of exploring a world-class atlas, it is worth the investment.
