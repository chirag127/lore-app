The writing style is clear, informative, and accessible. National Geographic's cartographers and editors write with precision about geographic features, explaining tectonic processes, climate patterns, and human geography in language that is accurate without being overly technical. Map labels are carefully placed and styled to maximize readability without cluttering the visual field. The thematic text sections are written for a general audience, assuming curiosity but not specialized knowledge.

The narrative structure is hierarchical, moving from global thematic maps to continental overviews to regional detail. This structure allows readers to understand the context before diving into specific regions. Each continent section begins with an overview spread showing the continent at a glance, followed by detailed regional maps with increasing levels of detail. The thematic sections provide cross-cutting analysis of global issues that transcend regional boundaries.

The rhetorical approach is authoritative and objective. National Geographic presents itself as a trusted source of geographic information, and the atlas's tone reflects this confidence. Data sources are cited, mapping methodologies are explained, and the limits of cartographic knowledge are acknowledged. The atlas does not advocate for specific political positions but rather aims to present accurate information that readers can use to form their own understanding.

Readability is excellent for the format. The large page size allows maps to be presented at readable scales, and the layout guides the eye from overall context to specific details. An extensive index makes it easy to find specific locations. The thematic sections use graphs, charts, and infographics alongside maps to present data in multiple complementary formats.

National Geographic's atlas tradition, dating back to the early 20th century, represents a particular approach to cartography that values both scientific accuracy and aesthetic beauty. The atlas is both a reference work and an object of beauty, designed to be consulted for information and enjoyed for its visual qualities.
