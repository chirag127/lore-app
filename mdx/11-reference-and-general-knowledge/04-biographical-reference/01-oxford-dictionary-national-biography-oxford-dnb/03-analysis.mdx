# Strengths

## Strength 1: Unmatched Authority

Each entry is written by a specialist scholar and peer-reviewed. The editorial standards are exacting, and the quality of scholarship is consistently high. For biographical facts about British historical figures, the DNB is the gold standard.

## Strength 2: Comprehensive Scope

With over 60,000 entries spanning British history from prehistoric times to the present, the DNB has no rival in its coverage of British biography. The inclusion of previously marginalized groups in the 2004 edition significantly improved its comprehensiveness.

## Strength 3: Scholarly Apparatus

Bibliographies accompany each entry, providing a clear path for further research. The cross-referencing system connects related entries and enables users to trace networks of influence and association.

# Weaknesses

## Weakness 1: Cost

The print edition is expensive, and the online edition requires an institutional or personal subscription. This limits access primarily to libraries and universities.

## Weakness 2: Academic Tone

Entries are written for an academic audience and assume some historical knowledge. General readers may find the tone scholarly and the level of detail overwhelming for casual reference.

## Weakness 3: British Focus

The DNB is explicitly limited to British figures. This focus is part of its mission, but it limits its utility for readers seeking biographical information about non-British historical figures.

# Criticism

## Critic 1: *The Times Literary Supplement*

"A monumental achievement of scholarship and editing. The Oxford DNB surpasses its predecessor in every respect: more inclusive, more scholarly, and more useful. It is the indispensable starting point for any biographical research on British historical figures."

## Critic 2: *History Today*

"The Oxford DNB represents a significant improvement over the Victorian original, particularly in its expanded coverage of women and marginalized groups. The online edition with quarterly updates ensures the work remains current."

# Historical Context

## From the DNB to the Oxford DNB

The original Dictionary of National Biography was published between 1885 and 1900 under the editorship of Leslie Stephen and Sidney Lee. It contained 29,000 entries and was shaped by Victorian assumptions about who mattered. The Oxford DNB (2004), funded by a major grant from the British Academy, undertook a complete revision that doubled the number of entries and transformed the work's ideological framework.

## Current Status

The Oxford DNB continues to expand online with approximately 200 new entries added each year. It remains a living work, with ongoing revision and correction of existing entries.

# Final Assessment

**10/10** — The definitive biographical reference for British history, unmatched in authority and scope. The 2004 revision transformed a Victorian monument into a 21st-century scholarly resource. Essential for any academic library and invaluable for serious researchers.
