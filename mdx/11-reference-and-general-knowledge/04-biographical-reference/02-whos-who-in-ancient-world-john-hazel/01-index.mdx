# Who's Who in the Ancient World

## Executive Summary

John Hazel's Who's Who in the Ancient World is a compact biographical dictionary covering the key figures of Greek and Roman history and mythology. From Alexander the Great to Zeus, from Socrates to Seneca, the book provides concise, reliable biographical information with historical context. It bridges the gap between mythological figures known from literature and historical figures known from archaeology and written sources, acknowledging that in the ancient world the line between history and myth is often blurred.



## Key Takeaways

- **Coverage spans** from Mycenaean period to the fall of Rome
- **Includes mythological** figures alongside historical statesmen and writers
- **Each entry** provides biography, context, and assessment of significance
- **Cross-references** link related figures across mythology and history
- **Genealogical tables** of major mythological families

## Who Should Read

- **Classics students**: Quick reference for figures encountered in ancient literature
- **General readers**: Identify figures in classical texts and references
- **Travelers**: Context for Mediterranean historical sites
- **Anyone**: Quick biographical context for ancient figures

## Who Should Skip

- **Specialists** needing more detailed treatment
- Readers focused on **non-Greco-Roman** civilizations
- Those seeking **in-depth analysis** rather than concise entries

## Difficulty

**Medium** — Reference work requiring some familiarity with classical history. Accessible to motivated general readers.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: Not designed for linear reading
- **Reference**: Consult as needed
