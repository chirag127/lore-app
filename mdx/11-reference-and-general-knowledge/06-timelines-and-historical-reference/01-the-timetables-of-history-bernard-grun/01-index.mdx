# The Timetables of History

## Executive Summary

Bernard Grun's The Timetables of History is an indispensable chronological reference that presents world history in parallel columns, allowing readers to see at a glance what was happening simultaneously in politics, literature, science, art, music, religion, and daily life. Covering 7,000 years from 5000 BCE to 1990, it enables readers to place any historical event in the context of its time.



## Key Takeaways

- **Covers 7,000 years** from 5000 BCE to 1990 CE
- **Seven categories** displayed in parallel columns
- **See simultaneous events** across different fields
- **Categories** include politics, literature, science, art, music, daily life
- **Index** of names and subjects makes specific events easy to find

## Who Should Read

- **History enthusiasts**: Understand chronological relationships
- **Students**: Chronological knowledge for exams
- **Writers**: Contextualize historical events
- **Trivia fans**: See how events relate in time

## Who Should Skip

- Those seeking **narrative history** rather than reference
- Readers who prefer **deep analysis** of specific periods
- People who find **tabular references** visually unappealing
