The writing style is clear, direct, and designed for quick consumption. Each fact is stated in one or two sentences, typically following a pattern: a surprising claim followed by the specific details that make it remarkable. The tone is light and engaging, conveying the author's own wonder at the facts being presented.

The narrative structure is thematic, grouping related facts into categories. Within each category, there is no particular order — the facts are arranged for variety and surprise. The structure encourages browsing rather than linear reading, with each page offering a new discovery.

The rhetorical approach is revelatory and wonder-filled. Morse presents facts as discoveries to be shared, gifts of knowledge that enrich the reader's understanding of the world. The implicit message is that reality is stranger and more wonderful than we usually acknowledge.

Readability is very high. The facts are short, clearly written, and self-contained. The book can be opened to any page and read with pleasure.

This book fits within the genre of compact trivia collections that were popular before the internet made such information ubiquitous. Its unique contribution is the selection of genuinely remarkable facts that reliably produce a sense of wonder in readers.
