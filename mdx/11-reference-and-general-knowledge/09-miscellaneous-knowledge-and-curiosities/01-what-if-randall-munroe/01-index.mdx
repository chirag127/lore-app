# What If?

## Executive Summary

Randall Munroe, creator of the webcomic xkcd, takes absurd hypothetical questions from his readers and answers them with serious, rigorous science. What would happen if you tried to hit a baseball pitched at 90% of the speed of light? What if everyone on Earth jumped at the same time? What if you built a periodic table made entirely of actual elements? The answers are hilarious, illuminating, and surprisingly educational.



## Key Takeaways

- **Absurd questions** often lead to profound scientific insights
- **Applying physics** to extreme scenarios reveals the limits of our world
- **Scientific literacy** means knowing how to reason, not just what to memorize
- **The process** of finding the answer is more valuable than the answer itself
- **Humor** makes science more accessible and memorable

## Who Should Read

- **xkcd fans**: More of Munroe's brilliant humor-science
- **Curious minds**: Satisfy your weirdest hypothetical questions
- **Science enthusiasts**: Rigorous physics applied creatively
- **Young readers**: Science IS fun

## Who Should Skip

- Those who prefer **strictly serious** science without humor
- Readers who find **hypothetical thought experiments** pointless
- People who want **systematic science education**

## Difficulty

**Easy** — Complex physics explained with humor and clarity. Accessible to all readers.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: 4-5 hours
