# The Disappearing Spoon

## Executive Summary

Sam Kean takes readers on a rollicking journey through the periodic table, telling the human stories behind each element. From the poison that killed Napoleon to the element that made the first atomic bomb possible, from the battle over who discovered what to the mad scientists who risked everything in pursuit of new elements, the periodic table is revealed as a treasure trove of human drama.



## Key Takeaways

- **The periodic table** contains stories of madness, love, war, and discovery
- **Elements have shaped** human history in surprising ways
- **Discovery of elements** often involved fierce competition and occasional fraud
- **Many elements** have surprising and dangerous properties
- **The periodic table** continues to grow as new elements are synthesized

## Who Should Read

- **Chemistry skeptics**: This book will change your mind about chemistry
- **Science enthusiasts**: The human side of discovery
- **History buffs**: Science's role in historical events
- **Story lovers**: Narrative nonfiction at its best

## Who Should Skip

- Those seeking a **textbook introduction** to chemistry
- Readers who prefer **systematic science** to anecdotal approach
- People who want **science without human stories**

## Difficulty

**Easy** — Engaging narrative accessible to all readers. Chemistry concepts explained in context.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: 7-8 hours

## Final Verdict

**Highly Recommended** — A brilliant demonstration that chemistry is anything but boring. Kean's storytelling transforms the periodic table into a collection of fascinating human dramas. One of the best popular science books ever written.
